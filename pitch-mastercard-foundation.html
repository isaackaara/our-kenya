Program Alignment

This is already your mission

Our Kenya doesn't need the Mastercard Foundation to change direction. Every program pillar you already fund connects directly to what we've built.

Young Africa Works needs cultural context

Dignified work requires dignified identity. Young Kenyans who understand economic history — Jua Kali, the cooperative movement, M-Pesa — make better entrepreneurs. They see themselves in a lineage of problem-solvers, not just job-seekers.

Identity drives agency. Agency drives employment.

Inclusive Creatives, meet digital heritage

Kenya Inclusive Creatives Program funds digital creative work. Our Kenya IS digital creative work — 7,796 articles, 145 story trails, 58 interactive knowledge graphs. Cultural preservation at scale, built with the same tools the creative economy runs on.

Cultural preservation IS creative economy work.

Ajira meets heritage

391,000 youth trained via Ajira. 260+ centres across 47 counties. Our Kenya was built with the exact digital skills Ajira teaches: content creation, knowledge management, data visualisation, web development.

A platform that practices what Ajira preaches.

Scholars need this

Baobab Summit. Wings to Fly. 60,000+ students on scholarships learning about their country from fragmented textbooks and Wikipedia. Our Kenya is the reference library they deserve — comprehensive, source-verified, and free.

60,000 scholars deserve a world-class resource.

The Proposal

Our Kenya as educational infrastructure

A three-phase program that expands Kenya's most comprehensive history resource, connects it to classrooms, and preserves oral traditions before they're lost.

Phase 1 — Year 1

Content Expansion

2,000 new Gold Standard notes covering the stories the Foundation cares about most

  • Entrepreneurship and innovation histories
  • County-level economic stories (all 47 counties)
  • TVET pioneers and vocational success stories
  • Women in Kenya's economic history
  • Youth movement histories (1960s to Gen Z)
  • New knowledge graphs and story trails
Phase 2 — Year 2

School Outreach

Curriculum-aligned packages that bring Our Kenya into classrooms across all 47 counties

  • CBC and 8-4-4 aligned trail packages
  • Teacher guides and lesson plans
  • Student engagement tracking with certificates
  • University research partnerships
  • Ajira centre integration (260+ locations)
  • Pilot 10 counties, then scale to 47
Phase 3 — Year 3

Oral History Preservation

Record and digitise elder oral histories before they're lost forever

  • Field recordings with county cultural officers
  • Audio-first, mobile-accessible format
  • Transcription into the knowledge graph
  • Youth training in oral history methodology (jobs)
  • Elder recognition and attribution
  • Permanent digital archive
KES 15–25M over 3 years

Modest by Foundation standards. KCB 2jiajiri received KES 10 billion. Young Africa Works Kenya is KES 30 billion. This is less than 0.1% of existing program budgets for a permanent, scalable digital resource that serves every other program the Foundation funds in Kenya.

Phase budgets are flexible and can be structured around the Foundation's fiscal calendar and disbursement preferences. We're building infrastructure, not burning through a media buy.
