Uasin Gishu County, located in the western highlands of the Rift Valley, is Kenya's grain basket and a major center of athletic excellence. The county seat of [[Uasin Gishu County|Uasin Gishu County]] has emerged as a hub for world-class distance runners, while the broader county economy centers on grain cultivation and mixed farming.

## Agricultural Production

Uasin Gishu County is characterized by favorable altitude, fertile volcanic soils, and reliable rainfall that made it ideal for both traditional pastoralism and modern commercial agriculture. The county is primarily agricultural, with dominant crops including maize, wheat, sunflower, pyrethrum, and barley. The grain farming sector, particularly wheat and maize, has earned the county recognition as Kenya's grain basket. This agricultural productivity supported population growth and created wealth concentrations that enabled later investment in education and athletics.

## Eldoret and Running Culture

Eldoret, the county capital, has become one of the world's premier distance-running destinations. The high altitude (approximately 2,100 meters), cool climate, and rolling terrain create ideal conditions for distance-running training. Numerous training camps operate in and around Eldoret, attracting elite runners from Kenya and globally. International runners visit to benefit from the altitude, training infrastructure, and access to experienced Kalenjin coaches and runners.

The running culture is now embedded in Eldoret's economic and social life. Running success brings prestige, sponsorship, and prize money. Young people pursue athletics as a primary career path. Sporting goods companies (Nike, Adidas, On Running) maintain significant presence in the region. The concentration of running talent and infrastructure creates a virtuous cycle where young runners train alongside world-class athletes, coaches provide quality instruction, and successful examples inspire the next generation.

## Population Diversity

While Kalenjin (particularly Nandi and other sub-groups) form the majority population of Uasin Gishu County, the county has attracted significant settler populations from other Kenyan groups. Kikuyu, Luhya, and other groups migrated into the county during and after colonial period, seeking agricultural land, commercial opportunities, and employment. This migration created a multi-ethnic county with potential for both economic dynamism and political tension around land ownership and resource distribution.

## Historical Significance

Historically, Uasin Gishu was a Kalenjin stronghold. The Nandi displaced [[Maasai|Maasai]] from the region in pre-colonial times and used it as grazing land. During colonial period, Afrikaner farmers from South Africa settled around Eldoret and Nyahururu, establishing farms and creating a European settler presence. These settlers introduced new farming techniques and exported-oriented agriculture, though most left after independence.

The county's political history during the Moi era (1978-2002) reflected its Kalenjin demographic majority, with strong political alignment with the regime. Post-Moi, the county has witnessed more competitive politics among Kalenjin sub-groups and between ethnic groups over county resources and representation.

## Flower Farming

In addition to grain cultivation, Uasin Gishu has developed significant flower farming, particularly around Eldoret and outlying areas. Cut flower production for export, particularly to European markets, created employment and generated export income. This agribusiness sector complemented grain farming and contributed to regional economic diversification.
