# The Meru: Eastern Flank of Mount Kenya

The [[Meru]] people represent one of Kenya's most significant yet overlooked ethnic communities. Numbering approximately 2.3 million (about 6 percent of Kenya's population), they rank as the third or fourth largest Bantu-speaking group, surpassed only by the [[Kikuyu]], [[Luhya]], and sometimes [[Luo]].

The [[Meru]] inhabit the northeastern slopes of [[Mount Kenya]], a position that has defined their history, economy, and cultural identity. Despite their numerical strength and cultural sophistication, Meru narratives remain underrepresented in Kenya's dominant national histories.
