# Kiraitu Murungi: Minister, Governor, and Constitutional Reformer

Kiraitu Murungi is one of Kenya's most experienced political leaders, with a career spanning decades as an academic, advocate, parliamentarian, minister, and governor.

## Background and Early Career

Murungi is a lawyer and law teacher educated in Kenya and internationally. He began his career in academia and civil society, establishing himself as an intellectual voice on constitutional and legal matters.

## Parliamentary Service

Murungi served as Member of Parliament representing [[South Imenti]] Constituency in [[Meru County]], building a political base and national profile.

## Cabinet Service Under Kibaki

During the [[Daniel arap Moi]]'s later years and particularly under President [[Kibaki]] (2003-2013), Murungi held several cabinet positions:

- **Minister of Energy and Petroleum**: Overseeing Kenya's energy sector
- **Minister of Justice and Constitutional Affairs**: A role that placed him at the center of Kenya's constitutional reform process

## Constitutional Reform Leadership

Murungi was prominent in Kenya's 2010 constitutional reform, a major political transition that introduced a new constitution and devolved government system. His legal expertise and political positioning made him a central figure in these reforms.

## Senate Service

Following the 2010 constitutional transition, Murungi served as a Senator representing Meru County in the newly created national Senate.

## Governorship (2017-2022)

In the 2017 elections, Murungi successfully ran for Governor of Meru County under the Jubilee Party, serving a five-year term (2017-2022).

As governor, Murungi focused on county administration and development.

## 2022 Electoral Loss and Aftermath

In the 2022 general elections, Murungi lost his bid for re-election as governor. This represented a notable political setback for someone who had not lost an election in 30 years.

## Contemporary Status

As of 2026, Murungi remains a significant political figure in Meru and Kenya, though no longer holding executive office. He has authored books on politics and happiness, reflecting broader intellectual interests beyond active politics.

His career exemplifies the trajectory of Kenyan political elites who have adapted across multiple regime changes and constitutional transitions.
