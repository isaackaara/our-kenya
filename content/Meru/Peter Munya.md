# Peter Munya: Governor and Cabinet Secretary

[[Peter Munya]] is a [[Meru]] politician who rose to prominence through county and national government positions in Kenya's devolved governance system.

## Early Career and Background

Munya is from Muthaara in Meru County. He built a political base in Meru before emerging as a significant national figure.

## Election as Meru County Governor (2013)

In the 2013 general elections, Munya was elected as the first elected Governor of Meru County following Kenya's devolution to county government. He defeated the educationist Kilemi Mwiria in a close race.

## Tenure as Governor (2013-2017)

Munya served as Meru County Governor from March 27, 2013, to August 18, 2017. His tenure focused on county administration and development.

After his term ended in 2017, Kiraitu Murungi succeeded him as governor.

## Cabinet Service Under Kenyatta

Following his governorship, Munya was appointed Cabinet Secretary for Agriculture under President Uhuru Kenyatta. This placed him in a senior national position overseeing Kenya's agricultural sector, a critical portfolio given agriculture's importance to the national economy.

Munya's tenure as Agriculture CS involved policy work on agricultural development, food security, and rural economy.

## Service Under Ruto

When President William Ruto took office in September 2022, Munya was initially succeeded as Agriculture CS by Mithika Linturi. However, Ruto subsequently dismissed Linturi in 2024, creating a vacancy in the position.

## Political Position in 2026

As of 2026, Munya has aligned with opposition politics against President Ruto, joining other opposition politicians ahead of the 2027 presidential elections.

When asked about potential government positions, Munya stated clearly that he is seeking the Meru governorship rather than any national position, expressing disinterest in returning to cabinet service.

## Meru Gubernatorial Ambitions

Munya is positioning himself as a candidate for Meru County Governor in upcoming elections, seeking to return to county leadership.

His political trajectory demonstrates the role of regional strongmen in Kenya's devolved governance system.
