The Kikuyu presence in Nairobi is foundational to the city's creation and development. Nairobi has a Kikuyu-majority or near-majority population, and Kikuyu are overrepresented in the city's professional, commercial, and political classes. Kikuyu have also migrated to the diaspora, establishing communities in the UK, US, South Africa, and other countries.

## Nairobi's Kikuyu Population

Nairobi, established as a colonial railway headquarters in 1899, became a cosmopolitan urban center where Kikuyu migrated in large numbers. By independence, Kikuyu constituted a substantial minority in Nairobi. By the 2020s, Kikuyu are the largest or near-largest ethnic group in Nairobi's population.

Kikuyu migration to Nairobi reflected economic opportunity, escape from rural land shortage, and attraction to urban employment and commercial opportunities.

## Kikuyu-Dominant Neighborhoods

Specific Nairobi neighborhoods have become strongly associated with Kikuyu communities:

- **Westlands:** An upscale neighborhood that has become a center for Kikuyu and Asian professional and commercial elites. Westlands houses corporate offices, expensive residences, and commercial establishments.

- **Parklands:** Another upscale neighborhood with significant Kikuyu presence, characterized by medium to high-income residential areas and commercial establishments.

- **Karen suburb:** Named after Karen Blixen (author of "Out of Africa"), Karen is an upscale suburb where Kikuyu and other elite Kenyans reside alongside expatriates and Asian residents.

- **Eastlands:** Including neighborhoods like Gikomba, Eastlands hosts significant Kikuyu trading and commercial communities. Gikomba market has become a major trading center for second-hand goods and general merchandise.

- **South B, South C, Nairobi West:** Middle-class neighborhoods with substantial Kikuyu populations.

## Urban Kikuyu Middle Class

Nairobi's Kikuyu middle class emerged through coffee wealth, education, and commercial opportunity. Urban Kikuyu professionals work in law, medicine, engineering, teaching, and business. Kikuyu entrepreneurs control significant commercial enterprises.

The Kikuyu middle class in Nairobi tends to be relatively secular, educated, and engaged with global consumer culture while maintaining some connection to rural homes and cultural identity.

## Kikuyu Trading Networks

Kikuyu traders dominate significant economic sectors in Nairobi. Wholesale markets, retail commerce, and import-export businesses often feature Kikuyu ownership and management.

The Gikomba market and surrounding areas represent a hub of Kikuyu commercial activity. Kikuyu traders export goods, operate retail businesses, and function as middlemen in supply chains.

Eastleigh, while increasingly cosmopolitan, maintains significant Kikuyu trading populations involved in various commodity chains.

## Remittances and Connection to Home

Nairobi-based Kikuyu maintain strong connections to their home regions in central Kenya through remittances, property investment, and regular visits. Many urban Kikuyu send money to rural relatives, invest in land in their home counties, and travel home for major ceremonies and celebrations.

These financial and social connections bind urban and rural Kikuyu communities together and constitute a major source of rural development investment.

## Kikuyu in the Diaspora

Kikuyu have migrated internationally, establishing communities in:

- **United Kingdom:** Kikuyu professionals and students settled in the UK, particularly in London. The UK has a long history of Kikuyu presence dating to colonial period.

- **United States:** Kikuyu professionals and economic migrants have established communities in major US cities. Kikuyu doctors, engineers, and business people constitute significant diaspora populations.

- **South Africa:** Kikuyu migrated to South Africa for employment and commercial opportunities, establishing communities in Johannesburg and other cities.

- **Canada and Australia:** Smaller Kikuyu communities have established themselves in these countries.

## Diaspora and Cultural Maintenance

Diaspora Kikuyu maintain cultural identity through:
- Kikuyu-language churches and Christian organizations
- Cultural associations and social clubs
- Preparation of traditional foods
- Participation in diaspora political and social movements
- Investment in home communities and property

Diaspora Kikuyu sometimes maintain strong political interest in Kenya, following elections and political events and occasionally sending remittances for particular political purposes.

## Contemporary Dynamics

Contemporary Nairobi Kikuyu are diverse, ranging from wealthy business elites to informal sector workers and street vendors. However, Kikuyu overrepresentation in professional and commercial classes is notable.

Nairobi's Kikuyu diaspora communities maintain networks with Kenya, sending remittances and maintaining property interests. Many diaspora Kikuyu express aspirations to eventually return to Kenya, though actual return rates are lower than aspirations.

The Kikuyu presence in Nairobi and the diaspora represents both successful economic integration and ongoing cultural identity maintenance. Kikuyu navigate between cosmopolitan urban identity and Kikuyu cultural connection.
