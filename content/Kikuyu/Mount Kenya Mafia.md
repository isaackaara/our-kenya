The term, used by political analysts, journalists, and rivals, evokes both geographic identity (the Mount Kenya region (Nyeri), Kirinyaga, Murang'a, Kiambu, as the Kikuyu heartland) and the opacity of their operations.

## Key Facts

- The core figures most associated with the label: John Michuki (Minister of Internal Security, then Transport, then Environment), Njenga Karume (business magnate, cabinet minister), Kiraitu Murungi (Minister of Justice, then Energy), Chris Murungaru (Minister of Internal Security), and Uhuru Kenyatta (then opposition leader and Kibaki's most prominent Kikuyu peer)
- These figures were accused of using their proximity to Kibaki to monopolise government procurement contracts, banking access, security sector appointments, and infrastructure tenders; the accusation was that the NARC coalition's anti-corruption promise was subordinated to ethnic business interests within months of the 2003 inauguration
- John Michuki was widely regarded as the most powerful figure in the inner circle; as Minister of Internal Security he oversaw the security crackdown on [[Mungiki]] in 2007 and was accused of directing extrajudicial killings; he also shut down the *Standard* newspaper's printing press and raided its offices in 2006 following investigative reporting on the government
- Njenga Karume (1929-2012): one of Kenya's wealthiest individuals, a self-made businessman and politician from Githunguri, Kiambu; represented continuity with the [[Kenyatta Presidency]] era elite; his wealth and political connections spanned decades
- The Anglo-Leasing scandal (2004-2005): a series of fraudulent security and infrastructure contracts signed under the NARC government, worth billions of shillings, in which procurement was awarded to fictitious or politically connected companies; the scandal severely damaged the Kibaki government's anti-corruption credibility and implicated several figures in the inner circle
- The phrase represented a structural reality: Kikuyu business networks had funded the NARC campaign and expected returns; the [[Kikuyu Business Dominance]] pattern was both the resource base and the potential liability of the Kibaki presidency
- The Mount Kenya Mafia dynamic contributed to the fracturing of the NARC coalition and the emergence of Raila Odinga's "Pentagon" opposition ahead of the 2007 election, setting the conditions for the [[2007-2008 Post Election Violence]]
