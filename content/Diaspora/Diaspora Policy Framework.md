A comprehensive diaspora policy framework represents government's systematic approach to diaspora engagement, establishing formal mechanisms, institutional structures, and policy objectives governing diaspora relationships with Kenya. The diaspora policy framework emerged from growing recognition that diaspora economic contributions, investment capacity, and cultural significance warranted formal policy attention and institutional development. diaspora policy frameworks typically address multiple dimensions including investment incentives, remittance facilitation, citizenship access, political participation, and cultural engagement. Framework development has involved contributions from diaspora organizations, government agencies, international development partners, and policy research institutions.

The [[Diaspora Task Force|diaspora task force]] represents primary institutional mechanism within diaspora policy frameworks, established to coordinate diaspora engagement across government agencies and implement diaspora policy initiatives. The diaspora task force has typically convened representation from foreign affairs ministries, finance ministries, commerce agencies, and sometimes diaspora representatives. Task force responsibilities have included diaspora policy development, diaspora engagement coordination, investment promotion, and remittance facilitation. However, task force effectiveness has varied depending on government commitment, budget allocation, and coordination across agencies. Diaspora organizations have reported variable responsiveness and follow-through from task force initiatives.

Investment incentive policies within diaspora frameworks aim to facilitate diaspora capital flows toward development objectives. diaspora-targeted investment incentives have included tax exemptions for diaspora-funded projects in priority sectors, simplified business registration processes for diaspora entrepreneurs, and preferential financing terms for diaspora investment. Some frameworks have established diaspora investment vehicles facilitating institutional investment mechanisms. However, investment incentive implementation has frequently been incomplete, with diaspora investors reporting difficulty accessing advertised incentives due to bureaucratic complexity or inconsistent application. Clarification and simplification of investment incentive processes remains ongoing policy priority.

Remittance policy frameworks have attempted to reduce transfer costs and facilitate efficient diaspora-to-Kenya fund transmission. Frameworks have included regulation of money transfer services, promotion of digital financial services reducing transfer friction, and bilateral agreements with major diaspora destination countries facilitating transfer efficiency. Central Bank of Kenya regulation of money transfer services and digital payment systems represents framework implementation mechanisms. However, transfer costs remain substantially higher than policy targets, limiting remittance efficiency improvement despite policy attention. Competition among money transfer service providers has improved some corridor efficiency while other corridors remain expensive.

Citizenship and immigration policy frameworks address diaspora access to Kenyan citizenship, visas, and residency. diaspora policy frameworks have explored dual citizenship provisions enabling diaspora to maintain Kenyan citizenship alongside host country nationality. Some frameworks have proposed simplified visa procedures for diaspora visits to Kenya. Property ownership frameworks have sometimes included diaspora-specific provisions addressing land purchase by non-resident Kenyans. diaspora policy regarding immigration has sometimes been inconsistent, with diaspora access to citizenship and property ownership varying depending on political administration and implementation capacity.
