Basket weaving in Kenya represents traditional craft practice producing functional and decorative baskets employing diverse materials and weaving techniques. Basket weaving traditions exist across Kenyan communities, with regional variations in materials, techniques, and forms reflecting local environments and cultural traditions. Professional basket weavers create products serving domestic use, commercial sale, and artistic expression. Basket weaving remains economically important craft providing income for practitioners while maintaining cultural traditions and artistic heritage. Contemporary basket weavers navigate between tradition and innovation, commercial markets and artistic integrity.

Traditional basket weaving employs local materials including grasses, reeds, palms, and other plant fibers. Weavers gather materials, prepare them for weaving, and employ diverse weaving techniques creating varied forms and patterns. Different basket types serve different functions including storage, carrying, ceremonial use, and decorative purposes. Basket forms and patterns often reflect cultural meanings and aesthetic preferences. The weaving process requires skill developed through practice and cultural transmission, with knowledge passed through generations. Traditional basket weaving practices maintain important aspect of Kenyan cultural heritage.

Basket weaving has significant economic importance, particularly in rural areas where weaving generates income and supports livelihoods. Commercial basket production supplies tourist markets, craft galleries, and domestic markets. Baskets function as cultural ambassadors marketed internationally as authentic Kenyan craft products. However, commercialization has sometimes affected traditional forms as baskets adapt to market demand and tourist preferences. Contemporary weavers navigate tensions between maintaining traditional integrity and meeting commercial needs. The economics of basket weaving affects both sustainability and cultural transmission of traditional practices.

Contemporary basket weaving encompasses both traditional practice maintaining heritage and artistic innovation exploring baskets as contemporary medium. Weavers experiment with materials, forms, and scales creating artistic works. Basket exhibitions increasingly appear in fine art contexts recognizing weaving sophistication. International recognition of African basket weaving as significant artistic tradition has elevated status of Kenyan practitioners. Contemporary Kenyan basket weavers create work serving cultural, economic, and artistic functions. Basket weaving remains vital traditional craft and contemporary artistic practice within Kenya's cultural landscape.
