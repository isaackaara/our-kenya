Typography in Kenya developed as discipline within graphic design addressing design and use of typefaces, letterforms, and text composition. Typography combines functional communication needs with aesthetic and expressive possibilities of letterforms. The development of typography as professional practice reflected growth of graphic design industry and demands for sophisticated visual communication. Typographers and designers engaged with typeface selection, spacing, arrangement, and composition to create effective and beautiful text-based communications. Typography remains fundamental design discipline shaping visual communication across media.

Early typography in Kenya followed conventions established in colonial period and international design traditions. Typography in printed materials including publications, advertisements, and official documents employed typefaces selected for clarity, legibility, and aesthetic qualities. The expansion of printing infrastructure and publishing from independence onward increased demands for thoughtful typography. Typography education became standard component of graphic design and print production training. Designers learned typographic principles, letterform history, and practical skills for effective text composition. The sophistication of typographic practice increased as design professionalization advanced.

Digital typography from the 1990s onward transformed typographic practice through font technology and digital design tools. Digital typography enabled new creative possibilities and greater accessibility to type design. Type designers and foundries created fonts specifically for digital environments addressing screen display requirements. Digital typography tools made typeface selection and text composition more flexible and widely accessible. However, digital typography also raised concerns about typeface proliferation and declining typographic standards. The democratization of font creation tools has enabled more people to design typefaces though quality varies widely.

Contemporary Kenyan typography engages with digital typographic possibilities while maintaining traditional design principles. Typographers address challenges of designing for multiple platforms and technologies. Typography in digital environments differs from print typography requiring different approaches to legibility, sizing, and interaction. Contemporary designers work fluidly across print and digital typography. The continued importance of typography reflects enduring significance of text in communication. Typography remains active design discipline with ongoing innovation and professional practice.
