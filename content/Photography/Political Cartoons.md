Political cartoons in Kenya represent visual commentary on political events, policies, and personalities using caricature, satire, and visual metaphor. Political cartoonists employ visual wit and artistic skill to communicate critique, commentary, and social observation about public affairs. The political cartoon tradition has long history globally and has developed in Kenya particularly within journalism and media criticism. Political cartoons combine artistic skill with political understanding and capacity for social observation. The medium has served important functions in public discourse and social commentary while sometimes attracting controversy and political pressure.

Political cartooning in Kenya has been practiced by journalists and illustrators working for newspapers and publications. Cartoonists create illustrations accompanying news stories and columns, offering visual commentary on events and issues. Quality political cartoons employ sophisticated visual language combining recognizable subjects with visual metaphor, exaggeration, and wit. Political cartoons can make complex issues understandable and memorable through visual representation. The best political cartoons achieve both artistic quality and effective communication. Political cartoonists in Kenya have addressed independence, political transitions, economic issues, and social change through visual commentary.

The relationship between political cartoons and political power has been complex, with cartoons sometimes serving official propaganda while other times offering critical commentary threatening to authorities. Cartoonists have exercised freedom of expression through visual commentary, sometimes facing consequences for critical work. The capacity of visual media to communicate powerfully makes political cartooning significant form of political expression. Political cartooning has been protected form of speech in many contexts, though constraints have applied in various periods. Contemporary political cartoonists continue offering social and political commentary through visual means.

Contemporary Kenyan political cartooning continues within journalism, digital media, and independent artistic practice. Political cartoonists address contemporary issues including governance, corruption, inequality, and social change. Digital platforms enable wider distribution of political cartoons while also enabling rapid response to current events. Political cartoons represent important form of visual journalism and public discourse. The combination of artistic skill and political insight required for effective political cartooning keeps the tradition vital. Political cartoons contribute to Kenya's visual culture and democratic discourse.
