Comic art in Kenya represents practice combining sequential images with narrative text creating stories through visual sequence and dialogue. Comic artists employ visual storytelling conventions including panel layout, perspective, character design, and visual pacing. The comic medium allows sophisticated narrative expression combining visual and textual elements. Comics can achieve high artistic quality while remaining accessible to broad audiences. The history of comics in Kenya includes both imported foreign comics and locally-produced comic works. Comic art remains minority practice within Kenya's visual culture though maintaining dedicated practitioners and audiences.

Early comics in Kenya primarily consisted of imported American and European comics circulating through commercial channels. These imported comics influenced local culture and introduced comic conventions to Kenyan audiences. As local publishing industries developed, Kenyan publishers printed comics including reprints and original productions. Educational comics served literacy and educational purposes, using comic format to teach subjects accessibly. Comic illustrations appeared in newspapers and magazines. However, local original comic production remained limited with most comics consumed being foreign productions.

The development of local comic production depended upon viable markets, publishing infrastructure, and artists with comic-specific skills. Comic art requires understanding of sequential narrative, panel composition, and visual pacing distinct from single-image illustration. Very few Kenyan artists have developed careers primarily as comic creators, though illustrators and cartoonists employ comic techniques. The economics of comic publishing in Kenya has remained challenging, with small local markets limiting publisher investment. Digital platforms have created new possibilities for comic distribution and independent comic creation. Contemporary Kenyan comic artists create work addressing local themes and contributing to global comic cultures.

Contemporary comics in Kenya employ diverse genres and approaches from superhero narratives to autobiographical comics to political comics. Digital tools enable comic creation and distribution. Graphic novels and collected comic volumes represent formats gaining presence in literary culture. Comics employ sophisticated visual and narrative language allowing complex storytelling. The comic medium's accessibility and narrative power continue attracting artists despite limited economic opportunities. Contemporary Kenyan comics contribute to broader visual and literary cultures while remaining minor market segment. Comic art represents artistic possibility within Kenya's visual landscape.
