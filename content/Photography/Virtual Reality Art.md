Virtual reality art in Kenya represents emerging artistic practice utilizing VR technology creating immersive visual and spatial experiences. VR artists employed headset technology and immersive software creating three-dimensional environments and interactive experiences. Photography documenting VR art reveals the technical infrastructure and artistic approaches enabling virtual reality artistic expression. The visual archive of Kenyan VR art demonstrates VR's emerging role in contemporary artistic practice and its potential for novel aesthetic experiences.

Virtual reality technology adoption in Kenya developed gradually as VR hardware became more accessible and affordable. Artists experimented with VR systems developing VR-based artistic works. Photography of VR adoption reveals artists engaging with emerging technology. The visual record documents early experimentation with VR artistic possibilities. Images of technical setup show VR infrastructure and equipment. VR technology adoption represented emerging frontier in artistic technology adoption.
