Illustration art in Kenya encompasses work created to accompany or explain text, communicate information visually, or serve decorative purposes. Illustrators employ diverse media and styles creating work for publications, advertising, children's books, editorial materials, and commercial applications. The illustration profession developed from practical needs for visual communication in publications and commercial materials. Professional illustrators developed specialized skills and stylistic approaches recognizing illustration as distinct artistic discipline. The illustration field includes diverse specializations serving different industries and purposes.

Early illustration in Kenya developed primarily within colonial publishing, printing, and administrative needs. Government publications, commercial printing, and publications required illustrative work. Illustrators and artists provided images serving communicative and decorative purposes. The professionalization of illustration followed development of printing industries and publishing. With growth of advertising, publishing, and commercial industries, demands for professional illustration increased. Illustrators developed specialized skills in particular genres including scientific illustration, editorial illustration, children's book illustration, and advertising illustration. Professional standards and organizations emerged recognizing illustration as distinct creative practice.

The relationship between illustration and fine art has remained contested, with some viewing illustration as commercial work distinct from artistic practice while others recognize illustration as legitimate artistic expression. Talented illustrators developed individual styles and gained recognition and following. Illustration can achieve high artistic standards and address complex themes through visual language. Contemporary illustration spans from commercial applications to fine art practice with illustrators working across these categories. The distinction between illustration and fine art remains somewhat arbitrary with excellent work appearing in both domains.

Contemporary Kenyan illustration practices serve diverse functions from children's book illustration to editorial illustration to artistic illustration exploring visual themes. Digital tools have expanded illustration possibilities and accessibility. Illustrators work across print and digital media developing work for varied applications. Illustration remains vital practice within Kenya's visual culture serving communication, commercial, educational, and artistic functions. The continued development of illustration reflects ongoing demands for visual communication and expressive possibilities of illustrative media.
