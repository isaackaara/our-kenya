Performance art in Kenya emerged as contemporary artistic practice emphasizing live action, embodied presence, and temporal experiences rather than static artworks. Performance artists work with their bodies, voices, and physical presence to create immediate experiences engaging audiences. Performance art encompasses diverse approaches including dance, theatrical performance, ritual enactment, and experimental action. The emerging visibility of performance art in Kenya reflects broader internationalization of artistic practice and recognition of performance as legitimate artistic medium.

Performance art in Kenya often draws upon deep traditions of embodied cultural expression including traditional dance, theatrical traditions, and ritual practices. Contemporary performance artists build upon these traditions while also engaging global performance art discourse and experimental approaches. Some performance work explicitly addresses contemporary social, political, and cultural issues through physical enactment. Performance provides direct engagement with bodies, space, and audiences in ways static artworks cannot achieve. The immediacy and ephemeral nature of performance distinguish it from other artistic media.

The documentation and circulation of performance work poses specific challenges and opportunities. Since performance exists primarily in the moment of enactment, documentation through photography, video, or description becomes essential for extending reach beyond those present at live events. Digital media have enabled wider circulation of performance documentation, though documentation inevitably differs from live experience. Performance archives including video documentation and written accounts preserve performance histories. The documentation of performance becomes itself an artistic and historical practice.

Performance art remains minority practice in Kenya's art world where material-based work and gallery-centered practice remain dominant. However, performance art has gained growing visibility through art festivals, university programs, and independent artists working outside traditional gallery systems. International exposure to global performance art discourse influences Kenyan practitioners. Contemporary performance artists create sophisticated work addressing complex themes through embodied practice. The development of performance art expands Kenya's artistic vocabulary and possibilities for artistic expression.
