Textile art and design in Kenya encompasses creation of patterned fabrics, textiles, and woven works employing traditional and contemporary techniques. Textile designers create patterns, dyes, and fabric designs serving fashion, interior design, and fine art purposes. The textile tradition in Kenya includes both inherited weaving and textile practices and contemporary textile design. Professional textile designers work in fashion, interior design, textile manufacturing, and fine art contexts. Contemporary textile art explores textile as artistic medium creating conceptual and aesthetic work. Kenya's textile heritage provides rich foundation for contemporary textile innovation.

Kenya's textile heritage includes traditional weaving, dyeing, and fabric practices reflecting cultural traditions and regional styles. Kikoi fabrics, Maasai beadwork textiles, and other traditional forms represent cultural textile heritage. Colonial and post-independence textile industries produced manufactured textiles for commercial markets. Textile design education developed teaching pattern design, dye processes, and contemporary textile approaches. Professional textile designers worked within manufacturing creating designs for commercial textiles. Contemporary textile artists also emerged exploring textiles as fine art medium. Traditional heritage and contemporary practice coexist within Kenya's textile landscape.

Textile art practice employs varied approaches including pattern design, dyeing, weaving, embroidery, and mixed textile techniques. Artists create textiles addressing cultural themes, aesthetic exploration, and conceptual concerns. Woven textiles employ handweaving or loom technology creating complex patterns and textures. Dyed textiles explore color relationships and patterning through dyeing processes. Contemporary textile artists combine traditional techniques with contemporary artistic concerns creating hybrid practices. Textile art achieves sophistication and complexity comparable to other fine art media. Textile exhibitions increasingly appear in contemporary art contexts recognizing textile as legitimate artistic medium.

Contemporary Kenyan textile artists create work addressing cultural identity, sustainability, and artistic exploration. Some textile artists incorporate traditional Kenyan designs and techniques into contemporary work. Others employ textiles as medium for conceptual exploration or environmental commentary. Digital textile design enables new creative possibilities while traditional textile techniques remain valued. Textile arts provide economic opportunity within fashion, interior design, and fine art contexts. The continued development of textile art reflects textile's enduring significance in cultural expression and artistic creation.
