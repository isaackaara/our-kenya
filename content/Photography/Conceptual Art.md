Conceptual art in Kenya represents artistic practice emphasizing ideas and concepts as primary artistic material rather than focusing on visual form or craftsmanship. Conceptual approaches prioritize artistic concepts, allowing diverse media and presentation strategies serving the ideas. This artistic approach emerged globally from the 1960s onward and gradually influenced Kenyan artistic practice, particularly among artists engaged with contemporary international art discourse. Conceptual strategies appear in works spanning varied media including installation, performance, text, photography, and video.

Conceptual art challenges traditional definitions of art based on material skill and aesthetic beauty, instead valuing intellectual content and idea development. Artists working conceptually may employ photography, found objects, documentation, or dematerialized concepts as artistic media. The conceptual approach allows artists to explore complex ideas including politics, identity, representation, and social critique. Kenyan conceptual artists have engaged contemporary issues through conceptually-driven work, sometimes using locally-specific materials and contexts to explore universal themes.

The emergence of conceptual art in Kenya reflected broader internationalization of artistic practice and access to contemporary art discourse through education, travel, and media. Artists trained in international contexts or influenced by international contemporary art incorporated conceptual approaches into their practice. However, conceptual art remains minority practice in Kenya where material-based and figurative art remain more prominent. The accessibility and appeal of conceptual art remains limited compared to representational work communicating more directly to broader audiences.

Contemporary Kenyan conceptual artists continue developing sophisticated work addressing complex themes through ideas-driven approaches. Installation art, performance art, and photo-conceptual work have gained greater visibility within Kenya's art world. International exhibitions and biennales increasingly include work by Kenyan conceptual artists, bringing recognition to these practices. The relationship between conceptual approaches and other artistic traditions remains dynamic, with many artists combining conceptual frameworks with diverse media and aesthetic concerns. Conceptual art represents important innovation in Kenya's artistic landscape though remaining minority practice.
