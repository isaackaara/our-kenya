Quality standards for commodities emerged as merchant communities sought to ensure reliable commodity characteristics and prevent fraud. [[Mombasa]], [[Zanzibar]], [[Lamu]], and ports developed procedures for inspecting goods and certifying quality. The standardization enabled merchants unfamiliar with specific commodities to trade with confidence. The quality certification created reputation distinctions among merchants.
