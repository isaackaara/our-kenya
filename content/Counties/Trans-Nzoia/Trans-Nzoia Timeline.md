# Trans-Nzoia Timeline

This timeline documents key dates and events in Trans-Nzoia County history from the colonial period through 2026.

## Early Colonial Period (1895-1920s)
1895: European explorers document [[../../Conservation/Mount Elgon National Park|Mount Elgon]] and western Kenya region
1902: British colonial administration established in Trans-Nzoia area
1905-1920: European settler farms established in Trans-Nzoia highlands
1910: Kitale established as colonial administrative center

## Colonial Development (1920s-1950s)
1920-1945: White Highlands period, European farm expansion continues
1930s: Agricultural development with focus on crop cultivation
1945: Post-World War II colonial administrative reforms
1952-1960: Mau Mau Rebellion period, affecting Trans-Nzoia region
1952: Jomo Kenyatta detention (separate from Trans-Nzoia)

## Independence and Land Redistribution (1963-1980s)
1963: Kenya independence, devolution of power from colonial authority
1965-1975: Land redistribution programs, European farms transferred to African ownership
1965: Kitale Museum established
1970s: Agricultural commercialization, NCPB silo expansion
1978: Smallholder agricultural production increases substantially

## Modern Development (1990s-2000s)
1990s: Market liberalization affecting grain and agricultural trade
1997: County administrative divisions established
2000: Agricultural modernization initiatives expand
2005: Inter-communal conflict tensions increase in Mount Elgon area

## Conflict Period (2006-2008)
2006: Sabaot Land Defence Force (SLDF) insurgency begins
2006-2008: Armed conflict in Mount Elgon, community displacement
2008: Government security operations conclude SLDF conflict

## Devolution and Contemporary Period (2010-2026)
2010: Constitution review process nearing completion
2013: Devolution implemented, Trans-Nzoia County Government established
2013: First county elections, devolved administration begins
2015: Second election cycle, revised county governance structures
2017: National election cycle, regional political changes
2020: Third county election, continued devolved governance
2022: National election, gubernatorial races
2022-2026: Continued agricultural development, climate change adaptation initiatives
2026: Contemporary period of sustainable development planning
