# Murang'a Timeline

1900: Fort Hall established as colonial administrative center.
1900-1910: Colonial administration consolidates control. Land appropriation begins.
1905-1920s: Christian missionaries establish schools and churches.
1920s-1950: Agricultural development. Coffee and tea cultivation expands.
1950-1960: Mau Mau uprising. Murang'a District deeply involved. Oathing ceremonies mobilize communities. British counterinsurgency operations conducted.
1963: Kenya achieves independence. Fort Hall district renamed Murang'a.
1970s-1980: Agricultural cooperatives expand. Coffee and tea marketing organizations develop.
1990s-2000: Educational expansion. Higher education opportunities increase.
2000s-2012: Urbanization accelerates near Nairobi. Residential development expands.
2013: Devolution creates Murang'a County government.
2019: Census documents approximately 1.1 million population.
2020-2021: COVID-19 pandemic affects agriculture and healthcare.
2022-2026: County development initiatives. Climate adaptation emphasis. Infrastructure improvement.
