Murang'a Town serves as the county capital and primary commercial and administrative center, functioning as the hub for county government, commerce, education, and healthcare services. Established during the colonial period as the administrative center for Fort Hall District, the town has evolved from a colonial administrative post into a contemporary Kenyan county town reflecting both colonial urban planning legacies and post-colonial urban expansion dynamics.

Colonial-period town development focused narrowly on administrative functions and European convenience, with the town serving primarily as a seat of district administration. Urban planning reflected colonial racial segregation patterns, with separate residential, commercial, and administrative zones demarcating European, Asian, and African spatial territories. Post-independence urban development has fundamentally reorganized these colonial spatial patterns, though some colonial infrastructure remains.

Contemporary Murang'a Town functions as the center of county government operations, housing the governor's office, county assembly hall, and county government ministries. This concentration of government institutions in the town attracts job-seekers and investors seeking government contracts or services. The town benefits from centralized government investment in office infrastructure and government employee payroll spending.

Commercial activity in Murang'a Town remains modest compared to larger Kenyan towns, though town center areas feature shops, trading centers, and markets serving local and regional demand. Agricultural product marketing (particularly coffee and tea), supply of agricultural inputs, and provision of retail trade goods constitute primary commercial activities. Market facilities including the main central market provide spaces for traders selling agricultural products, household goods, and various commodities. Market governance and infrastructure improvements have been periodic focuses of town administration.

Services including banking, mobile money services, telecommunications, education, healthcare, and government administration locate in the town, making it a service center for broader county areas. Major banks maintain branches in Murang'a Town, providing financial services to county residents and businesses. Mobile money services including M-Pesa and competitors provide money transfer and financial service access more broadly accessible than banking. Telecommunications companies operate service outlets providing mobile phone service, internet access, and related communications services.

Education facilities in Murang'a Town include secondary schools and various primary schools, drawing students from surrounding areas. Technical and vocational training institutions locate in the town, providing training services. The presence of educational institutions makes the town a destination for families seeking quality education.

Healthcare services including Murang'a Teaching and Referral Hospital, private clinics, and health centers locate in the town, making it the county's primary healthcare center. This concentration of quality healthcare facilities attracts patients from surrounding areas, creating demand for accommodation, meals, and other services for visiting patients and family members.

Urban infrastructure development has expanded water supply systems, though water shortages occur periodically during dry seasons. Electricity connectivity in the town remains fairly reliable, though supply constraints occasionally cause outages. Road infrastructure connecting the town to major highways and surrounding areas remains important for urban accessibility. Telecommunications infrastructure has expanded substantially, with mobile coverage extending broadly and internet access points increasing.

Urban governance challenges include inadequate waste management, water supply insufficiency, traffic congestion in commercial areas, informal settlement expansion creating slum conditions, and coordination challenges between town and county authorities. Informal settlement development around the town periphery houses low-income in-migrants, often lacking adequate water, sanitation, and formal urban services. These informal settlements create political and social equity concerns.
