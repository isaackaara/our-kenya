# Tana River Source

The Tana River, Kenya's longest river, originates in the Aberdares and [[../../Conservation/Mount Kenya National Park|Mount Kenya]] region that includes portions of Murang'a County. The river's headwaters flow from mountain springs and streams fed by forest runoff. The Tana flows northeastward through Kenya, eventually reaching the Indian Ocean. The river provides water for agriculture, pastoral use, and human consumption across its drainage basin. Forest conservation on the Tana's source mountains is critical for water security.
