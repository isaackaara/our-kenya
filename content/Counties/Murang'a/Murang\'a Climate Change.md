# Murang'a Climate Change

Murang'a County faces climate change impacts including changing rainfall patterns affecting agricultural productivity. Erratic rainfall threatens coffee, tea, and dairy production. Higher temperatures stress crops and livestock. Extended dry seasons reduce water availability and pasture. Farmers are adapting through improved irrigation, drought-resistant varieties, and adjusted practices. Agroforestry provides adaptation benefits. Long-term climate projections suggest significant changes to highland agriculture, creating uncertainty for farming communities.
