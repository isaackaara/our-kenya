# Fort Hall History

Fort Hall was the colonial name for Murang'a, named after Francis Hall, who established the fort in 1900 as a center of British colonial administration. The name "Fort Hall" designated the administrative post and the district it controlled. The fort served as the seat of the colonial district administrator. At independence in 1963, the district was renamed Murang'a, reclaiming the pre-colonial place name. The historic fort is no longer extant, but colonial-era structures and archives preserve the history.
