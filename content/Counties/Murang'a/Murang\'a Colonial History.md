# Murang'a Colonial History

Fort Hall was established as the colonial administrative center in 1900, serving as the district headquarters. Colonial policies introduced individual land ownership displacing communal systems. European settler farms were established in some areas, displacing Kikuyu communities. Colonial taxation and forced labor affected rural populations. Mission schools introduced Christian education. Colonial administration transformed traditional governance structures. The district experienced significant disruption during the Mau Mau period.
