# Eliud Kipchoge

Eliud Kipchoge is widely regarded as the greatest marathon runner in history. Born in 1984 in Kapsisiywa, near Nandi County (adjacent to Uasin Gishu), Kipchoge grew up in the Rift Valley's running culture. He began his running career in secondary school and quickly emerged as an exceptional talent, winning national and regional competitions.

Kipchoge's greatest achievement was becoming the first person to run a marathon in under two hours, completing the distance in 1 hour, 59 minutes, and 40 seconds in Vienna on October 12, 2019. This achievement was scientifically and culturally significant, representing the breakthrough of a barrier long considered impossible. The run was completed under controlled conditions (paced, supported, with optimal weather), but nevertheless demonstrated human athletic potential. Kipchoge has also won Olympic medals (gold in Rio 2016, silver in Tokyo 2020) and multiple world championships.

Kipchoge is associated with the NN Running Team based in Kenya and represents Kenya internationally. His success has elevated Kenya's status in global running and has inspired countless Kenyan youth to pursue running. Kipchoge's wealth and international fame have made him a symbol of Kenyan athletic excellence and have provided economic opportunity for his family and community. His achievements are celebrated in Kenya and internationally as examples of human excellence and perseverance.
