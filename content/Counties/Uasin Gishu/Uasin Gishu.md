# Uasin Gishu County

Uasin Gishu County is located in the western Rift Valley highlands, centered on Eldoret city. With a population of approximately 1.2 million, Eldoret is Kenya's fifth-largest city. The county is famous for three interconnected phenomena: the Rift Valley Athletics Academy, birthplace of many of the world's best distance runners; wheat farming on the broad plateau; and the 2007-2008 post-election violence, particularly the Kiambaa church burning.

The county is predominantly Kalenjin (Nandi and Kipsigis subgroups), though it has significant Kikuyu, Luo, Luhya, and other settler communities who arrived during the post-independence land redistribution era. This ethnic diversity has created the same tensions present in Nakuru, manifesting violently during the 2007-2008 crisis when settler communities were attacked.

Uasin Gishu's economy is based on wheat farming (Kenya's main wheat-growing region), dairy, maize, and increasingly, light industry and services centered on Eldoret city. The county's international fame derives from its athletic tradition, with training camps attracting runners from throughout the world seeking to improve performance at altitude. The county's political significance derives from William Ruto's representation of Uasin Gishu's Sugoi constituency and his rise to the presidency (2022).
