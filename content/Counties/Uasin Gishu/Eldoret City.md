# Eldoret City

Eldoret is Kenya's fifth-largest city and the capital of [[../Uasin Gishu/Uasin Gishu County|Uasin Gishu County]], located in the western Rift Valley at approximately 2,100 meters elevation. The city was founded by European settlers in the early 20th century as a settler farming community and administrative center. The name "Eldoret" derives from [[../../Maasai/Maasai|Maasai]] "El Derit", meaning "sandy river", referring to local water features.

The city developed as a commercial and agricultural center serving the surrounding farming region. After independence, Eldoret grew steadily as the population increased and [[../../Kalenjin/Kalenjin|Kalenjin]] community developed the city as a regional hub. The city became politically significant during the Moi presidency (1978-2002), when Kalenjin political dominance meant government investment and patronage benefited Eldoret. Moi University, established in 1984 and named after the president, became Eldoret's anchor educational institution.

Eldoret today is a bustling city with modern commercial buildings, hotels, restaurants, and retail establishments serving residents and visitors. The city is a transport hub with road connections to Nairobi, Kisumu, Kericho, and other regions. Eldoret International Airport, upgraded in recent years, serves as a cargo and passenger facility. The city's economy is based on agricultural marketing (particularly wheat and dairy), light industry, services, and increasingly, real estate development. Eldoret's cool altitude and athletic tradition have made it an important destination for distance running training camps.
