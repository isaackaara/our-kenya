## Historical Timeline

1900s,1920s: British colonial administration establishes administrative centres and military installations in the Northern Frontier District, including present-day Isiolo area.

1930s: Colonial government expands administrative infrastructure in Isiolo, creating district headquarters and administrative offices.

1940s: World War II military activities, including defensive installations and supply lines through northern Kenya, affect Isiolo region.

1960: Kenyan independence movement gains momentum. Isiolo begins transition to post-colonial governance.

1963: Kenya gains independence. Northern Frontier District remains administratively distinct, managed through special colonial-era policies.

1967: Northern Frontier District integrated into regular Kenyan administrative system following 1967 referendum.

1970s: Wildlife tourism expands. Samburu, Buffalo Springs, and Shaba national reserves become established destinations.

1980: Joy Adamson murdered at Shaba National Reserve on January 3, shocking international conservation community.

1990s: Pastoral production faces increasing pressure from population growth and land use change. Inter-communal conflicts intensify.

2000s: LAPSSET Corridor project conceived as regional development framework linking Horn of Africa with Indian Ocean ports.

2010: Kenya adopts new Constitution establishing devolved government system.

2013: First Isiolo County elections held following constitutional devolution. County government established.

2015: LAPSSET Corridor implementation begins with infrastructure development projects.

2019: Severe drought affects pastoral production and food security across northern Kenya.

2022,2023: Prolonged drought continues. Humanitarian response addresses food emergencies.

2026: Isiolo County development continues within LAPSSET framework. Climate change impacts intensify.
