# [[../../Conservation/Lake Turkana|Lake Turkana]] Wind Power Project

The Lake Turkana Wind Power project (LTWP) represents the largest wind farm in Africa and a transformative energy and development project in Marsabit County. The project harnesses the region's consistent winds for electricity generation and transmission to Kenya's national grid.

The project is located in Loiyangalani in Marsabit County, where geographical and climatic conditions create strong, consistent winds suitable for large-scale wind power generation. The proposed facility includes numerous wind turbines and associated infrastructure.

Development of Lake Turkana Wind Power began in the mid-2000s with feasibility studies and environmental impact assessments. Project financing involved private investors, government agencies, and development finance institutions. The project received government approval and environmental clearance.

The facility generates substantial electricity for Kenya's national grid, contributing to national energy supply. Wind power represents renewable, zero-carbon energy production compared to fossil fuel power plants. The project supports Kenya's renewable energy targets.

Economic benefits include construction employment during development phases, permanent operational employment, and financial benefits to government through taxation and royalties. Local community employment and supply contracts create income opportunities.

Environmental and social impacts remain contested. Water resource concerns arise from construction and operational activities in an arid region. Land use displacement affects pastoral communities. Wildlife habitat impacts require assessment and mitigation.

Community engagement and benefit-sharing mechanisms aim to ensure local communities share project benefits. Revenue allocation to county government and community development projects represents part of benefit-sharing arrangements.

Implementation challenges have included financing delays, environmental concerns, and community consultation processes. Project completion and operational status have varied with these developments.
