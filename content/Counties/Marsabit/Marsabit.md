# Marsabit

Marsabit is a semi-arid district in northern Kenya, encompassing the volcanic Marsabit Mountain and surrounding semi-desert plains. Home to multiple pastoral and trading communities including Rendille, Samburu, Borana, and Somali groups.

## Geography

Marsabit Mountain, rising to 1,707 meters, dominates the landscape and creates a highland microclimate with forests and water sources in an otherwise arid region. The district encompasses approximately 66,000 km² of rangeland, characterized by scattered water points and sparse vegetation.

## Communities and Economy

### Pastoral Communities
- Rendille: Primarily camel herders
- Samburu: Cattle and small stock herders
- Borana: Pastoral communities with ties to Ethiopia and southern Kenya
- Somali: Trading and pastoral communities

### Economy
- Pastoral livelihoods based on camels, cattle, goats, and sheep
- Trade in livestock and agricultural products
- Wildlife tourism (Marsabit National Park)
- Subsistence agriculture in highland zones

## Challenges

- Severe drought cycles (1984, 2011, 2016-2017, 2022)
- Insecurity and pastoral conflicts
- Limited government services and infrastructure
- Climate change intensifying aridity
