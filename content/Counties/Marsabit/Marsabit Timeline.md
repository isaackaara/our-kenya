## Historical Timeline

1890s,1900s: British colonial forces establish control over northern territories through military expeditions and administrative occupation. Marsabit region becomes part of the Northern Frontier District (NFD).

1920s,1940s: Colonial administration establishes administrative centres including Marsabit town. Light-touch indirect rule systems developed.

1940s,1950s: World War II military activities and post-war infrastructure development expand in northern Kenya. Limited development focus compared to other regions.

1960: Kenya independence movement gains momentum. Marsabit remains administratively peripheral.

1963: Kenya gains independence. Northern Frontier District remains administratively distinct.

1967: Northern Frontier District integrated into regular Kenyan administrative system.

1970s,1980s: Wildlife tourism expands. Marsabit National Park becomes established destination. Pastoral communities experience increasing population pressure and resource competition.

1980s,1990s: Drought cycles create food crises. Inter-communal conflicts intensify over water and pasture competition. Government security responses increase.

2000s: LAPSSET Corridor project conceived as regional development framework. [[Lake Turkana|Lake Turkana]] Wind Power project planning begins.

2010: Kenya adopts new Constitution establishing devolved government system.

2013: First Marsabit County elections held following constitutional devolution. County government established.

2015: Lake Turkana Wind Power project development continues. Infrastructure and investment arrive in Loiyangalani area.

2019,2023: Severe and prolonged drought affects pastoral production and food security. Humanitarian response addresses food emergencies. Climate change impacts intensify.

2026: Marsabit County development continues with wind power and county development initiatives. Climate impacts remain critical challenge.
