Marsabit town is the county headquarters, situated on the forested slopes of Mount Marsabit. The town occupies a unique position as a highland settlement rising above the surrounding Chalbi Desert, creating distinctive climatic and ecological conditions.

The town serves as the administrative centre for Marsabit County government. Government offices, courts, and official buildings cluster in the town centre. The presence of county government institutions provides employment and services to surrounding communities.

Marsabit town functions as a commercial hub for the pastoral region. Markets facilitate livestock and goods trading. Shops and businesses serve local populations and passing traders. The town provides banking, postal, and telecommunication services.

The forested environment of Mount Marsabit creates temperate conditions contrasting sharply with the surrounding desert. This creates an oasis effect, with different vegetation and cooler temperatures than adjacent lowlands. The forest also provides forest products including honey and firewood.

Tourism infrastructure has developed around Mount Marsabit, with hotels and guides serving visitors interested in wildlife, culture, and scenery. The National Park and crater lakes attract international and domestic tourists.

Transportation connections link Marsabit town to Isiolo and Ethiopia via the A2 highway, though road conditions vary seasonally. The town serves as a rest point for travellers on longer journeys.

Settlement patterns reflect the town's administrative and commercial importance, with population concentrations in commercial districts and government quarters. Housing ranges from formal government buildings to informal structures housing lower-income residents.

Water access in Marsabit town relies on boreholes and rainwater harvesting from the forested mountain, providing relative water security compared to surrounding areas.
