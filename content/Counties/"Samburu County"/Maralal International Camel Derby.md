The Maralal International Camel Derby is an annual event held in Maralal, Samburu County, showcasing camel racing and Samburu pastoral culture. Held typically in August, the event attracts international participants, international media, and tourists from around the world.

The event features camel races where competitors ride camels in competitive racing events. Camel racing requires skill and understanding of animal behaviour. Races draw riders from Samburu and neighbouring pastoral communities alongside international participants.

Cultural performances including traditional dances, songs, and ceremonies accompany the camel racing. These performances showcase Samburu cultural traditions, warrior culture, and pastoral identity. Performers represent Samburu communities and invite tourist participation.

The event originated in the 1970s as a modest local celebration and expanded into an international event through tourism development. International media coverage brought the event global recognition.

Tourism revenue from the camel derby supports local businesses including hotels, restaurants, and tour operators. The event generates income for cultural performers, camel owners, and service providers.

Cultural expression through the camel derby maintains and celebrates Samburu pastoral traditions. The event serves as a platform for younger people to learn and participate in traditional cultural practices.

International participation and media coverage position Samburu culture in global consciousness. However, concerns arise about cultural commodification and authenticity when traditional practices are performed for external audiences.

The event depends on weather conditions, security stability, and tourist interest. Periodic cancellations or disruptions due to drought, conflict, or other circumstances affect the event's continuity.

Economic benefits and cultural importance make the camel derby a significant annual event for Samburu County, contributing to tourism development and cultural preservation.
