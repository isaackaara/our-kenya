Maralal is Samburu County's headquarters, a highland town situated at approximately 2,300 meters elevation in the Samburu landscape. The town's higher elevation creates cooler, more temperate conditions than surrounding pastoral rangelands, providing relief from the county's lower-altitude heat.

The town functions as the administrative centre for Samburu County government. Government offices, courts, and administrative buildings cluster in the town centre. Government presence provides employment and services to surrounding communities.

Maralal serves as a commercial hub for the pastoral region. Markets facilitate livestock trading and goods commerce. Shops and businesses serve local populations and passing traders. The town provides banking, postal, and telecommunication services.

The highland location supports vegetation different from surrounding rangelands. Trees and shrubs provide forest products including honey, poles, and fuelwood. This vegetation contrasts with the sparser scrubland typical of lower-altitude pastoral areas.

The Maralal International Camel Derby, held annually in Maralal, attracts international visitors, media, and participants. The event showcases Samburu pastoral culture through camel racing and cultural performances. Tourism revenue from the event supports local businesses and cultural organisations.

Transportation connections link Maralal to Isiolo and other towns via the A65 highway, though road conditions vary seasonally. The town serves as a rest point for travellers on longer journeys.

Settlement patterns reflect the town's administrative and commercial importance. Population concentrations in commercial districts and government quarters create a distinct urban centre within the pastoral region.

Water access in Maralal relies on boreholes and seasonal water sources, though the highland location provides relatively better water availability than surrounding lowlands.

Development initiatives including education, health, and commercial projects centre on Maralal as the logical growth point for county services.
