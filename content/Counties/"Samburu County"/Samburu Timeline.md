## Historical Timeline

1890s,1900s: British colonial forces establish control over northern territories through military expeditions and administrative occupation. Samburu region becomes part of colonial administration.

1920s,1940s: Colonial administration establishes administrative centres including Maralal. Light-touch indirect rule systems developed through local headmen.

1940s,1950s: World War II military activities and post-war infrastructure development occur in northern Kenya. Limited development focus compared to other regions.

1960: Kenya independence movement gains momentum. Samburu remains administratively peripheral to independence politics.

1963: Kenya gains independence. Samburu District becomes a regular administrative district.

1970s: Wildlife tourism expands. Samburu National Reserve becomes established destination. Pastoral communities experience increasing population pressure and resource competition.

1970s,1980s: The Maralal International Camel Derby originates as a local celebration and expands into an international event attracting international participants and media.

1980s,1990s: Drought cycles create food crises. Inter-communal conflicts intensify over water and pasture competition. Government security responses increase.

2000s: Development initiatives focus on pastoral livelihood improvement and wildlife conservation. Community conservancy concepts emerge.

2010: Kenya adopts new Constitution establishing devolved government system.

2013: First Samburu County elections held following constitutional devolution. County government established.

2015,2020s: Tourism development continues. Camel derby event attracts growing international attention. Community conservancy initiatives expand.

2019,2023: Severe and prolonged drought affects pastoral production and food security. Humanitarian response addresses food emergencies. Climate change impacts intensify.

2026: Samburu County development continues with tourism and pastoral livelihood initiatives. Climate change represents critical ongoing challenge.
