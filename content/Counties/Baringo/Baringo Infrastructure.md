Baringo County's infrastructure encompasses roads, water supply systems, electricity networks, and telecommunications services, with development concentrated on main transportation corridors and urban centers while pastoral areas remain underserved.

## Road Network

Main roads connect Kabarnet to Nairobi and regional centers. The Kabarnet-Nairobi road has received improvements in recent years to enhance road quality. Secondary roads connecting to Lake Baringo, pastoral areas, and regional centers remain in variable condition.

Topographic challenges including the Kerio Valley create transportation obstacles limiting road development in some areas.

## Lake Baringo Infrastructure

Tourism infrastructure around Lake Baringo includes roads, water supply, electricity, and accommodation facilities. Island development including Island Camp represents a significant tourism investment.

Water transport and ferry services connect island communities.

## Water Infrastructure

Water supply infrastructure includes boreholes, piped water systems in towns, and water ponds for pastoral use. Water supply remains inadequate, particularly in pastoral areas where water scarcity constrains populations.

Water development projects have focused on pastoral areas with boreholes and conservation techniques, though coverage remains limited.

## Electricity and Energy

Urban areas including Kabarnet have grid electricity supply. Rural areas increasingly use solar and off-grid electricity solutions. Renewable energy development including solar has expanded electricity access.

## Telecommunications

Mobile phone coverage has expanded substantially, providing communication access across much of the county. Internet connectivity remains variable, with better access in towns.
