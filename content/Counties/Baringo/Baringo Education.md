Baringo County's education sector provides primary and secondary schooling to pastoral, agricultural, and urban communities, with challenges of limited resources, quality variations, and adapting formal education to pastoral livelihood contexts.

## School Infrastructure

Primary schools distributed across Baringo serve pastoral, agricultural, and urban communities. Secondary schools concentrate in Kabarnet and other trading centers.

School infrastructure faces resource constraints including limited facilities, inadequate teaching materials, and difficulties in teacher recruitment and retention.

## Pastoral Community Education

Pastoral communities historically had limited formal education participation due to pastoral occupation demands and cultural values prioritizing pastoral over academic education.

Contemporary education enrollment has increased with government free education policies, though pastoral families continue facing challenges balancing school attendance with pastoral responsibilities.

## Education Quality

Education quality remains variable, with examination performance reflecting resource and teacher quality disparities. Teacher recruitment and retention remain ongoing challenges.

## Curriculum Relevance

National curriculum applicability to pastoral contexts remains limited. Curriculum incorporating pastoral knowledge systems and livelihood skills could improve education relevance and retention.

## Girls' Education

Girls' education faces particular challenges in pastoral communities with early marriage and domestic responsibilities reducing school participation. Girls' education rates have improved with incentive programs.
