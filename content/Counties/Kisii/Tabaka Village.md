Tabaka village, located in southern [[Kisii County.md|Kisii County]], is internationally renowned as the center of Kenya's soapstone carving industry. The village and surrounding areas produce the majority of Kenya's soapstone sculptures and carvings that are exported worldwide.

## Location and Setting

Tabaka is situated in:
- Southern [[Kisii County.md|Kisii County]]
- Accessible from Kisii town
- Surrounded by rural agricultural areas
- Dense population supporting carving activity
- Natural deposits of soapstone

The village has developed organically around soapstone deposits and carving tradition.

## Soapstone Quarrying

Quarrying operations include:
- Small-scale artisanal quarrying
- Manual extraction techniques
- Multiple quarry sites throughout the area
- Family and community involvement in quarrying
- Stone quality variation by location
- Environmental impacts from extraction

Stone extraction provides raw material for carving.

## Carving Tradition

The soapstone carving tradition features:
- Centuries-old artistic practice
- Hand carving using simple tools
- Apprenticeship system transmitting skills
- Family-based carving workshops
- Male-dominated craft activity
- Diverse artistic styles and designs

Carving represents important cultural practice and economic activity reflecting [[Gusii People Kisii.md|Gusii]] heritage.

## Carved Products

Artisans produce:
- Decorative sculptures and figurines
- Functional bowls and vessels
- Animal and human figure carvings
- Ornamental items and souvenirs
- Religious and spiritual pieces
- Contemporary artistic creations

Products appeal to tourists, collectors, and export markets.

## Economic Importance

The carving industry provides:
- Employment for thousands of carvers
- Income for quarriers and helpers
- Livelihood for trading and export businesses
- Community wealth accumulation
- Foreign exchange from exports
- Tourism activity supporting related services

The industry is Tabaka's primary economic activity.

## Markets and Trade

Products reach markets through:
- Local market sales to tourists
- Traders purchasing for resale
- Wholesale export to international buyers
- Individual artist-direct sales
- Art galleries and retail outlets
- Online commerce and international sales

Carvers market products through diverse channels.

## Challenges

The industry faces obstacles including:
- Declining soapstone deposits
- Market price volatility
- Competition from imported crafts
- Limited diversification
- Environmental degradation from quarrying
- Occupational health risks for carvers
- Youth migration from the sector

## Preservation and Development

Efforts to support the sector include:
- Skills training and apprenticeship programs
- Quality improvement initiatives
- Market development and export support
- Environmental management practices
- Cooperative organization of carvers
- Cultural heritage documentation

## Tourism

Tabaka attracts tourists including:
- Visitors from Kenya and abroad
- Craft buyers and collectors
- Cultural tourism participants
- Tour operators including Tabaka as destination
- Accommodation and restaurant services
- Local guide and transportation services

Tourism provides additional income opportunities.

## Cultural Significance

Beyond economic importance, soapstone carving:
- Preserves artistic and cultural heritage
- Transmits traditional skills
- Expresses [[Gusii People Kisii.md|Gusii]] identity and creativity
- Attracts international cultural attention
- Contributes to Kenya's global cultural reputation
