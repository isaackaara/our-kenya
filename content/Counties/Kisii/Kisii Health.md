Healthcare in [[Kisii County.md|Kisii County]] is delivered through a network of public and private facilities, with Kisii Teaching and Referral Hospital serving as the county's main health facility. Health outcomes reflect the county's economic development and access to healthcare services.

## Kisii Teaching and Referral Hospital

The county's flagship health institution provides:
- Emergency and trauma services
- Surgical and medical specialties
- Maternal and child health services
- Out-patient consultation services
- Training facilities for health professionals
- Referral services for complex cases

The hospital plays a critical role in the county health system and serves patients from neighboring counties as a regional referral center.

## Health System Structure

The county health system includes:
- Primary health centers and dispensaries in sub-counties and wards
- Community health volunteer networks
- Private clinics and nursing homes
- Maternity and child health facilities
- Nutritional and disease surveillance programs

## Key Health Challenges

Kisii faces health challenges including:
- High malaria incidence in lower-altitude areas
- Waterborne diseases affecting communities with limited water access
- Maternal and infant mortality rates above national averages
- Communicable disease burden
- Limited capacity in rural facilities
- Shortage of specialized medical personnel

## Health Service Delivery

County government has expanded health service coverage through:
- Training additional community health workers
- Improving facility infrastructure
- Providing maternal and child health programs
- Implementing disease surveillance systems
- Strengthening laboratory and diagnostic services

## Maternal and Child Health

Special emphasis is placed on:
- Antenatal and postnatal care programs
- Skilled birth attendance promotion
- Immunization services
- Nutrition programs for vulnerable populations
- Family planning and reproductive health services
