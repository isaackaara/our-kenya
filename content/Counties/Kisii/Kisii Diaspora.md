Significant numbers of [[Kisii County.md|Kisii]] people reside outside their home county, particularly in Nairobi and other Kenyan cities, as well as internationally. The diaspora maintains connections to home while building lives and careers in other locations.

## Migration to Nairobi

Nairobi hosts the largest diaspora population:
- Young adults seeking employment and educational opportunities
- Families relocating for economic advancement
- Professionals and business people
- Informal settlements housing migrant communities
- Established residential neighborhoods with [[Kisii County.md|Kisii]] populations

Nairobi's economic opportunities drive substantial migration from the county.

## Other Kenyan Urban Centers

[[Kisii County.md|Kisii]] people reside in:
- Kericho and other western Kenya cities
- Kisumu and Lake Region cities
- Mombasa and coastal cities
- Nakuru and Rift Valley cities
- Other regional centers offering employment

Urban migration patterns reflect economic opportunity distribution across Kenya.

## International Migration

Some Kisii people have relocated internationally:
- Migration to East African countries
- Settlement in Southern African countries
- Movement to Western Europe and North America
- Professionals working abroad
- Students studying in international institutions
- Economic migrants and refugees

International migration provides opportunities though often involves separation from family.

## Reasons for Migration

Push and pull factors include:
- Limited land and economic opportunity in home county
- Unemployment in rural areas
- Educational advancement opportunities
- Urban employment and business opportunities
- Family reunification
- Escape from rural poverty

Economic motivation drives most migration decisions.

## Diaspora Connections to Home

Migrant communities maintain linkages including:
- Remittances sent to support families at home
- Periodic visits to county during holidays
- Investment in property and business in home county
- Political participation through voting (for eligible citizens)
- Cultural association participation
- Family obligations and responsibilities

## Diaspora Contributions

Diaspora communities contribute to home county development:
- Financial remittances supporting family livelihood
- Investment in agricultural and business enterprises
- Funding of infrastructure and community projects
- Knowledge and skills transfer
- Technology and modern practice introduction
- Healthcare and education support

Remittances from diaspora represent significant income source for many rural families.

## Challenges and Vulnerabilities

Diaspora members face challenges including:
- Housing insecurity in urban areas
- Limited formal employment and job insecurity
- Discrimination and social marginalization
- Family separation and relationship strain
- Vulnerability to exploitation
- Limited access to social services

## Cultural Preservation

Diaspora communities work to preserve culture:
- Cultural associations and community organizations
- Language transmission to children
- Celebration of cultural events
- Religious community participation
- Maintaining identity and heritage

## Return Migration

Some diaspora members return:
- Retirement and settling at home
- Business and investment ventures
- Family care responsibilities
- Seasonal circulation between home and city
- Economic downturns forcing return
