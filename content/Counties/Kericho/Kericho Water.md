Kericho County benefits from abundant water resources reflecting its position as one of Kenya's highest-rainfall regions, with reliable water availability supporting both human consumption and agricultural production. Natural water sources include numerous springs, streams, and rivers that flow from the highland areas toward the surrounding lowlands. These water sources have historically provided water for pastoralist and agricultural communities.

Contemporary water infrastructure includes piped water systems serving urban areas and some rural settlements, providing treated water to households and institutions. Water supply challenges include contamination from agricultural runoff and domestic waste, requiring treatment and management. Rural areas with limited piped water access depend on springs, boreholes, and collected rainwater for domestic use. Water demand exceeds supply in some areas, particularly during dry seasons when stream flow declines. Tea estates historically constructed and managed their own water systems, providing water to estates and worker communities. Competition for water between agricultural users, domestic consumption, and environmental flows presents ongoing management challenges. Climate change impacts on rainfall patterns threaten water availability despite the region's relatively abundant resources. Investment in water infrastructure, conservation, and management represents a development priority for county government and communities.
