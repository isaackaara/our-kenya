# Kericho Real Estate

Kericho's real estate market reflects the region's economic prosperity, pleasant climate, and appeal as a residential destination. Property values in Kericho town and surrounding areas have appreciated significantly, particularly in recent decades as wealth increased and infrastructure improved. Residential properties including houses and apartments are owned by both local residents and external investors seeking highland investment properties.

Commercial properties including shops, offices, and restaurants cluster in Kericho town's central business district, where demand remains strong from traders and service providers. Agricultural land including tea estates and smallholder plots represents significant real estate value, though transactions are sometimes complicated by historical disputes over land ownership and titling. The real estate market has attracted external investors including land developers, builders, and foreign nationals seeking holiday homes or investment properties. Rising land values have created both opportunities for land-owning communities and concerns about affordability and displacement of lower-income residents. Property development has accelerated in recent years, with new residential estates and commercial complexes under construction. The real estate market also includes informal housing settlements on urban peripheries, where low-income residents occupy properties with insecure tenure. Land speculation and the conversion of agricultural land to residential use remain ongoing trends.
