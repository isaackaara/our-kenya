This timeline documents major events and developments in Nyandarua County's history through 2026.

## Pre-Colonial Period
- Traditional [[Kikuyu|Kikuyu]] settlement and land use
- Sacred groves and ceremonial sites
- Age-set initiation systems and social organization
- Pastoral and agricultural economy

## Colonial Period (1890-1963)
- 1890s: European colonial establishment in Aberdare region
- 1904: Kikuyu Reserve established, excluding Africans from highlands
- 1920s: Limited African settlement in some areas
- 1950s: Aberdare National Park established
- 1952-1960: [[Mau Mau Uprising|Mau Mau]] uprising, significant Nyandarua participation
- 1960: State of Emergency lifted

## Post-Independence Period (1964-1989)
- 1964: Kenya independence
- 1965-1975: Land redistribution schemes to African settlers
- 1970s: Smallholder agricultural development expansion
- 1980s: Wheat production increases

## Modern Development (1990-2012)
- 1990s: Agricultural market liberalization
- 2000s: Productivity improvement focus
- 2005: Constitutional reform discussions
- 2010: New constitution with devolution provisions

## Devolution Era (2013-Present)
- 2013: Nyandarua County government established
- 2014: County development initiatives launched
- 2015: Water and infrastructure projects
- 2017: National political upheaval impacts
- 2020: COVID-19 pandemic effects
- 2022: Development plan updates
- 2023: Climate change impacts evident
- 2024: Infrastructure expansion continues
- 2025: Agricultural and development focus
- 2026: Ongoing development and challenges

## Agricultural Development
- 1960s-1970s: Settler farm transition to smallholder agriculture
- 1980s-1990s: Cooperative system formalization
- 2000s: Wheat quality improvement
- 2010s: Sustainability and value addition focus
- 2020s: Climate adaptation and resilience building

## Infrastructure Development
- 1960s-1970s: Road network establishment
- 1980s-1990s: Road improvement and maintenance
- 2000s: Electricity grid expansion
- 2010s: Water supply system improvements
- 2020s: Digital infrastructure expansion

## Environmental Trends
- 1960s-1980s: Forest clearing for agriculture
- 1990s-2000s: Early conservation efforts
- 2010s: Protected area management emphasis
- 2020s: Climate adaptation and ecosystem management
