# Makueni Timeline

Key dates in Makueni County history through 2026:

1895: Africa Inland Mission (AIM) establishes missionary presence in Kamba regions, beginning systematic Christian conversion.

1903: British colonial administration establishes formal governance structures in the Makueni region.

1910s-1920s: Christian missionary institutions expand in Makueni, establishing schools and health services.

1950s: Mau Mau conflict affects Makueni through colonial counterinsurgency operations and detention camps.

1963: Kenya gains independence; Makueni becomes part of the postcolonial nation.

1970s-1990s: Makueni experiences development challenges including environmental degradation and limited infrastructure investment.

1992: Kenya transitions to multiparty democracy; Makueni voters participate in the first multiparty elections.

1995: Utooni Development Organisation formalizes sand dam technology and begins promoting water harvesting innovation.

2000s: Water harvesting and sand dam technologies spread across Makueni in response to water scarcity.

2010: New constitution establishes devolved governance; Makueni County government structure created.

2013: Kibwana elected as Makueni Governor; begins implementing innovative governance programmes.

2014: Makueni County implements universal health coverage (UHC) programme, pioneering county-level health financing innovation.

2017: Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) opens, passing through Makueni and enhancing transport connectivity.

2018: Makueni County mango processing plant established as development infrastructure investment.

2020: COVID-19 pandemic affects Makueni economy and health services; adaptation and recovery underway.

2022: Kibwana completes two-term governor tenure; new governor elected under constitutional limits.

2026: Makueni County continues managing devolved governance, health service delivery, and development priorities.
