# West Pokot County

West Pokot County is in the northwestern Rift Valley region of Kenya, bordering Uganda. The county has approximately 700,000 people. [[../../Kalenjin/Kalenjin|Kalenjin]] (also called Suk) are the dominant Kalenjin sub-group. Kapenguria is the county headquarters. West Pokot is predominantly pastoral with livestock as primary livelihood. The county has dramatic topography from hot arid lowlands to cool highlands exceeding 2,500 meters.

## Overview
The landscape transitions from Rift Valley floor (approximately 1,000 meters) to highlands exceeding 2,000 meters. Water is a critical limiting resource in pastoral zones. The county experiences periodic droughts. Inter-communal cattle raiding and conflict are recurring challenges.

## Key Sites
- Kapenguria Town (county headquarters)
- Cherangany Hills (water tower)
- Mount Sekerr
- Nasalot National Reserve
- Jomo Kenyatta detention site
