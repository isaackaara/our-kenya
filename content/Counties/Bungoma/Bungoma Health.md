Healthcare in Bungoma County is provided through government health facilities, private clinics, and traditional practitioners. Bungoma County Referral Hospital serves as the primary government facility providing specialized services. Health system capacity has expanded through devolution though challenges from demand, resources, and disease burden persist.

## Bungoma County Referral Hospital

Bungoma County Referral Hospital is the major government healthcare facility providing inpatient, outpatient, emergency, and specialized services. The hospital serves as the referral point for cases from lower-level health facilities. Hospital capacity includes multiple wards, operating theatres, and diagnostic equipment.

## Health Facility Network

Primary health centres and dispensaries distributed throughout Bungoma provide basic outpatient and maternal health services. Community health workers extend services to remote areas. Private clinics and pharmacies complement government services, serving populations with ability to pay. Religious organizations operate some health facilities.

## Health Challenges

Communicable diseases including malaria, respiratory infections, and water-borne diseases remain significant health burdens. Maternal and child health indicators reflect developmental constraints. Non-communicable diseases including hypertension and diabetes are increasing. Nutrition and food security issues affect health outcomes.

## Service Delivery Constraints

Health facilities face staffing limitations and equipment insufficiency. Medication availability fluctuates. Infrastructure maintenance requires funding. Distance to facilities constrains rural access. User fees, despite government free healthcare policy, remain barriers for low-income populations.

## Health Promotion and Prevention

Community health education focuses on disease prevention and healthy behaviours. Vaccination programmes target key population groups. Water and sanitation projects aim to reduce disease transmission. Health promotion activities address maternal and child health priorities.
