# [[../../Conservation/Mount Elgon National Park|Mount Elgon]] Caves

Mount Elgon's volcanic geology creates numerous caves including Kitum Cave and other formations. These caves have served historically as shelter, salt mining sites, and elephant visitation destinations. Kitum Cave has received international scientific attention for its unusual geology and wildlife activity. The caves represent unique geological and ecological features contributing to Mount Elgon's ecological significance.

## Kitum Cave

Kitum Cave is the most renowned Mount Elgon cave, featuring distinctive geological formations and unusual underground features. The cave is known for elephant visitation, with elephants entering to mine salt deposits. The cave also attracts researchers and tourists interested in geological and wildlife phenomena. Scientific research has investigated elephant behaviour and cave ecology.

## Elephant Salt Mining

African elephants periodically visit Mount Elgon caves, particularly Kitum Cave, to extract salt and minerals by digging into cave walls. This behaviour is unique and of significant scientific interest. Elephant visitation occurs seasonally with patterns reflecting dietary needs and water availability. The elephant mining activity has shaped cave geology through selective mineral extraction.

## Cave Geology

Mount Elgon's volcanic geology creates diverse cave formations. Lava tubes, mineral deposits, and other formations reflect volcanic history. Some caves contain mineral deposits of ecological and economic interest. Speleological research documents cave formations and geological processes.

## Wildlife Ecology

Caves provide habitat for various wildlife species adapted to underground environments. Bats utilize caves as roosting sites. Birds may nest in cave entrances. Insects adapted to cave conditions are present. The unique cave ecology supports specialized species.

## Human Use and Tourism

Caves have served historically as shelter and refudia for human populations. Contemporary tourism visits caves as points of interest. Guided tours facilitate cave access and interpretation. Tourism provides some local income generation.

## Conservation Status

Mount Elgon National Park protects caves within park boundaries. Some caves face threats from human visitation impacts and mineral extraction. Conservation efforts aim to maintain geological and ecological integrity. Research on cave ecology contributes to conservation planning.
