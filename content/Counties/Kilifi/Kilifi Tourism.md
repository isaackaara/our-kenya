# Kilifi Tourism

Tourism is the dominant economic sector in Kilifi County, attracting approximately 300,000 international tourists annually and generating substantial foreign exchange revenue. Malindi and Watamu beach resorts concentrate most tourism activity, with hotel employment and supporting service provision creating thousands of jobs. Tourism infrastructure including resort hotels, restaurants, transportation services, and entertainment facilities reflects the sector's economic importance.

The tourism economy extends beyond beach resorts to include cultural heritage tourism. Malindi's historical sites including the Vasco da Gama Pillar attract history-interested tourists. Arabuko-Sokoke Forest attracts birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. These diverse attractions create tourism demand beyond beach resort experiences.

Tourism employment provides wages for thousands, though wages often remain low and employment is sometimes seasonal or insecure. Hotel workers, guides, cooks, cleaners, and support staff depend on tourism income. Informal sector workers including massage providers, craft vendors, and restaurant operators also depend on tourism spending.

Tourism infrastructure development has concentrated along beaches and in established resort areas. This has created service provision disparities, with resorts having reliable water and electricity while neighboring communities lack adequate services. Tourism development has sometimes prioritized resort infrastructure over community service provision.

Environmental impacts from tourism include water consumption, sewage generation, and marine pollution from resort operations. Coastal erosion from development and climate change threatens infrastructure and beach environments. Careful environmental management is essential to maintain tourism sustainability.

Tourism revenue remains volatile, affected by international economic conditions, security concerns, and health crises. The COVID-19 pandemic devastated Kilifi's tourism economy, throwing thousands into unemployment. Economic recovery has been gradual.
