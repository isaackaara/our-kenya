# Watamu

Watamu is a small beach village and marine resort destination located between Malindi and Kilifi on Kilifi County's coast. The village has a population of approximately 5,000 permanent residents, with population increasing substantially during tourist seasons. Watamu is famous for the Watamu Marine National Park, hawksbill turtle conservation, and pristine coral reefs making it a premier marine tourism destination.

The Watamu Marine National Park protects coral reefs, fish populations, and other marine species from fishing and damaging activities. The park encompasses 10 square kilometers of marine area and prohibits fishing while allowing snorkeling, diving, and other non-extractive uses. The marine park has recovered significantly from fishing pressure, with increased fish populations and coral coverage compared to unprotected areas.

Watamu has become particularly famous for hawksbill turtle conservation. Nesting hawksbill turtles arrive seasonally to lay eggs on Watamu beaches. Conservation programs protect nests, monitor hatchlings, and conduct research on turtle populations. These conservation efforts have increased nesting populations and public awareness of marine turtle threats. Tourism revenue partially funds turtle conservation work.

The coral reefs around Watamu support extraordinary fish biodiversity, with hundreds of fish species visible to snorkelers and divers. The reefs provide food and income for local fishing communities while attracting tourists paying for snorkeling and diving experiences. Balancing fishing access with tourism conservation creates ongoing tensions.

Beach resort development in Watamu remains less intensive than in Diani or Malindi, with smaller properties and a quieter resort atmosphere. The village atmosphere appeals to tourists seeking more intimate experiences than large resort complexes provide. Several small and medium-sized hotels, vacation rental properties, and beachfront restaurants serve tourists.

Fishing remains an important livelihood for Watamu residents, with artisanal fishers harvesting reef fish, octopuses, and crustaceans. Fishing communities coexist with tourism development, though marine protected area designation restricts fishing in park areas. Fishing communities sometimes express tensions about conservation restrictions limiting fishing access to productive areas.

Infrastructure development in Watamu has been focused on tourism support, with roads, electricity, and water supply serving resorts and tourism facilities. Expansion of basic services to all residents remains ongoing. Water scarcity during dry seasons affects both residents and tourism facilities.
