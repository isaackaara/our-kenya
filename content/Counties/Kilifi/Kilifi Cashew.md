# Kilifi Cashew

Cashew nut farming has become increasingly important in Kilifi County, with farmers recognizing income-generation opportunities. Cashew cultivation in Kilifi is less extensive than in Kwale but continues expanding. The industry generates millions in annual income for farmers and processors, creating employment throughout value chains from cultivation through export.

Cashew trees tolerate Kilifi's climate and soils well, with cultivation expanding particularly in inland areas away from intense tourist development. Farmers have invested in cashew plantations, with mature trees providing reliable annual production. Raw cashews cannot be eaten directly but must be processed to extract the valuable kernel.

Cashew processing occurs in local facilities and internationally, with some processing occurring in Kenya and some export of raw nuts for processing elsewhere. Processing provides employment for workers engaged in roasting, shell removal, kernel sorting, and grading.

Market prices for cashew kernels fluctuate based on global supply and demand, creating income uncertainty for farmers. Better-connected farmers receive higher prices than those with limited market information.
