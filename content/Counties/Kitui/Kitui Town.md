Kitui town is the county headquarters and primary commercial centre of Kitui County. Located in the central region of the county, Kitui town functions as the administrative and economic focal point. The town is relatively modest in size compared to major Kenyan urban centres, reflecting both the county's underdevelopment and semi-arid environment.

Kitui town has grown gradually as the seat of county government and principal commercial hub. Government offices, administrative facilities, and county services concentrate in Kitui town. The town provides banking, trading, and services for the broader county population.

Market activities in Kitui town reflect agricultural and pastoral production from surrounding areas. Livestock marketing occurs regularly, with cattle, goats, and sheep from pastoral communities entering the town's markets. Agricultural products from farming areas provide market commodities.

The town's infrastructure has expanded with devolved governance, though improvements remain limited. Roads, water supply, and public facilities have seen investment but remain basic compared to larger Kenyan towns. Water scarcity limits the town's growth and service provision.

Kitui town hosts county government offices, health facilities, and educational institutions serving the county population. The town serves as a commercial centre for wholesale and retail trading connecting rural producers to consumers.

Urban unemployment and limited employment opportunities make the town relatively quiet economically. Most employment relates to government and small-scale commerce. Youth migration to larger towns limits town growth.

The town's physical appearance reflects resource constraints, with basic infrastructure and limited modern development. Nevertheless, Kitui town remains central to county identity and governance.
