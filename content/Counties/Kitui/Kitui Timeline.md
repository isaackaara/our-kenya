# Kitui Timeline

Key dates in Kitui County history through 2026:

1849: Johann Ludwig Krapf becomes first European to observe [[../../Conservation/Mount Kenya National Park|Mount Kenya]] from Kitui region, contributing to European geographical knowledge.

1895: Africa Inland Mission (AIM) establishes missionary presence in Kamba regions of Kitui.

1903: British colonial administration establishes formal governance structures in Kitui.

1910s-1920s: Christian missionary institutions expand in Kitui, establishing schools and health services.

1950s: Mau Mau conflict affects Kitui through colonial counterinsurgency operations.

1963: Kenya gains independence; Kitui becomes part of the postcolonial nation.

1970s-2000: Kitui experiences development challenges including environmental degradation, limited infrastructure investment, and underdevelopment relative to other regions.

1992: Kenya transitions to multiparty democracy; Kitui voters participate in multiparty elections.

2000s: Geological surveys identify massive coal deposits at Mui Basin, initiating development debate.

2010: New constitution establishes devolved governance; Kitui County government structure created.

2013: First county elections held; Kitui County government established under devolved governance.

2015: Coal mining debate intensifies with proposals and environmental impact assessments.

2017: Standard Gauge Railway opens, passing through Kitui and enhancing transport connectivity.

2020: COVID-19 pandemic affects Kitui economy and service delivery; adaptation underway.

2021-2026: Drought periods intensify climate change impacts; food security crises occur; humanitarian response mobilized.

2026: Kitui County continues managing devolved governance, climate change impacts, and development priorities including coal mining controversy.
