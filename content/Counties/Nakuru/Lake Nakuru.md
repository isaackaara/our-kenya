[[../../Conservation/Lake Nakuru|Lake Nakuru]] is a shallow alkaline lake in [[../Nakuru/Nakuru County|Nakuru County]], famous worldwide for its massive flamingo populations. The lake's water chemistry creates ideal conditions for blue-green algae (Spirulina), which attracts hundreds of thousands of flamingos, turning the lake pink. At its peak in the 1970s and 1980s, the flamingo population exceeded 2 million birds.

[[../../Conservation/Lake Nakuru|Lake Nakuru]] National Park, established in 1968, protects the lake and its shores. In the 1990s, the park expanded to include the Nakuru Rhino Sanctuary, housing both black and white rhinoceroses as part of Kenya's effort to save these endangered species from poaching. The sanctuary has been moderately successful, though rhinos remain vulnerable to organized poaching networks.

The lake's ecology is fragile and sensitive to water levels, pollution, and climate variability. In the 2000s and 2010s, rising water levels caused significant damage to park infrastructure and affected the flamingo population. The lake supports both tourism and subsistence fishing, though fishing is restricted in the national park. Local communities depend on [[../../Conservation/Lake Nakuru|Lake Nakuru]] for water, though they have limited access to protected areas.
