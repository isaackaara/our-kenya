# Hell's Gate National Park

[[../../Conservation/Hell's Gate National Park|Hell's Gate National Park]] is located between Lakes Naivasha and Nakuru in the central Rift Valley, covering approximately 68 square kilometers of spectacular volcanic landscape. The park's name derives from the narrow gorge and dramatic cliffs that form the Olkaria Gorge, through which the Olkaria River flows. Volcanic steam vents emerge from the ground, creating geothermal features that give the park its distinctive character.

Hell's Gate is unique among Kenya's national parks in allowing visitors to walk and cycle within park boundaries rather than requiring vehicles. The gorge walk is the most popular attraction, leading visitors through towering red cliffs and past boiling springs. Birdwatchers consider Hell's Gate exceptional, with over 400 bird species recorded. The park supports populations of zebra, wildebeest, antelope, and other wildlife, though large predators are limited.

The Olkaria Geothermal Station, located within the park, generates significant electricity for Kenya's national grid. The geothermal development has created tension between conservation and energy production, with concerns about environmental disruption and wildlife disturbance. Tourism and geothermal production both generate revenue, but they also place demands on the park's fragile ecosystem. Local [[../../Maasai/Maasai|Maasai]] communities have traditional claims to Hell's Gate land and resources.
