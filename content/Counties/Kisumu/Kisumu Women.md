# Women in Kisumu's Economy: Market Traders and Fish Processors

Women play crucial economic roles in Kisumu, particularly as market traders, fish processors, and informal service providers. Women's economic activities sustain millions of livelihoods and are essential to household survival.

## Market Traders

Women dominate Kisumu's informal retail markets, selling vegetables, fruits, grains, household goods, and other items. Market trading provides income for women and employment for family members assisting in trade activities.

## Fish Processing and Trade

Women are involved extensively in fish processing, drying, smoking, and trading. Fish processing is labor-intensive and provides income for thousands of women. Processing facilities employ women workers preparing fish for local consumption or export.

## Fish Vendors

Women are prominent as fish vendors in markets and streets, selling fresh or processed fish to consumers. Fish vending is accessible to women with limited capital and provides reliable income.

## Agricultural Activities

Women engage in agriculture, cultivating vegetables, grains, and other crops for household consumption and market sale. Agricultural work is labor-intensive and often unpaid within households.

## Small Enterprises

Women operate small enterprises including food vending, clothing sales, hairdressing, beauty services, and other informal businesses. Small enterprise income is crucial for household survival.

## Household Food Security

Women's market activities and food processing contribute substantially to household food security. The income from women's activities often determines whether families can afford adequate nutrition.

## Income Control

While women generate substantial income, cultural norms regarding male authority mean men often control household money. Women's earnings contribute to household welfare but women may have limited control over income.

## Labor Conditions

Women in informal economic activities typically work long hours under difficult conditions, without benefits, job security, or legal protections. Earnings are often modest despite substantial work.

## Vulnerability

Economic dependence on informal activities creates vulnerability to economic shocks, price fluctuations, and market competition. Women's livelihoods remain precarious.

## Leadership and Organization

Women's trader associations and cooperatives provide mutual support, savings programs, and collective bargaining power. These organizations increase women's economic influence and reduce vulnerability.
