The fishing industry is the largest economic sector in Kisumu and surrounding areas, employing hundreds of thousands directly and indirectly. [[Lake Victoria Kenya|Lake Victoria]]'s fish stocks support livelihoods and provide protein for millions.

## Fish Landing Sites

Major fish landing sites in Kisumu include Dunga Beach and Hippo Point, where fishers bring their daily catch. These landing sites are bustling markets where fish are purchased by traders, processors, and consumers.

## Artisanal Fishing

Most fishing is conducted by artisanal fishers using small boats (dhows, kayaks, motorized boats) and traditional and modern fishing equipment. Artisanal fishers typically operate within a few kilometers of shore, targeting tilapia and other species.

## Commercial Fishing

Commercial fishing operations use larger vessels and more sophisticated equipment. Commercial fishing vessels may operate throughout the lake and carry larger catches.

## Nile Perch Processing

The Nile perch, while an ecological disaster for endemic fish species, created a major export industry. Nile perch processing facilities in Kisumu process fish for export to international markets, particularly Europe. Processing provides significant employment.

## Fish Exports

Fish exports, particularly Nile perch fillets, are a major source of foreign exchange for Kenya. The fishing industry contributes substantially to Kenya's international trade balance.

## Small-Scale Traders

Thousands of small-scale traders buy fish from fishers and resell in local markets or to larger traders. This trade provides livelihoods for traders and their families.

## Fish Consumers

Fish is an essential protein source for millions of people in the region. Fish affordability and availability are important to food security and nutrition.

## Fishing Community

The fishing community has distinct characteristics, including specialized knowledge, equipment, and social organizations. Fishers form associations for mutual support and advocacy.

## Environmental Challenges

Overfishing, the Nile perch predation, water hyacinth, pollution, and changing water levels threaten fish stocks. Sustainable fishing remains an ongoing concern.
