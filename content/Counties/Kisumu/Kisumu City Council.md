Kisumu City County is one of Kenya's 47 counties under the 2010 devolved governance system. The county government is responsible for local service delivery, development, and governance.

## Governance Structure

The Kisumu City County Government consists of an elected governor, a deputy governor, and a county assembly of elected representatives. The governor serves as chief executive of the county government.

## Governors

Several governors have served Kisumu since devolution in 2013. Governors have varied in their governance effectiveness and focus areas, with attention to port development, education, healthcare, and informal settlement upgrading.

## Service Delivery Challenges

The county government faces challenges including limited revenue, inadequate infrastructure, gaps in healthcare and education provision, and high informal settlement populations with minimal access to basic services.

## Economic Development

County government initiatives focus on economic development, fishing industry support, port revitalization, and industrial development. However, limited resources constrain development efforts.

## Urban Planning and Infrastructure

The county government is responsible for urban planning, infrastructure development, and service delivery. Challenges include informal settlement management, inadequate water supply, poor waste management, and deteriorating infrastructure.

## Healthcare and Education

The county government provides primary healthcare through health facilities and educational services through county schools. Both sectors face resource constraints and quality challenges.

## Development Planning

The county government develops development plans and budgets. However, implementation is often constrained by limited resources and administrative capacity.

## Community Engagement

The county government increasingly engages communities in governance through consultative processes, though participation quality varies.

## Challenges and Opportunities

[[../Kisumu/Kisumu County|Kisumu County]] government faces ongoing challenges in service delivery and development. Opportunities include regional trade integration, fishing industry development, and tourism expansion.
