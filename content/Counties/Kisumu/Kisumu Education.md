# Kisumu's Education Sector: University and Schools

Kisumu is home to Maseno University and numerous primary and secondary schools. Education is an important sector for employment and human capital development.

## Maseno University

Maseno University, a public university established in 1991, is located in Kisumu and serves students from Nyanza Region and beyond. The university offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various fields including education, agriculture, engineering, and social sciences.

## Primary and Secondary Schools

Kisumu has numerous primary and secondary schools serving local and regional students. School quality varies, with elite schools providing excellent education and others struggling with inadequate resources.

## Teacher Training Colleges

Teacher training colleges in Kisumu provide initial teacher training for primary and secondary teachers. These colleges contribute to human resource development for the education sector.

## Technical and Vocational Training

Technical and vocational institutions provide skills training in trades, technology, and business, preparing workers for employment.

## Education Challenges

Education in Kisumu faces challenges including limited resources in public schools, inadequate teacher training and pay, high student-to-teacher ratios, limited learning materials, and inadequate infrastructure.

## Quality Disparities

Significant disparities exist between elite schools serving wealthy families and schools serving low-income communities. Access to quality education varies substantially by socioeconomic status.

## Educational Outcomes

Educational performance in Kisumu lags behind national averages in some areas, though some schools perform well in national examinations.

## School Infrastructure

School infrastructure in many Kisumu institutions is inadequate, with some schools lacking adequate classrooms, laboratories, libraries, and basic facilities.

## Dropout Rates

High dropout rates, particularly among boys and in low-income areas, remain a challenge. Economic constraints and other factors drive student dropout.
