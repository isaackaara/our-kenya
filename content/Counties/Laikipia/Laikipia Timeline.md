This timeline documents major events and developments in Laikipia County's history through 2026.

## Pre-Colonial Period
- Traditional pastoral settlement and land use
- [[Maasai|Maasai]], [[Samburu|Samburu]], Pokot, and Turkana pastoral traditions
- Cattle herding economies
- Social organization through age-set systems

## Colonial Period (1890-1963)
- 1890s: European colonial establishment in Laikipia region
- 1904: European settler farms established
- 1920s: Colonial land allocation finalized
- 1950s: Wildlife conservation efforts begin
- 1952-1960: [[Mau Mau Uprising|Mau Mau]] uprising, limited Laikipia participation
- 1960: State of Emergency lifted

## Post-Independence Period (1964-1989)
- 1964: Kenya independence
- 1965-1975: Limited land redistribution in Laikipia
- 1970s: Conservancy concept development
- 1980s: Wildlife conservation expansion

## Modern Development (1990-2012)
- 1990s: Conservancy model expansion
- 2000s: Conservation success and pastoral tension growth
- 2005: Constitutional reform discussions
- 2010: New constitution with devolution provisions

## Devolution Era (2013-Present)
- 2013: Laikipia County government established
- 2014: County development initiatives launched
- 2015: Conservation and development projects
- 2017-2018: Wildlife invasions and escalated conflict
- 2020: COVID-19 pandemic effects
- 2022: Post-conflict reconstruction
- 2023: Climate change impacts evident
- 2024: Infrastructure and development initiatives
- 2025: Conservation and pastoralist tensions
- 2026: Ongoing development and conflict management

## Conservation Development
- 1970s-1980s: Private conservancy establishment
- 1990s: Community conservancy model emergence
- 2000s: Conservancy network expansion
- 2010s: Conservation success achievements
- 2020s: Climate adaptation and sustainability focus

## Infrastructure Development
- 1960s-1970s: Road network establishment
- 1980s-1990s: Road maintenance and improvement
- 2000s: Electricity grid expansion
- 2010s: Water development emphasis
- 2020s: Digital infrastructure expansion

## Land and Conflict Trends
- 1960s-1990s: Settler farm consolidation
- 1990s-2000s: Early conservancy establishment
- 2000s-2010s: Conservation expansion and pastoral tension
- 2017-2018: Peak land conflict and invasions
- 2020s: Conflict management and land negotiation attempts
