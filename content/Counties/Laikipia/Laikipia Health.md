# Laikipia Health

Healthcare in Laikipia County comprises public facilities, private clinics, and traditional practitioners. The county hospital provides tertiary care. Health centers and dispensaries offer primary healthcare. Private clinics supplement public services in towns. Healthcare coverage has expanded though access and quality challenges persist. Pastoral communities face particular healthcare access challenges. Maternal and child health services remain important priorities.

## Public Health System

County government operates public facilities. These facilities typically charge low fees. However, medication shortages and staffing limitations affect quality. Services reach most communities though quality varies.

## Healthcare Workforce

Doctors, nurses, and health workers staff facilities. Urban facilities have better staffing than rural areas. Brain drain to urban centers affects rural quality. Limited training facilities constrain workforce growth.

## Maternal and Child Health

Maternal and child health services have improved. Facility delivery rates have increased. Immunization programs reach most children. However, healthcare quality in remote areas remains limited.

## Infectious Diseases

Malaria is less common at high altitude. Communicable disease surveillance occurs. HIV/AIDS services including testing are available. Disease prevention focuses on immunization and health education.

## Nutrition and Food Security

Pastoral nutrition depends on livestock. Drought periods create food insecurity. Nutrition monitoring occurs in health facilities.

## Private Healthcare

Private clinics and hospitals operate in towns. Better infrastructure characterizes private facilities. Costs limit private care access. Private pharmaceutical shops sell medications.

## Health Financing

User fees at public facilities remain barriers. National health insurance (NHIF) covers employed people. Out-of-pocket spending on health remains high.
