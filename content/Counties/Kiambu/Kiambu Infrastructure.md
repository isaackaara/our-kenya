# Kiambu Infrastructure

Kiambu's road infrastructure has been progressively upgraded, most notably through the Thika Superhighway (officially the Thika Road/A2), completed in 2012 as Kenya's first modern expressway. The superhighway connects Nairobi to Thika and surrounding areas, dramatically reducing travel times and transforming commuting patterns. The Northern Bypass around Nairobi has also eased traffic congestion. Secondary roads connecting to Nairobi and between towns in Kiambu vary in condition, with many rural roads in poor condition requiring maintenance investment.

Water infrastructure is critical for Kiambu's growing urban and suburban population. The county depends on water supplied from various sources, with growing demand from increasing population and expanding commercial agriculture. Water supply shortages are reported periodically, particularly during dry seasons. Sewerage systems in urban areas are often inadequate, with many residential developments relying on pit latrines or septic tanks that create sanitation risks. Electricity supply has expanded but remains unreliable in some areas, constraining industrial development and household comfort.

Kiambu's infrastructure has not kept pace with rapid urbanization, creating bottlenecks in water, sewerage, and transportation. The county government and national government have invested in infrastructure expansion, but investment has been insufficient relative to demand. Informal settlements lack basic infrastructure, creating health and environmental hazards. Infrastructure investments generate economic activity but also risk environmental degradation if not managed sustainably. Infrastructure planning and coordination between Kiambu and Nairobi remain challenging despite the integrated urban system.
