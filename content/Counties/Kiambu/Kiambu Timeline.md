1895-1963 - Colonial period. Kiambu is core area of the White Highlands. European settler farms displace [[../../Kikuyu/Kikuyu|Kikuyu]] farmers and limit Kikuyu access to their ancestral land.

1910s-1940s - [[../../Kikuyu/Kikuyu|Kikuyu]] political and cultural organizations emerge in response to colonial dispossession and racial hierarchy.

1952-1960 - Mau Mau uprising. Kiambu is epicenter of [[../../Kikuyu/Kikuyu|Kikuyu]] anti-colonial insurgency. Thousands killed or detained. British counter-insurgency involves violence and detention camps.

1963 - Kenya independence. White farms subdivided and distributed to African buyers, primarily wealthy [[../../Kikuyu/Kikuyu|Kikuyu]].

1963-1978 - [[../../Kikuyu/Jomo Kenyatta|Jomo Kenyatta]] presidency. Gatundu becomes political center of power. Kenyatta's [[../../Kikuyu/Kikuyu|Kikuyu]] background benefits Kiambu with political patronage and investment.

1978-2002 - Moi presidency. Kiambu's political influence declines as power shifts to Moi's Kalenjin base. However, Kenyatta family retains significant wealth and influence.

1980s-2000s - Rapid urbanization begins, particularly in areas near Nairobi. Real estate development accelerates in Ruiru and Kiambu suburbs.

2002-2007 - Post-Moi transition. Kibaki presidency (Kikuyu). Kiambu benefits from renewed Kikuyu political prominence.

2007-2008 - Post-election violence. Kiambu is relatively safe but receives Kikuyu IDPs fleeing violence in other counties.

2009-2012 - Thika Superhighway construction transforms transport and accelerates suburban development.

2010 - Constitutional reform creates county government structure. Rift Valley Province abolished.

2012 - First county elections. Kiambu becomes independent county with elected governor.

2012-2022 - County government development: education, health, water, and road improvements. Real estate boom continues.

2020 - COVID-19 pandemic disrupts economy and services.

2022 - Presidential and county elections. Political competition intensifies.

2026 - Kiambu remains Kenya's Kikuyu heartland and a critical political county. Real estate development continues, though sustainability concerns grow.
