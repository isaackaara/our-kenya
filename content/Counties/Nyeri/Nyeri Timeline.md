# Nyeri Timeline

1900s: British colonial administration establishes Nyeri District as administrative center.
1905-1920s: Christian missionaries establish schools and churches.
1950-1960: Mau Mau uprising; Nyeri is epicenter of anti-colonial resistance. Dedan Kimathi leads forest fighters. British counterinsurgency operations.
1957: Dedan Kimathi executed. Mau Mau struggle intensifies.
1963: Kenya achieves independence. Nyeri becomes a district in independent Kenya.
1941: Robert Baden-Powell dies in Nyeri and is buried at St Peter's Church.
1952: Princess Elizabeth learns she is Queen while staying at Treetops Lodge.
1970s-1980s: Agricultural cooperatives expand. Coffee and tea marketing organizations develop.
1990s-2000s: Educational expansion. Higher education access increases.
2004: Wangari Maathai wins Nobel Peace Prize. Her environmental legacy elevated internationally.
2013: Devolution creates Nyeri County government. County-level governance begins.
2019: Census documents approximately 760,000 population.
2020-2021: COVID-19 pandemic affects agriculture and healthcare.
2022-2026: County development initiatives. Climate adaptation emphasis. Agricultural modernization efforts.
