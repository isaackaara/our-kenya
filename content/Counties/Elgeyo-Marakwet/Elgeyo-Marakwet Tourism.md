Tourism represents an increasingly important economic sector in Elgeyo-Marakwet County, centred on the dramatic Kerio Valley landscape, Iten's running tourism prominence, and the unique Marakwet irrigation heritage. The Kerio Valley's scenic beauty, with dramatic escarpments and river valley views, attracts tourists seeking natural landscape experiences. Walking trails, viewpoints, and scenic drives provide tourism opportunities for visitors.

Running tourism dominates contemporary tourism in the county, with international athletes traveling to train in Iten and participating in training camps and running clinics. Athletes seeking altitude training and elite coaching converge on Iten throughout the year. Running tourism supports hotels, restaurants, transportation, and coaching services. Tourism infrastructure including accommodation facilities has expanded substantially to serve visiting athletes. Cultural tourism focused on the Marakwet irrigation system and Elgeyo-Marakwet heritage has potential but remains underdeveloped. Local communities offer cultural experiences and heritage demonstrations to visiting tourists. Valley eco-tourism showcasing wildlife, vegetation, and geological features attracts nature-oriented visitors. Tourism development has created employment opportunities and economic benefits for county residents. However, tourism dependence creates vulnerability to fluctuations in international travel and athlete recruitment patterns. Tourism infrastructure development has required county government and private sector investment. Community participation in tourism and benefit distribution remain important considerations for sustainable development.
