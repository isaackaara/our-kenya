Elgeyo-Marakwet's real estate market has experienced significant growth, driven by economic prosperity from running tourism, agricultural production, population increase, and appeal as a residential and investment destination. Iten and surrounding towns have experienced property value appreciation, attracting both local residents and external investors. Residential properties including houses and apartments are owned by local families, returning diaspora, and external investors.

Commercial properties including shops, offices, restaurants, and accommodation facilities concentrate in Iten's central business district, where demand remains strong from traders and service providers. Running-related commercial properties including training camps, coaching facilities, and athlete accommodation represent a growing real estate segment. Agricultural land including smallholder plots represents significant real estate value, though some transactions are complicated by tenure disputes. The real estate market has attracted external investors including land developers, builders, and foreign nationals seeking vacation homes or investment. Rising land values have created opportunities for landholders and concerns about affordability and displacement. Property development has accelerated, with new residential estates and commercial complexes under construction. The real estate market includes informal housing settlements where low-income residents occupy properties with insecure tenure. Land speculation and conversion of agricultural land to residential use represent ongoing trends. Valley properties command premium prices for scenic value, though topography limits development.
