Healthcare in Vihiga County is provided through government health facilities, private clinics, and traditional practitioners operating with severe resource constraints. Health services face challenges from high population density, limited resources, and disease burden. Government facilities provide basic services though capacity remains constrained. Health outcomes reflect developmental constraints and limited service capacity.

## Government Health Facilities

Primary health centres and dispensaries distributed throughout serve local populations. Government health facilities provide basic outpatient and maternal health services. Limited inpatient capacity restricts specialized care. Referral arrangements direct complex cases to regional hospitals. Staff constraints limit service hours and availability.

## Private Healthcare

Private clinics and pharmacies provide alternative healthcare access. Private services serve populations able to afford fees. Quality and oversight of private practitioners varies. Private healthcare expands in urban areas and among higher-income populations.

## Health Challenges

Communicable diseases including malaria and respiratory infections remain significant burdens. Maternal and child health indicators reflect developmental constraints. Water-borne diseases result from inadequate water and sanitation infrastructure. Nutrition and food security issues affect health outcomes. Non-communicable diseases including hypertension are increasing.

## Service Delivery Constraints

Staff limitations restrict facility opening hours and service availability. Medication shortages create service gaps. Facility infrastructure limitations affect service quality. Limited equipment constrains diagnostic capability. Maintenance funding is inadequate for facility preservation.

## Water and Sanitation

Water supply is inadequate for household needs. Many populations rely on unprotected water sources. Sanitation facilities are limited. Fecal matter contamination creates disease risks. Water quality monitoring is inadequate. These factors contribute to disease burden.

## Health Promotion

Community health education focuses on disease prevention. Vaccination programmes target key population groups. Maternal health education attempts to improve birth outcomes. Nutrition education addresses undernutrition. These efforts are often underfunded and reach limited populations.
