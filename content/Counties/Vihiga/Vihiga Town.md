Vihiga Town is the small county headquarters of Vihiga County, serving as the administrative and commercial centre for the tiny county. With population estimated at approximately 10,000-15,000, Vihiga Town is considerably smaller than headquarters of other Kenyan counties. The town provides administrative functions and limited commercial services to surrounding rural areas. Urban growth has been modest reflecting the county's small size and limited economic base.

## Urban Functions

Vihiga Town serves as the administrative centre where county government offices operate. Commercial activity is limited, with basic retail and service provision. The town provides some professional services including legal, medical, and banking functions. Market activities connect surrounding rural areas to regional trade networks.

## Urban Services

Water supply serves the town though reliability varies seasonally. Electricity infrastructure reaches the town with varying supply reliability. Healthcare facilities including a small health centre serve town residents. Educational institutions including secondary schools serve students from surrounding areas.

## Connectivity

The town is connected to Kisumu and other regional centres by roads. Public transport is available though limited in frequency. Communications infrastructure including mobile networks provides connectivity. Internet access is available though limited bandwidth in some areas.

## Economic Activity

Commercial activity is limited relative to larger county towns. Petty trading and informal services provide employment. Government employment provides stable income to civil servants. Agricultural product marketing provides commercial activity linking farmers to consumers.

## Development Challenges

Infrastructure limitations constrain development potential. Water supply remains unreliable seasonally. Power supply is inadequate for significant industrial development. Limited commercial activity constrains economic growth. Housing and service expansion requires modest investment.

## Community Life

Town residents maintain close connections to surrounding rural areas. Religious institutions provide important social services and gatherings. Community organizations and self-help groups organize local development. Town residents and surrounding communities interact continuously through markets and services.
