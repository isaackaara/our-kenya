Busia County is in western Kenya on the Uganda border, adjacent to Siaya and Kakamega counties. The county has a population of approximately 900,000 people. It is the most ethnically diverse county in western Kenya, with Luhya (Samia, Teso, Marachi sub-groups), Luo, and Iteso communities. The Busia border crossing with Uganda is one of Kenya's busiest international crossings. Busia town is the county headquarters and main commercial hub. The county is primarily agricultural and pastoral, with sugarcane farming as a major commercial crop. The Yala Swamp (one of Kenya's largest wetlands) is located in Busia County. Lake Victoria shoreline in the south provides fishing opportunities and connections to Siaya County.

## Overview
The county landscape transitions from highland agricultural zones to lowland pastoral areas. Elevation ranges from approximately 1,100 meters in northern areas to lake level (1,134 meters) in the south. The county experiences a tropical highland climate in upper areas and hot lowland climate in pastoral zones. Rainfall supports both agricultural and pastoral production, with periodic droughts creating challenges. The county economy is anchored in agriculture (sugarcane, maize), fishing, and cross-border trade. Political administration involves devolved county government with connection to national political structures. The county's border location and ethnic diversity create complex social and political dynamics.

## Key Sites and Features
- Busia Town (county headquarters and border crossing)
- Yala Swamp (major wetland, conservation and livelihood significance)
- Lake Victoria shoreline (fishing, trading, ecological importance)
- Sugarcane growing zones (commercial agriculture)
- Border trading centers (informal and formal commerce)
