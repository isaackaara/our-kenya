Real estate and property transactions have increased significantly in Busia County, particularly in Busia town. Agricultural land remains the dominant property type, with holdings ranging from small plots to larger farms. Land titles obtained from government provide legal ownership documentation. Land prices have appreciated in recent years. Urban property development has expanded in Busia town with residential buildings and commercial structures. Land subdivision is increasingly common as larger farms are divided for resale. Commercial property in Busia central business district commands premium prices. Residential property in Busia suburbs provides housing for government employees, traders, and professionals. Property rental generates income for landlords. Informal property transactions without formal titles remain common in some areas.

## Property Markets and Transactions
Private land sales between individuals occur both formally and informally. Real estate agents in Busia facilitate property transactions. Banks provide lending against property for purchase. Title registration through land registry ensures ownership documentation. Land transfer taxes and registration fees are collected during transactions. Commercial property development attracts investment from traders and entrepreneurs. Urban residential development provides housing for town workers. Agricultural land investment has been attractive to external investors. Tourism-related property development is emerging. Land speculation increases as investors expect price appreciation. Cooperative land purchases enable group acquisition. Women's property ownership is increasing but lags male ownership rates.

## Land Access and Ownership Challenges
Young farmers face difficulties accessing sufficient land for farming. Land scarcity from fragmentation limits expansion opportunities. Informal land claims without formal title create disputes. Squatter communities on undocumented land face eviction risks. Landless populations face significant livelihood challenges. Women's property rights remain constrained by inheritance patterns. Environmental protection requirements restrict some land use. Land pricing is increasingly unaffordable for low-income populations. Border location affects land values and investment patterns. Climate change makes agricultural land investment uncertain. Government land acquisitions for infrastructure occasionally displace residents. Corruption in land allocation remains a challenge.
