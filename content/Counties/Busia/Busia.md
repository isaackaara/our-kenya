# Busia

Busia is a county in western Kenya bordering Uganda, with the town of Busia serving as the commercial and administrative center. The county is home to approximately 750,000 people, predominantly Luhya and Luo communities.

## Geography and Location

Busia lies on the Kenya-Uganda border, with the Nzoia River forming a natural boundary. The county experiences a humid tropical climate with two rainy seasons, suitable for agriculture.

## History

### Pre-Colonial
Luhya and Luo communities established agricultural and pastoral settlements in the region, with active trade networks connecting to Uganda and coastal Swahili merchants via Lake Victoria.

### Colonial Period
Busia emerged as a railway junction and border trading post following the Uganda Railway's construction. The town developed as a commercial center connecting Uganda and Kenya.

### Post-Independence
Busia became a district headquarters within Kisumu District, later gaining county status under 2010 devolution. Political prominence through various national figures.

## Economy

- Sugarcane farming and sugar production
- Maize and other grain cultivation
- Livestock herding
- Border trade and commerce
- Small-scale fishing in western areas

## Border Dynamics

Busia's position as Kenya's primary border crossing with Uganda makes it strategically important for regional trade and has shaped its commercial development.
