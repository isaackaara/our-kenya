The 2010 Kenya Constitution established a devolved system transferring significant authority from national government to 47 county governments. Samburu County Government, created in 2013 following the general election, exercises powers over health, education, agriculture, and local infrastructure.

County government structure includes a governor (elected chief executive), county assembly (legislators), county cabinet (executives appointed by governor), and county administration (permanent civil service). This structure mirrors national government organisation but operates at county level.

Revenue sources for county operations include allocation from the national exchequer (from general taxation), local revenue collection through business permits and property taxes, and donor funding for specific projects. Limited local revenue means Samburu County depends heavily on national government transfers.

County development planning involves strategic planning processes, budgeting, and periodic reviews. The Samburu County Integrated Development Plan (CIDP) guides investment priorities. Planning processes involve stakeholder consultation and community participation, though effectiveness varies.

Devolution has expanded service provision and local accountability but also created coordination challenges between county and national governments. Wildlife conservation, pastoral development, and tourism regulation involve multiple government levels.

Inter-county cooperation occurs through councils of governors and sector-specific working groups addressing shared challenges including pastoral production and wildlife management.

Community participation mechanisms including public participation in budgeting and planning, citizen petitions, and accountability mechanisms theoretically increase responsiveness. Implementation effectiveness remains variable, with pastoral communities sometimes having limited voice in governance processes.

Tourism development creates complex coordination requirements between county government, national authorities, private sector operators, and wildlife conservation agencies.
