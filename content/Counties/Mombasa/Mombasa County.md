# Mombasa County

Mombasa County encompasses Kenya's primary coastal region and Indian Ocean port city, serving as the nation's gateway to maritime trade and a major urban economic center.

## Geography

Mombasa lies on the Kenyan coast, centered on the historic island of Mombasa connected to the mainland by bridges. The county encompasses coastal and near-coastal areas with a humid tropical climate.

## History

### Swahili Sultanate Era
Mombasa developed as a major Swahili city-state, a center of Islamic civilization and Indian Ocean trade from at least the 13th century. Fort Jesus (1593) marked Portuguese control.

### Colonial Period
British control transformed Mombasa into East Africa's primary port and railway terminus. The Uganda Railway's completion in 1901 cemented Mombasa's role as colonial Kenya's economic lifeline.

### Post-Independence
Mombasa remains Kenya's primary port and a major commercial and tourist destination.

## Economy

- Port operations and maritime trade
- Tourism (beaches, cultural sites, diving)
- Manufacturing and food processing
- Commerce and services
