Mombasa is home to numerous primary and secondary schools and higher education institutions, contributing significantly to the city's economy and serving the educational needs of the coastal region.

## Primary and Secondary Schools

Mombasa hosts numerous primary and secondary schools, including both public and private institutions. The schools serve local students and, in some cases, students from surrounding regions and other countries. School quality varies, with elite private schools providing excellent education and under-resourced public schools struggling with overcrowding and insufficient materials.

## Technical University of Mombasa

The Technical University of Mombasa (TUM), established as a technical institution, provides technical and vocational education in engineering, ICT, business, and other fields. TUM serves students from Mombasa and the surrounding region, providing skills training for the job market.

## Kenyatta University Mombasa Campus

Kenyatta University, one of Kenya's major universities, has a campus in Mombasa offering various undergraduate and postgraduate programs. The campus serves distance learning students and full-time students seeking university education in the coastal region.

## Other Higher Education Institutions

Other universities and colleges with presence in Mombasa include Mount Kenya University, Amity University, and smaller institutions offering diploma and certificate programs.

## Coast General Hospital Training

The Coast General Teaching and Referral Hospital serves as a teaching facility for healthcare training programs, preparing nurses, clinical officers, and other health professionals.

## Islamic Education

Mombasa's mosques and Islamic institutions provide Islamic education to children and adults. Quranic schools teach Islamic teachings and Arabic language, particularly to Muslim youth.

## Technical Skills Training

Vocational training centers in Mombasa provide skills training in trades, technology, and business, preparing workers for employment in the hospitality, tourism, maritime, and other sectors.

## Educational Challenges

Mombasa's education sector faces challenges including limited resources in public schools, inadequate infrastructure, teacher shortages, and high dropout rates in low-income communities. Access to quality education varies significantly by socioeconomic status.

## Economic Importance

Education is an important employer in Mombasa, with thousands working as teachers, administrators, support staff, and in education-related services. The education sector contributes substantially to the local economy.
