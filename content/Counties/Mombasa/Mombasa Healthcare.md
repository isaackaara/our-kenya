Mombasa's healthcare system includes public and private facilities, with the Coast General Teaching and Referral Hospital serving as the largest public hospital. The healthcare sector faces challenges including resource constraints, staffing shortages, and disease burden.

## Coast General Teaching and Referral Hospital

Coast General Hospital is Mombasa's primary public healthcare facility. The hospital serves as the main teaching hospital for health professional training and as the referral center for complex cases from surrounding areas. Despite its importance, the hospital faces chronic resource constraints, overcrowding, and infrastructure challenges.

## Private Healthcare Facilities

Mombasa has several private hospitals and clinics serving those who can afford private care. These facilities offer higher quality and shorter wait times than public facilities but at substantially higher cost.

## Healthcare Challenges

Mombasa's healthcare system faces numerous challenges including inadequate funding, insufficient medical staff, shortages of essential medicines and supplies, aged equipment, and inadequate infrastructure. These challenges translate into poor service quality and delayed treatment.

## Disease Burden

Mombasa faces a substantial disease burden including infectious diseases (malaria, typhoid, cholera, respiratory infections), waterborne diseases linked to inadequate water supply and sanitation, HIV and AIDS (Mombasa has significant HIV prevalence), and chronic diseases (diabetes, hypertension).

## Maternal and Child Health

Maternal and child health services are important in Mombasa, with government and NGO-supported programs targeting reduction of maternal mortality and child malnutrition. However, healthcare access remains limited in lower-income areas.

## Communicable Disease Control

Mombasa hosts centers for disease surveillance and control, working to monitor and respond to disease outbreaks. However, limited resources constrain disease control effectiveness.

## Health Professional Training

Coast General Hospital serves as a training facility for nurses, clinical officers, and other health professionals, contributing to health workforce development in the coastal region.

## Informal Settlement Health

Health provision in informal settlements is particularly limited. Informal clinics and health workers provide basic services, but resources are scarce and quality is variable. Disease burden in informal settlements remains high.

## Mental Health Services

Mental health services in Mombasa are very limited, with inadequate facilities and trained mental health professionals. Mental health is often neglected despite significant disease burden.
