While the port dominates Mombasa's economic profile, the city's economy encompasses tourism, retail, manufacturing, fishing, and service sectors that collectively employ tens of thousands and contribute substantially to the regional and national economy.

## Port-Related Industries

The port generates direct employment through cargo handling, shipping agency services, warehousing, and cargo brokerage. Port-dependent industries include petroleum distribution, wholesale trade, and import-export commerce. The Kenya Ports Authority and related industries employ several thousand workers.

## Tourism and Hospitality

Tourism is Mombasa's second-largest economic sector. Hotels, restaurants, tour operators, craft producers, and entertainment venues collectively employ thousands. The sector generates foreign exchange and contributes to local government revenue.

## Retail and Commerce

Mombasa is a major retail center serving the Coast Region and neighboring areas. Shopping centers, supermarkets, wholesale markets, and street traders collectively handle enormous volumes of commerce. The retail sector employs thousands and serves as an economic engine for many families.

## Fishing and Marine Products

Fishing remains important in Mombasa's economy, both as a subsistence activity and as a commercial industry. Artisanal fishers work the waters off the coast, while commercial fishing vessels operate from the port. Fish processing and export generate income and employment.

## Small Manufacturing

Mombasa hosts small and medium manufacturing enterprises, particularly in food processing, textiles, plastics, and metal fabrication. The Mombasa Industrial Area accommodates several hundred small to medium enterprises.

## Agricultural Trade

Mombasa serves as a distribution center for agricultural products from surrounding regions. Fruits, vegetables, and other produce pass through Mombasa's markets destined for export or domestic consumption.

## Real Estate and Construction

Real estate development and construction have accelerated in Mombasa, driven by tourism development, port expansion, and migration to the city. Construction and real estate services generate substantial economic activity.

## Government Employment

Mombasa hosts a substantial government sector, including county administration, national government offices, and security forces. Government employment provides salaries and stimulates spending in the local economy.

## Economic Challenges

Mombasa's economy faces challenges including port competition from Tanzania and Ethiopia, tourism volatility, limited industrial diversification, and dependence on global commodity prices and trade patterns. Economic growth in Mombasa lags Nairobi and other highland regions.
