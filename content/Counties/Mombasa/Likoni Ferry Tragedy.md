The Likoni ferry crossing, connecting Mombasa Island to the mainland south of the city, has been the site of multiple tragedies, including capsizing incidents and drownings that have claimed hundreds of lives over decades.

## Historic Ferry Service

The ferry service was the primary connection between the island and the mainland before road bridges were constructed. The ferry transported vehicles, pedestrians, and goods across the water channel separating the island from the southern mainland.

## Capsizing Incidents

Multiple ferry vessels have capsized in the Likoni Channel, resulting in significant loss of life. These incidents have been attributed to overcrowding, inadequate maintenance, poor vessel design, adverse weather conditions, and navigational errors.

## 2022 Tragedies

In May 2022, a ferry capsized in the Likoni Channel, resulting in the deaths of multiple passengers. The incident reignited concerns about ferry safety, maintenance standards, and passenger safety regulations. Investigations attributed the incident to overcrowding and vessel instability.

## Safety Concerns

The ferry service has faced persistent safety concerns including:
- Overcrowding beyond designed capacity
- Inadequate maintenance of aging vessels
- Poor safety training for passengers and crew
- Lack of adequate life-saving equipment
- Navigational challenges in the channel

## Regulatory Response

Investigations of ferry tragedies have led to temporary suspensions of service, vessel inspections, and regulatory reforms. However, implementation of safety standards has been inconsistent.

## Passengers and Dependence

The ferry remains heavily used by thousands of residents who cross daily for work, commerce, and family reasons. For many, the ferry is the most affordable crossing option, despite safety concerns.

## Alternative Routes

Road bridges have been constructed to provide alternative crossings, reducing ferry dependence for vehicle traffic. However, the ferry remains important for pedestrians and continues to operate despite safety concerns.

## Socioeconomic Context

Ferry passengers are predominantly lower-income residents who cannot afford car ownership or toll roads. Safety concerns disproportionately affect vulnerable populations.
