Forest resources in Nyamira County have been depleted through clearing for agriculture and settlement, with limited forest cover remaining. Forest conservation is increasingly important for water supply, biodiversity, and climate regulation.

## Forest Status

Remaining forests include:
- Significant forest loss historically
- Limited forest cover today
- Degraded forest in some areas
- Community forest management efforts
- Private tree plantations
- Protected area remnants

## Water Tower Function

Forests provide:
- Watershed protection and recharge
- Stream and river flow regulation
- Water supply support
- Flood mitigation
- Dry season water availability
- Groundwater recharge

## Biodiversity

Forest ecosystems support:
- Flora species diversity
- Fauna and wildlife habitat
- Medicinal plant species
- Endemic species presence
- Ecological function
- Genetic resource preservation

## Resource Extraction

Forests provide:
- Firewood and charcoal
- Timber for construction
- Medicinal plants
- Honey and bee products
- Fruit and nut gathering
- Other forest products

## Conservation Challenges

Forest protection faces:
- Population pressure and clearing
- Illegal logging activities
- Charcoal production
- Agricultural expansion
- Settlement pressure
- Inadequate law enforcement

## Management Approaches

Conservation efforts include:
- Community forest associations
- Tree planting initiatives
- Protected area management
- Sustainable harvesting practices
- Forest user group organization
- Government forest department oversight

## Economic Value

Forests provide:
- Environmental service value
- Livelihood products
- Economic activity support
- Carbon sequestration
- Poverty reduction potential
- Climate resilience support

## Future Outlook

Forest conservation priorities:
- Reforestation programs
- Protected area expansion
- Sustainable management
- Community benefit sharing
- Law enforcement
- Climate change mitigation
