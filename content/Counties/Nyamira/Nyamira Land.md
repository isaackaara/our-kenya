Land tenure in Nyamira County reflects customary systems and modern property law. Land pressure from high population density creates tensions and disputes affecting agricultural productivity and economic development.

## Land Tenure Systems

Landholding includes:
- Individual freehold title
- Customary group tenure
- Community land
- Government land
- Title registration in survey areas

## Land Pressure

Land challenges involve:
- High population density relative to land area
- Farm subdivision and fragmentation
- Limited land for youth access
- Insufficient land for viable farming
- Inheritance disputes
- Landlessness among poor populations

## Property Rights

Property ownership involves:
- Registered title documentation
- Customary land rights
- Women's land access limitations
- Inheritance patterns and succession
- Land sale and lease transactions
- Dispute resolution mechanisms

## Agricultural Land Use

Land use patterns include:
- Tea cultivation
- Food crop production
- Residential settlement
- Forest and grazing areas
- Market centers
- Government facilities

## Land Disputes

Common disputes involve:
- Boundary conflicts between neighbors
- Inheritance disagreements
- Title registration disputes
- Illegal land sales
- Women's property rights
- Eviction of poor farmers

## Land Administration

Land governance includes:
- County land control board
- Land registration services
- Dispute resolution mechanisms
- Land use planning
- Environmental protection
- Settlement regularization

## Women and Land

Women's land rights involve:
- Limited ownership and inheritance
- Widowhood property dispossession
- Marriage land rights
- Divorce property settlements
- Legislative protections
- Practical enforcement challenges

## Conservation

Land conservation efforts include:
- Soil conservation measures
- Forest protection
- Water source preservation
- Environmental impact control
- Community conservation programs
