Education is valued in Nyamira County with networks of primary schools, secondary schools, and training institutions. County government has expanded educational services and infrastructure following devolution.

## Schools

Educational institutions include:
- Over 200 primary schools
- Numerous secondary schools
- Private educational facilities
- Vocational training centers
- Adult education programs

## Primary Education

Primary education characterized by:
- High enrollment rates
- Challenges with completion rates
- Teacher recruitment and deployment
- Infrastructure gaps in rural areas
- Curriculum implementation
- Food program support

## Secondary Education

Secondary education includes:
- Public and private schools
- National and county school categories
- Examination performance variation
- Infrastructure adequacy challenges
- Student boarding facilities
- Teacher qualifications

## Higher Education

Higher education options include:
- Universities in other counties
- Diploma and certificate programs
- Technical vocational training
- Distance learning opportunities
- Student financing challenges

## Literacy and Skills

Educational outcomes include:
- Relatively high literacy rates
- Skills training programs
- Youth development initiatives
- Adult literacy programs
- Language instruction
- Practical skills development

## County Education Services

County government provides:
- Primary school administration
- Teacher deployment support
- Infrastructure development
- Curriculum support
- Special needs education
- Quality assurance monitoring

## Challenges

Education sector faces:
- Infrastructure gaps
- Teacher shortage in rural areas
- Student retention challenges
- Quality of education concerns
- Limited specialist training
- Technology access limitations
