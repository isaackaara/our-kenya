Healthcare in Nyamira County is delivered through public and private facilities with county government managing primary and secondary services. Health outcomes reflect the county's economic development and infrastructure investment.

## Health Facilities

Healthcare infrastructure includes:
- County referral hospital
- Health centers in subcounties
- Dispensaries in ward locations
- Private clinics and hospitals
- Community health worker network

## Primary Healthcare

Primary services include:
- Preventive health programs
- Communicable disease surveillance
- Maternal and child health
- Family planning services
- Immunization programs
- Health education

## Secondary Care

Secondary services include:
- Hospital inpatient services
- Surgical procedures
- Specialized consultations
- Emergency services
- Maternity services

## Health Challenges

County faces health issues:
- High maternal mortality
- Waterborne diseases
- Malaria in lower areas
- Malnutrition in vulnerable groups
- HIV/AIDS prevalence
- Limited specialist services

## Staffing

Healthcare workforce includes:
- Doctors and medical officers
- Nurses and midwives
- Pharmacy staff
- Laboratory technicians
- Community health workers
- Staffing gaps in rural areas

## Maternal and Child Health

Particular focus on:
- Antenatal care programs
- Skilled birth attendance
- Postnatal care services
- Child immunization
- Nutrition support
- Family planning access

## County Health Services

County government manages:
- Service delivery coordination
- Infrastructure development
- Staff deployment
- Equipment procurement
- Community health programs

## Challenges and Gaps

Healthcare system faces:
- Limited equipment and supplies
- Medicines availability issues
- Staff capacity constraints
- Infrastructure gaps
- Quality variations
- Financial sustainability
