Youth in Nyamira County face significant challenges accessing employment and establishing livelihoods. Limited land availability and restricted urban opportunities drive youth migration to other regions in search of economic opportunities.

## Employment Challenges

Young people encounter:
- Limited formal employment
- Insufficient land for farming livelihoods
- Skill development constraints
- Low entrepreneurship support
- Rural employment limitations
- Urban job competition

## Migration Patterns

Youth migration includes:
- Movement to Nairobi seeking employment
- Migration to other Kenyan cities
- Temporary and circular migration
- Permanent relocation of some youth
- Remittances supporting home families

## Education and Skills

Youth development involves:
- Secondary education completion
- Higher education pursuit
- Technical and vocational training
- Skills development programs
- Continuing education opportunities

## Entrepreneurship

Youth business initiatives include:
- Small-scale trading
- Agricultural value addition
- Service provision
- Artisanal craft production
- Technology-based businesses
- Mobile money services

## Social Challenges

Youth face social issues:
- Substance abuse risks
- Limited recreational facilities
- Social marginalization
- High youth dependency ratios
- Family formation constraints

## Youth Organizations

Community involvement includes:
- Youth groups and associations
- Sports and recreational activities
- Educational groups
- Political participation
- Community development initiatives
- Peer support networks

## Future Prospects

Youth development priorities include:
- Job creation and opportunity development
- Education and skills improvement
- Entrepreneurship support
- Agricultural engagement innovation
- Property and inheritance access
- Service delivery to youth
