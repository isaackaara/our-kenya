Kajiado County's health sector provides services to pastoral, agricultural, and increasingly urban populations, with challenges of limited facility density, resource constraints, and disease burdens reflecting environmental conditions and pastoral livelihood vulnerabilities.

## Healthcare Infrastructure

Government health facilities including Kajiado County Hospital, health centers, and dispensaries provide healthcare services. Private healthcare providers operate in towns and urban areas, serving those with financial capacity.

Kajiado County Hospital serves as the referral facility for complex cases. Health centers provide outpatient services and basic inpatient care in secondary locations. Dispensaries serve as first-contact facilities in pastoral areas but often lack essential equipment.

## Health Challenges

Disease burden reflects pastoral living conditions including water and sanitation challenges, nutrition vulnerabilities, and limited preventive service access.

Malaria, waterborne diseases, and respiratory infections represent major health challenges, particularly affecting children. Maternal health challenges including high maternal and infant mortality reflect limited antenatal care and delivery service access in pastoral areas.

Malnutrition affects pastoral populations during drought periods when livestock losses reduce household food security.

## Health Workforce

Healthcare worker shortages constrain service provision. Recruitment and retention of qualified health professionals, particularly doctors and nurses, remain challenging due to rural location and limited professional development opportunities.

## Community Health Programs

Community health worker programs extend basic health services into pastoral areas. These programs focus on health education, disease surveillance, and preventive services including vaccination.
