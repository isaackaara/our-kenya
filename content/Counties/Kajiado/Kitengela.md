Kitengela represents the largest urban satellite town in Kajiado County and effectively functions as an extension of Nairobi's urban sprawl, with rapidly expanding residential, commercial, and mixed-use development driven by proximity to Nairobi and relative affordability compared to Nairobi proper.

Located approximately 25 to 40 kilometers south of central Nairobi along the Nairobi-Namanga highway, Kitengela has transformed from a small pastoral settlement into a sprawling urban center with a population estimated at 200,000 or more.

## Urban Development

Residential development in Kitengela includes planned estates with varied price ranges serving low, middle, and upper-income households. Estate development has been rapid and relatively unplanned, with infrastructure expansion lagging behind housing development.

Commercial development has proliferated including shopping centers, offices, hotels, and service facilities serving the expanding residential population.

Industrial development has also expanded, with light manufacturing and processing facilities taking advantage of relatively low land costs and highway proximity.

## Demographics and Population

Kitengela's population has grown from thousands in the 1990s to hundreds of thousands currently. Population growth reflects both natural increase and migration from Nairobi and surrounding pastoral areas seeking affordable housing.

The population is increasingly diverse, with residents from multiple ethnic groups and urban-origin populations alongside remaining pastoral families.

## Infrastructure Challenges

Infrastructure including water supply, sewerage, roads, and waste management has struggled to keep pace with population growth. Water scarcity remains a persistent challenge.

## Economic Activities

Beyond residential and commercial real estate, Kitengela's economy includes small-scale agriculture, petty trading, transportation, and service businesses serving the local population and Nairobi commuters.
