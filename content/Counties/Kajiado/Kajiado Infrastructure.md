Kajiado County's infrastructure includes roads connecting to Nairobi and Tanzania, water supply systems, electricity networks, and telecommunications services, with development focused on main transportation corridors and urban centers while rural pastoral areas remain underserved.

## Road Network

The Nairobi-Namanga highway represents the primary transportation route, connecting Kenya to Tanzania. The highway passes through Kajiado and has received improvements in recent years to enhance road quality and reduce travel times.

Secondary roads connecting Kajiado town, Amboseli, and pastoral areas remain in variable condition, with many rural roads deteriorating during rainy seasons.

## Water Infrastructure

Water supply infrastructure includes boreholes, piped water systems in towns, and water ponds for pastoral use. Water supply remains inadequate, particularly in arid pastoral areas where water scarcity constrains both human and livestock populations.

Water development projects have focused on pastoral areas with boreholes and conservation techniques, though coverage remains limited.

## Electricity and Energy

Urban areas including Kajiado town, Kitengela, and Ngong have grid electricity supply. Rural areas increasingly use solar and off-grid electricity solutions.

Renewable energy development including solar has expanded, providing electricity access to previously unconnected areas.

## Telecommunications

Mobile phone coverage has expanded substantially, providing communication access across much of the county. Internet connectivity remains variable, with better access in towns and urban areas.
