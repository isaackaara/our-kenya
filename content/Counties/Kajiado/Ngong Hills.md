# Ngong Hills

The Ngong Hills represent a distinctive ridgeline on [[../../[[../../Maasai/Maasai|Maasai]]/Maasai|Maasai]] escarpment located west of Nairobi in Kajiado County, forming a prominent landscape feature visible from Nairobi and providing scenic vistas across the surrounding plains and toward the Rift Valley.

The hills reach elevations of approximately 2,600 meters at their highest point and create a characteristic ridgeline profile visible across vast distances.

## Geographic Characteristics

The Ngong Hills form part of the Rift Valley escarpment system, created by tectonic activity. The ridgeline extends north-south for approximately 16 kilometers, visible from Nairobi and other surrounding areas.

The hills provide panoramic views of Nairobi, the surrounding plains, and the Rift Valley landscape, making them geographically significant landmark features.

## Ecological Significance

The hills support indigenous vegetation including grasslands, shrublands, and occasional woodland patches. These ecosystems support wildlife including antelope species, wild dogs, and other fauna adapted to hill and escarpment habitats.

Forest patches in the hills have been cleared for agricultural and residential development, reducing habitat extent.

## Cultural and Historical Significance

The Ngong Hills hold cultural significance for Maasai pastoral communities and have featured prominently in Kenyan and international literature through Karen Blixen's "Out of Africa."

## Contemporary Uses

The hills are used for residential development, agricultural production, and recreation including hiking and tourism activities. The viewpoints and hiking trails attract local and international visitors.

Ecological degradation through deforestation and overgrazing represents an ongoing conservation challenge.
