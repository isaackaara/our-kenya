# Kajiado Youth

Kajiado youth navigate distinctive opportunities and challenges shaped by pastoral heritage, land loss, education access improvements, urban-proximity employment opportunities, and limited economic prospects in traditional pastoral sectors.

## Education and Skills Development

Secondary and tertiary education provides pathways to professional employment in government, teaching, healthcare, and business. Youth education completion rates have improved with government free education policies.

Skills training programs focusing on tourism, hospitality, and business management address employment demand in growth sectors.

## Employment Challenges

Youth face limited employment opportunities despite education improvements. Unemployment and underemployment remain significant, particularly in rural areas and among those lacking advanced education.

Tourism and service sector employment provides opportunities for some youth, though employment remains seasonal and wages variable.

## Urban Migration

Substantial youth migration from pastoral areas to Nairobi and other urban centers reflects limited rural economic opportunities. Urban migration has become a defining demographic trend among Kajiado youth.

Youth diaspora have become increasingly engaged with urban economies and lifestyles, creating cultural shifts within communities.

## Livelihood Diversification

Youth increasingly pursue non-pastoral livelihoods including agriculture, trading, wage employment, and entrepreneurship. Pastoral occupations have become less attractive despite cultural heritage.

## Social Challenges

Unemployment and underemployment create frustration and vulnerability to substance abuse, crime, and other social problems. Limited economic prospects constrain life opportunities and family formation.
