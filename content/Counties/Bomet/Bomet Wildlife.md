# Bomet Wildlife

Wildlife bordering the Maasai Mara ecosystem creates both conservation opportunities and human-wildlife conflict challenges in Bomet County. Wildlife management requires balancing conservation with community livelihood protection.

## Wildlife Species

Fauna in county borders includes:
- Large predators (lions, leopards)
- Ungulate species (wildebeest, zebra, antelope)
- Elephant populations
- Smaller mammals
- Bird species diversity
- Reptile species

## Habitat Types

Ecosystems include:
- Savanna grasslands
- Woodland and bush
- Forest patches
- Water sources and riparian areas
- Wildlife corridors
- Protected area interfaces

## Conservation Value

Wildlife importance includes:
- Biodiversity hotspot
- Ecosystem service provision
- Cultural heritage value
- Tourism potential
- Genetic resource preservation
- Educational value

## Human-Wildlife Conflict

Conflict challenges include:
- Crop-raiding by wildlife
- Livestock predation by large carnivores
- Human safety concerns and incidents
- Property damage incidents
- Community livelihood threats
- Conflict resolution needs

## Conflict Mitigation

Responses to conflict:
- Community conservation groups
- Compensation programs
- Early warning systems
- Wildlife dispersal assistance
- Livelihood diversification
- Land use planning

## Wildlife Corridors

Movement pathways important for:
- Species migration and movement
- Genetic diversity maintenance
- Ecosystem connectivity
- Population sustainability
- Livelihood balance

## Conservation Partnerships

Coordination includes:
- Kenya Wildlife Service management
- County government cooperation
- Community conservation initiatives
- NGO conservation organizations
- Tourism operator involvement
- Cross-border agreements

## Tourism and Wildlife

Wildlife-related tourism:
- Mara ecosystem tourism access
- Employment generation
- Economic benefits
- Infrastructure development
- Conservation funding

## Research and Monitoring

Wildlife knowledge building:
- Research projects and studies
- Population monitoring
- Conservation assessments
- Human-wildlife conflict analysis
- Adaptive management
