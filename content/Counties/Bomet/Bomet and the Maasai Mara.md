Bomet County borders the Maasai Mara National Reserve, creating significant tourism, conservation, and economic development opportunities. The proximity to the Mara shapes county development priorities and livelihood strategies.

## Geographic Proximity

Bomet's location involves:
- Border with Maasai Mara National Reserve
- Unique ecosystems at reserve margins
- Wildlife habitat connectivity
- Access to Mara tourism opportunities
- Conservation area management role
- Cross-border natural resource issues

## Tourism Economy

Tourism activities include:
- Visitors accessing Mara from Bomet
- Tourist accommodation facilities
- Guide and porter services
- Transportation and logistics
- Restaurant and food services
- Craft and souvenir sales

Tourism generates employment and income for communities.

## Wildlife Conservation

Conservation issues include:
- Habitat protection at reserve boundaries
- Community wildlife corridor access
- Anti-poaching efforts and coordination
- Research and monitoring activities
- Environmental impact management
- Protected area regulations

## Human-Wildlife Conflict

Conflict challenges include:
- Crop-raiding by wildlife
- Livestock predation
- Human safety concerns
- Property damage incidents
- Conflict resolution mechanisms
- Compensation programs

Wildlife conservation and livelihood balancing requires ongoing effort.

## Community Benefits

Mara proximity benefits include:
- Employment in tourism services
- Community conservation partnerships
- Educational opportunities
- Infrastructure development
- Market opportunities
- Conservation awareness

## Economic Integration

County economic links to Mara:
- Tourism revenue generation
- Transportation and trade
- Agricultural supply to lodges

- Seasonal worker migration
- Infrastructure development

- County government and reserve management

## Development Challenges

Mara proximity creates challenges:
