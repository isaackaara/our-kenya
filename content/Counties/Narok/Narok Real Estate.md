Narok County's real estate market encompasses property development in Narok town, tourism-related commercial properties, agricultural land sales, and conservancy land conversions, reflecting expanding investment interest and property commodification in pastoral territories.

## Urban Property Development

Narok town has experienced property development including residential housing, commercial shops, office spaces, and hospitality facilities serving tourism and government administration. Property values have appreciated with infrastructure improvements and tourism growth.

Real estate speculation has increased, with investors from Nairobi and other regions purchasing land for anticipated appreciation.

## Tourism-Related Properties

Investment in safari camps, lodges, and hospitality facilities represents substantial real estate development attracting international capital and Kenyan investors. Tourism infrastructure development reflects economic opportunity perception in the wildlife tourism sector.

## Agricultural Land Markets

Agricultural land on the Mau Escarpment and elevated areas commands higher prices reflecting wheat farming potential and proximity to Nairobi markets.

Land prices have risen substantially with agricultural commercialization and improved road access.

## Group Ranch Land Sales

The conversion of group ranch land to individual titles facilitated land sales to external investors and property speculators. Cumulative sales have fragmented pastoral territories and reduced common property resources.

## Conservation and Protected Area Land

Land within conservancies has appreciated value with tourism development potential. Private conservancy investments have created new land categories combining conservation status with commercial operations.

## Property Rights and Disputes

Land title insecurity, boundary disputes, and historical sale controversies create legal complications and investment risks. Land administration challenges limit property market confidence.
