Narok County's health sector faces challenges of limited facility density, resource constraints, disease burden including climate-related illnesses, and service gaps particularly affecting pastoral communities in remote areas.

## Healthcare Infrastructure

Government health facilities including district hospitals, health centers, and dispensaries provide primary healthcare services across Narok County. Private healthcare providers operate in Narok town and other trading centers, serving those with financial capacity.

Narok County Hospital serves as the referral facility for complex cases. Health centers provide outpatient services and basic inpatient care. Dispensaries serve as first-contact facilities in remote areas but often lack essential equipment and medications.

## Health Challenges

Disease burden reflects environmental factors including water quality, sanitation, nutrition, and access to preventive services. Malaria, waterborne diseases, and respiratory infections represent major health challenges, particularly affecting children and vulnerable populations.

Maternal health services remain inadequate in pastoral areas, with high maternal and infant mortality rates reflecting limited antenatal care access, delivery service availability, and post-natal care.

Malnutrition affects pastoral communities during drought periods when livestock losses reduce household food security and purchasing power.

## Health Workforce

Healthcare worker shortages constrain service provision. Recruitment and retention of qualified health professionals, particularly nurses and doctors, remain challenging due to rural location, limited housing, and professional development opportunities.

## Community Health Programs

Community health worker programs extend health services into pastoral areas where formal facility access is limited. These programs focus on health education, disease surveillance, and basic services including vaccination and maternal health support.
