# Hola Massacre

The Hola Massacre of April 1959 occurred at a detention camp in Hola where British colonial guards beat and killed 11 Mau Mau detainees. The incident represents one of the most severe documented cases of colonial brutality during the Mau Mau emergency and contributed significantly to British Parliament's support for Kenyan independence.

The detainees were being held at the Hola camp during the Mau Mau emergency (1952-1960) when British and colonial authorities sought to suppress the independence rebellion. The specific circumstances of the massacre remain debated, though accounts indicate guards severely beat detainees, possibly for refusing to work or attempting escape.

The incident shocked the British Parliament and international observers. The Colonial Secretary acknowledged the deaths and ordered investigations. The incident contributed to shifting British public opinion toward supporting African independence movements rather than attempting to maintain costly colonial control.

The massacre affected Mau Mau veterans and detainees' families, creating lasting trauma and grievances. Survivors and descendants have sought acknowledgment and compensation for colonial abuses.
