# Hola Town

Hola is the county headquarters of Tana River County, a small town of approximately 8,000 people located along the Tana River. The town serves as the administrative center for county government operations, hosting the county commissioner's office, county assembly, courts, and government ministries.

Hola's historical significance centers on the Hola Massacre of 1959, when British colonial guards killed 11 Mau Mau detainees in a prison camp. The incident shocked the British Parliament and contributed to support for Kenyan independence. The massacre remains traumatic in Hola's collective memory.

The town's economy revolves around government employment, local commerce, and river-based activities. Agriculture in flood-recession areas supports farming families and provides produce for local markets. The Tana River remains important for fishing and water access.

Infrastructure remains limited despite being a county headquarters. Water scarcity during dry seasons affects residents. Health and education facilities remain under-resourced compared to national standards.
