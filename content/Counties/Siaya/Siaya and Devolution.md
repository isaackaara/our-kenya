Kenya's 2010 constitutional devolution transferred significant governance responsibilities to county governments, including [[Siaya County]]. Devolution established directly elected county governments responsible for service delivery, development planning, and local governance.

## County Government Structure

Siaya County Government comprises an elected governor, deputy governor, and county assembly with elected representatives. The county is divided into electoral wards, each electing a member to the county assembly. The governor serves as chief executive of county government, overseeing implementation of county functions.

## Devolved Functions

Siaya County Government is responsible for primary and secondary education, public health, water supply, roads, agriculture extension, and other development services. The national government retains responsibility for national security, foreign relations, national taxes, and other central functions. This division of responsibility creates complex intergovernmental relationships.

## County Development Planning

The county government develops integrated development plans, county budgets, and sector strategies. County budget allocation determines provision of services in health, education, water, and infrastructure. Budget constraints limit the county's ability to provide quality services across all sectors.

## Fiscal Challenges

Counties operate with budgets provided through national revenue-sharing arrangements and own-source revenues from local taxation and user fees. Siaya County's revenue base is limited relative to development needs, requiring prioritization of available resources.

## County Coordination with National Government

Siaya County works with national government agencies in areas of shared responsibility. Health services require coordination between county health facilities and national disease control programs. Education delivery involves coordination with national curriculum and examination authorities.
