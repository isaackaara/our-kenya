Kenya's 2010 constitutional devolution transferred governance responsibilities to county governments, including Homa Bay County. Devolution established directly elected county government responsible for health, education, water, agriculture, and other development services.

## County Government Structure

Homa Bay County Government comprises an elected governor, deputy governor, and county assembly with elected members from county wards. The governor heads the county executive, overseeing implementation of county functions and service delivery.

## Devolved Functions and Responsibilities

Homa Bay County Government is responsible for primary and secondary education, public health, water supply, roads, sanitation, and agriculture extension services. The national government retains responsibility for national defense, foreign relations, and national taxation. This division creates complex intergovernmental dynamics.

## County Budget and Resource Allocation

The county operates with budgets derived from national revenue-sharing and own-source revenues. Budget constraints limit service provision, requiring difficult prioritization decisions. Inadequate revenue constrains development initiatives.

## County Development Planning

Integrated development plans guide county spending and development priorities. County planners attempt to address development needs through planned investment. Implementation often falls short of planning due to resource constraints.

## Coordination with National Government

County health services require coordination with national disease control and health policy frameworks. Education delivery involves coordination with national curriculum authorities. Water and agricultural services require multi-level coordination.

## Fiscal Challenges and Sustainability

Counties operate with chronic fiscal constraints limiting service quality. Dependency on national revenue-sharing creates budget vulnerability. Limited local revenue generation constrains expansion of county services.
