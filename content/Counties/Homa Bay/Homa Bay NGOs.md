Non-governmental organizations work extensively in Homa Bay County, addressing development priorities including HIV prevention and treatment, health service provision, education support, and economic empowerment. NGOs supplement government services and implement targeted interventions.

## HIV and AIDS Organizations

HIV-focused NGOs operate in Homa Bay, providing testing, treatment support, and prevention services. Organizations work with key populations including fishing communities. Community mobilization addresses HIV issues and stigma reduction.

## Health Service Organizations

Health-focused organizations provide maternal health services, immunization support, and health education. Some organizations support county health facilities with equipment and supplies. Community health worker programs employ volunteers.

## Education Support Organizations

NGOs support education through scholarships, school feeding, and education advocacy. Organizations construct facilities and provide teaching materials. Education programs address access and quality barriers.

## Water and Sanitation

Water and sanitation organizations construct water facilities and promote hygiene practices. These efforts address water access and sanitation challenges prevalent in the county.

## Economic Empowerment

Microfinance organizations provide credit and savings services to individuals and groups. Agricultural organizations provide training and market linkages. Women and youth empowerment programs address economic opportunities.

## Environmental and Conservation Organizations

Environmental organizations work on water conservation, wetland protection, and sustainable resource management. Lake Victoria conservation efforts engage fishing communities.

## Governance and Rights Organizations

Human rights and governance organizations engage in community advocacy and governance accountability. Organizations support rights awareness and participatory governance.
