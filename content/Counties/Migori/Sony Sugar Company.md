# Sony Sugar Company

Sony Sugar (South Nyanza Sugar Company) is a government sugar company located in Migori County, southwestern Kenya. The company was established in 1979 and operates a sugar mill processing sugarcane into raw sugar. The company serves as a major employer and economic actor in the region.

## Company History and Establishment

Sony Sugar was founded in 1979 to increase national sugar production and reduce dependence on sugar imports. The company was initially managed by Mehta Group International, with later management by Booker Agricultural International (Booker Tate Ltd.). Management transitions reflect evolving operational approaches.

## Operations and Production

The mill processes sugarcane into raw sugar for local consumption and export. Production capacity affects company output and farmer cane purchasing. Factory operations require significant water and energy inputs.

## Farmer Relationship

Sony Sugar contracts with farming communities to supply sugarcane. Farmers negotiate cane prices with the company. Reliable cane supply is critical to company operations.

## Employment and Labor

The company employs hundreds of workers in factory operations, administration, and support functions. Employment provides formal wages to workers. Labor relations and worker welfare are important operational considerations.

## Economic Contribution

Sony Sugar contributes to national sugar production and exports. The company generates government revenue through taxation. Sugar industry provides income to farming families and communities.

## Recent Management Changes

As of May 2025, Busia Sugar Industry CEO Ali Ahmed Taib took over operations of Sony Sugar, indicating management transitions in the company. New management may bring operational changes.

## Challenges

Market competition from imported sugar affects industry profitability. Factory efficiency impacts company sustainability. Farmer payment delays occasionally occur, affecting farmer income and company relationships.

## Regional Impact

Sony Sugar represents a major economic enterprise in the region, affecting livelihoods of farming families and workers. Factory operations shape regional economy and development.
