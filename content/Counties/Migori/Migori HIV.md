Migori County has a high HIV burden, with prevalence rates estimated at approximately 7.02 percent among men. The HIV epidemic represents a serious public health challenge affecting the county's population and healthcare systems.

## Prevalence and Extent

HIV prevalence in Migori is among Kenya's highest, with substantial populations living with HIV. Surveillance data documents the burden. Fishing communities show elevated infection rates.

## Contributing Factors

Multiple factors contribute to high prevalence including population mobility, limited healthcare access, low condom use, multiple concurrent partnerships, and transactional sex. Fishing communities' characteristics create vulnerability.

## Fishing Community Vulnerability

Fishing communities experience elevated HIV prevalence due to mobile populations and social conditions including sex work. Population mobility creates infection risk and treatment access challenges.

## Treatment and Prevention

The county implements HIV testing, treatment, and prevention programs. Antiretroviral therapy programs provide treatment to HIV-positive individuals. Prevention programs emphasize testing, safe sex, and harm reduction.

## Community Impact

The HIV epidemic creates household impacts including death of working-age adults, orphaning of children, and economic strain. Healthcare systems experience strain. Stigma affects treatment-seeking behavior.

## Treatment Access

Access to antiretroviral therapy has improved, though challenges remain. Treatment adherence support and long-term management require strengthening. Periodic drug stock-outs affect treatment continuity.

## Prevention Programs

HIV prevention programs emphasize behavior change communication, condom distribution, and testing. Youth education addresses sexual health. Outreach programs provide prevention and health services.
