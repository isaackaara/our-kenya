# Mfangano Island

Mfangano Island is located in Lake Victoria's eastern portion at the mouth of the Winam Gulf in Migori County. The island covers approximately 56 to 65 square kilometers and rises to 1,694 meters at Mount Kwitutu. The island has a population estimated at over 26,000 residents and is the principal home of the Suba community.

## Geography and Topography

Mfangano Island is one of Lake Victoria's largest islands. The island has varied topography with Mount Kwitutu being the highest point. Vegetation includes forests and cultivated areas. The island's geography creates distinct microclimates.

## Suba Community and Settlement

The Suba represent the primary population, with their main settlement concentrations on Mfangano Island. The island is the center of Suba cultural identity and heritage. Suba fishing traditions have long roots on the island.

## Fishing Economy

Fishing represents the primary livelihood for island communities. Fish catches from Lake Victoria provide income and protein. Fishing communities face challenges from declining fish stocks.

## Ancient Rock Art

Mfangano Island contains ancient rock art sites with prehistoric paintings and engravings. These sites represent important cultural and archaeological heritage. The rock art provides evidence of historical human settlement and artistic traditions.

## HIV Epidemic

Fishing communities on Mfangano experience elevated HIV prevalence. Population isolation and health service access limitations create health challenges. The epidemic creates household and community impacts.

## Water and Sanitation

Water access remains challenging despite island location on a lake. Drinking water sources include boreholes and rainwater harvesting. Sanitation infrastructure is limited.

## Healthcare

Healthcare facilities are minimal on the island, requiring travel to mainland for serious conditions. Community health worker programs provide basic health services. Healthcare access represents a significant challenge.

## Tourism Potential

The island offers tourism potential through natural beauty, Suba cultural experiences, and ancient rock art sites. Tourism infrastructure development is minimal. Ecotourism development could support conservation.
