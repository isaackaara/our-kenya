Nairobi is the center of Kenya's education system, home to the country's best-performing schools and most prestigious universities. The concentration of educational institutions in the capital has made education an important sector for both local employment and the national economy.

## Primary and Secondary Schools

Nairobi hosts numerous elite primary and secondary schools, both public and private, that are among Kenya's best-performing institutions. Schools like Kenya High School, Nairobi High School, Pangani High School, and numerous private preparatory schools attract students from across Kenya and East Africa.

## Universities

The University of Nairobi, Kenya's oldest and largest university, is located in the city. Founded in 1970, it serves over 60,000 students across multiple campuses. Other universities with presence in Nairobi include Kenyatta University, Strathmore University, Mount Kenya University, and many smaller institutions.

## Education as Economic Sector

Education is a significant employer and economic sector in Nairobi. Thousands of teachers, administrators, and support staff work in schools and universities. Private education companies, tutoring centers, and educational technology firms contribute to the economy.

## Quality Disparities

While Nairobi hosts excellent schools and universities, significant disparities exist between elite institutions and schools serving lower-income communities. Informal settlement schools often lack basic resources, qualified teachers, and infrastructure.

## International Education

International schools offering curricula from other countries (British, American, international baccalaureate) operate in Nairobi, serving expatriate and wealthy Kenyan families. These schools command high fees but provide alternative educational pathways.

## Technical and Vocational Training

Nairobi also hosts technical colleges and vocational training institutions providing skills training in trades, technology, and business. These institutions prepare workers for the job market and support economic development.

## Research and Innovation

Nairobi's universities conduct research in various fields, including agriculture, health, engineering, and social sciences. Research output contributes to national development and international knowledge.
