# Nairobi: Kenya's Capital City

Nairobi is Kenya's capital and East Africa's largest city, with a metropolitan population of 4 to 5 million people. Founded in 1899 as a railway depot on the Uganda Railway, it has grown into the region's dominant financial, commercial, and cultural hub. The city sits at an altitude of 1,795 meters, giving it a temperate climate unusual for equatorial Africa.

## Overview

The city occupies a sprawling urban area divided into distinct neighborhoods reflecting its colonial origins and post-independence development. Nairobi is home to all 47 ethnic groups of Kenya, plus significant Asian, Arab, European, and international populations, making it one of Africa's most cosmopolitan cities.

The economy is dominated by financial services, telecommunications, UN agencies (UNEP and UN-Habitat headquarters), manufacturing, retail, and a growing tech sector known as Silicon Savannah. The city generates approximately 25 percent of Kenya's GDP.
