# Meru Town

Meru Town serves as the commercial and administrative hub of Meru County. Located at approximately 1,600 meters elevation in the mid-altitude agricultural zone, it functions as a regional service center for the surrounding agricultural communities. The town's strategic location makes it a natural gathering point for farmers bringing produce to market, traders sourcing agricultural inputs, and government administrators managing county affairs.

## Urban Development

The town has grown substantially since it was designated as the county headquarters following the devolution of government in 2013. Main Street runs through the commercial center, where shops, banks, and trading posts cluster. The town center includes the [[../../Meru/Meru|Meru]] County Government offices, numerous supermarkets catering to farmers and traders, and informal markets where agricultural produce changes hands daily.

## Economic Significance

Meru Town serves as the primary market outlet for miraa farmers from the Nyambene Hills. Weekly and daily trading in miraa generate substantial economic activity. The town also functions as a distribution hub for coffee, tea, and honey produced in Meru County. Agricultural input suppliers operate from the town, selling seeds, fertilizers, and farming equipment to surrounding farmers.

## Transport Hub

The town lies along the main road connecting Nairobi to Meru and onward to Isiolo. Regular matatu (minibus) services connect Meru Town to Nairobi, other Meru towns, and neighboring counties. This transport connectivity makes the town accessible to farmers marketing their produce and to traders seeking supplies.

## Services and Infrastructure

Meru Town hosts the main hospitals and health centers serving the county. Educational institutions including secondary schools draw students from surrounding areas. Banks and financial services institutions have established branches in the town. Hotels and guesthouses serve traders, government visitors, and business travelers.
