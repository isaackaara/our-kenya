Infrastructure development in Meru County has improved in recent decades, though gaps remain in road quality, water supply, and energy access. The county's road network connects towns and trading centers, facilitating agricultural marketing and commerce. Investment in infrastructure has accelerated since devolution in 2013, with county governments prioritizing development projects.

## Road Network

The main highway connecting Nairobi to Meru Town passes through the county, providing the primary transportation corridor. Secondary roads connect Meru Town to other market centers and constituencies. Numerous tertiary roads link smaller towns and rural areas, though many remain seasonally impassable during heavy rains. Road quality varies from well-maintained tarmac highways to rough earth tracks.

## Meru-Nairobi Route

The Nairobi to Meru road is a vital commercial artery carrying agricultural produce and consumer goods. The road supports daily matatu services connecting the two cities, taking approximately four to five hours under normal conditions. Regular maintenance of this route is critical for the county's economy and connectivity. Road rehabilitation projects have periodically upgraded sections of the route.

## Standard Gauge Railway Extension

A planned Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) extension from Nairobi toward Meru has been under discussion for several years. The proposed extension would improve transport efficiency for agricultural exports and reduce travel times. As of 2026, the SGR extension remains in planning stages, though feasibility studies have been completed. Implementation would substantially transform freight and passenger transport in the region.

## Water Supply Infrastructure

Water supply systems in Meru remain inadequate in many areas. Municipal water systems serve Meru Town and larger market centers, though supply often falls short of demand during dry seasons. Rural water supply depends on boreholes, springs, and rainwater harvesting. Development of piped water systems remains an ongoing challenge in remote areas.

## Energy Access

Electricity access has expanded in Meru County through Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC) grid extension and off-grid solutions including solar systems. Major towns have reliable electricity supply. Rural electrification remains incomplete, with many settlements lacking grid connection. Off-grid solar solutions are becoming more common for rural households.

## Healthcare Facilities

Meru County hosts the [[Meru|Meru]] Teaching and Referral Hospital, the county's largest healthcare facility. Smaller district and health centers provide basic services throughout the county. Healthcare infrastructure remains inadequate in some remote areas, requiring patients to travel long distances for specialized care.

## Educational Infrastructure

Secondary schools are distributed throughout the county, though infrastructure quality varies. Primary schools are more widely distributed, though some remote areas remain underserved. Technical and vocational training institutions serve students seeking skills training. Chuka University operates a campus in the region, providing higher education access.
