Machakos County, particularly the areas immediately east of Nairobi, has experienced significant industrial development driven by proximity to the national capital, available land, and government industrial promotion policies. The county hosts several Export Processing Zones (EPZs) and industrial parks that have attracted manufacturing enterprises and created employment.

The Athi River-Mavoko industrial corridor represents the primary industrial zone in Machakos County. Located on the Nairobi-Mombasa highway, this corridor benefits from excellent transportation connectivity, proximity to Nairobi's markets and services, and lower land costs than Nairobi proper. The corridor hosts diverse manufacturing activities including plastic processing, textile production, food processing, metal fabrication, and chemical manufacturing.

Export Processing Zones in Machakos County offer tax incentives and regulatory flexibility designed to attract manufacturing enterprises oriented toward international markets. These zones facilitate exports while generating employment and government revenue. EPZ enterprises often rely on imported raw materials and exported products, creating international supply chain integration.

Industrial development in Machakos has created employment opportunities for rural residents while also attracting migrant workers from other regions. Industrial jobs provide cash income and skills training, though working conditions and wage levels vary considerably. Industrial pollution, including air emissions and water contamination, represents an ongoing challenge requiring environmental management.

The presence of industrial activity has stimulated supporting services including banking, logistics, equipment supply, and business services. Transportation infrastructure, including improved roads and warehousing facilities, has been enhanced to support industrial operations. Industrial development has also generated government revenue that funds county services and infrastructure.
