The Nandi Hills represent the defining geographic feature of Nandi County, a prominent highland formation rising to approximately 2,400 metres at its highest points and providing the region's distinctive topography and environmental character. These hills, formed as part of the western Rift Valley escarpment, provide exceptional elevation and terrain that have shaped human settlement, agriculture, and notably, distance running excellence.

The cool highland climate of the Nandi Hills makes them ideal for tea cultivation and other high-value crops, supporting productive agriculture benefiting from reliable rainfall and cool temperatures. The elevation and terrain provide natural advantages for distance running, with the altitude supporting physiological adaptations beneficial for endurance athletes. Historical pastoral communities used the Nandi Hills for cattle herding, with extensive highland grasslands supporting livestock production. The strategic high terrain made the Nandi Hills defensible during the colonial period, contributing to Nandi military resistance capabilities. Contemporary Nandi Hills support dense human settlement and agricultural production, with hillside slopes terraced or cultivated in some areas. The distinctive landscape has become iconic in Nandi cultural identity and is featured prominently in regional and national representations of the county. Elevation varies across the county, creating multiple agro-ecological zones with different agricultural potential and settlement patterns.
