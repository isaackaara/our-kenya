Nandi County's education system reflects both colonial legacies and post-independence educational development, with particular emphasis on athletic training and coaching education. The county hosts numerous primary and secondary schools serving local populations, with some schools achieving national recognition for academic excellence. Kapsabet Boys High School, the most notable secondary school in the county, has achieved fame for producing numerous world-class distance runners alongside maintaining strong academic performance.

Colonial-era educational institutions established by missionaries and the state continue operating, often maintaining high standards. The expansion of education following independence, particularly after free primary education introduced in 2003, has significantly increased enrollment across the county. However, quality disparities persist between well-resourced urban schools and under-resourced rural facilities. Technical and vocational training institutions provide skills development for youth entering the workforce. Universities in neighbouring areas provide tertiary education access, though many county residents pursue higher education in Nairobi and other urban centres. Running coaching and athletic training schools have emerged as distinctive educational components, with specialised programmes developing athletic talent. Educational access remains a priority for county government and communities, with ongoing investments in facilities and teacher training. The integration of academic education with athletic training creates a distinctive educational environment in Nandi.
