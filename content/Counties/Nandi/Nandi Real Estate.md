Nandi County's real estate market reflects economic prosperity driven by agriculture and running tourism, population growth, and the region's appeal as a residential and investment destination. Kapsabet and surrounding towns have experienced property value appreciation, attracting both local residents and external investors. Residential properties including houses and apartments are owned by local families, returning diaspora, and external investors seeking highland property.

Commercial properties including shops, offices, restaurants, and accommodation facilities cluster in Kapsabet's central business district, where demand remains strong from traders and service providers. Running-related commercial properties including training camps, coaching facilities, and athlete accommodation represent a growing real estate segment. Agricultural land including tea estates and smallholder plots represents significant real estate value, though some transactions are complicated by historical disputes over ownership and titling. The real estate market has attracted external investors including land developers, builders, and foreign nationals seeking vacation homes or investment properties. Rising land values have created opportunities for land-owning communities and concerns about affordability and displacement. Property development has accelerated, with new residential estates and commercial complexes under construction. The real estate market includes informal housing settlements on urban peripheries where low-income residents occupy properties with insecure tenure. Land speculation and conversion of agricultural land to residential use represent ongoing trends shaping the county landscape.
