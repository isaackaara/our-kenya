Environmental literature in Kenya addresses ecological transformation, climate change, and humanity's relationship to natural worlds through narrative forms engaging urgent contemporary concerns. As writers confront environmental degradation and climate crisis, literature functions as vehicle for exploring environmental futures and mobilizing consciousness toward ecological restoration.

Kenyan writers increasingly engage environmental themes, addressing deforestation, wildlife loss, water scarcity, and climate change's impacts on pastoral and agricultural communities. Environmental literature depicts actual ecological consequences while exploring psychological and spiritual dimensions of environmental destruction and loss.

[[Wangari Maathai]], Kenya's Nobel Prize-winning environmental activist, produced writing integrating environmental advocacy with personal narrative and philosophical reflection. Her environmental writings demonstrated connections between environmental protection and women's liberation, establishing frameworks linking ecological and social justice concerns.

Contemporary Kenyan environmental literature addresses pressing ecological crises affecting the nation's future. Writers explore how environmental degradation affects different communities unequally, addressing environmental justice alongside ecological concerns. Environmental fiction and essays engage climate anxiety and possibilities for transformation.

Oral traditions addressing relationships between people and natural environments provide foundations for contemporary environmental literature. Writers drawing on indigenous ecological knowledge challenge assumptions that environmental thinking originated exclusively in Western scientific traditions, asserting African environmental epistemologies.

Poetry addresses environmental themes with particular intensity, with poets exploring loss and grief accompanying environmental transformation. Environmental poetry sometimes employs mythological and spiritual registers, engaging non-rational dimensions of environmental experience alongside analytical critique.

Children's environmental literature introduces young readers to ecological concerns and possibilities for environmental engagement. Educational environmental literature serves pedagogical purposes while cultivating ecological consciousness in emerging generations.

The relationship between environmental literature and environmental activism proves significant, with writers sometimes participating directly in conservation movements and environmental politics. Literature complements and extends environmental activism, reaching audiences through narrative and emotional engagement alongside political organizing.
