[[Samburu]] people live in Nairobi and other urban centers, maintaining ties to home communities through remittances and visits. Urban [[Samburu]] navigate between [[Samburu Pastoralism|pastoral identity]] and urban life. Diaspora communities maintain cultural identity through churches, associations, and [[Samburu Language|language]] use. Return migration occurs as individuals retire or respond to community needs.

## Urban Migration Patterns

Samburu migrate to cities for employment, education, and business opportunities. Young Samburu seek wage employment unavailable in pastoral areas. Urban residence enables higher incomes and access to services, though urban cost of living is substantially higher than rural areas.

## Remittance Flows

Urban Samburu regularly send remittances to rural family members, supporting household consumption and investment. Remittances provide critical income for rural families particularly during drought. Money transfer services enable efficient remittance delivery.
