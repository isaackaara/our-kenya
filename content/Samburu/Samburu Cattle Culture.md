Cattle hold profound cultural meaning in [[Samburu]] society, representing wealth, identity, social status, and spiritual significance. Cattle provide milk, meat, and products forming economic basis. Cattle ownership enables [[Samburu Marriage|marriage]], establishes prestige, and functions in ceremonial contexts. [[Samburu Pastoralism|Pastoral identity]] centers on cattle cultural importance.

## Wealth and Status

Cattle ownership indicates wealth and family status. Large herds signify prosperity and social prestige. Cattle numbers affect marriage prospects and community respect. Economic security depends substantially on livestock numbers.

## Subsistence and Production

Milk from cattle provides primary food source. Meat provides supplementary nutrition, particularly ceremonially. Cattle provide draft power and transportation historically. Economic livelihood depends fundamentally on cattle productivity.

## Spiritual Significance

Cattle have spiritual value in Samburu cosmology. Cattle are offered in ritual contexts and appeal to divine forces. Cultural identity deeply incorporates cattle, with pastoral identity inseparable from cattle herding.
