Tourism in [[Samburu County]] centers on wildlife viewing ([[Samburu National Reserve]] and [[Buffalo Springs Reserve]]) and increasingly, cultural tourism featuring [[Samburu]] communities. Tourism provides income and employment, though economic benefits are unevenly distributed and cultural commodification raises concerns. Tourism represents secondary livelihood for [[Samburu Pastoralism|pastoral communities]] but growing economic importance.

## Wildlife Tourism

Wildlife tourism attracts international visitors to see endangered species (Grevy's zebra, reticulated giraffe, gerenuk) in natural habitat. Game drives and guided wildlife viewing provide tourism activities. Tourism generates revenue for lodges, tour operators, and tourism employees. Wildlife tourism remains the primary tourism form in Samburu.

## Cultural Tourism

Cultural tourism attempts to showcase Samburu communities and traditions. Manyatta (traditional Samburu settlement) visits, beadwork demonstrations, and cultural performances represent cultural tourism activities. Communities sometimes earn income from cultural tourism, though payment for cultural participation may be minimal.

## Tourism Infrastructure

Lodges and camps provide accommodation for tourists, ranging from basic to luxury facilities. Tourism infrastructure is concentrated near wildlife reserves and Maralal town. Employment in tourism provides income for lodge staff, guides, drivers, and support workers. Local supply provision provides additional employment.

## Economic Benefits and Distribution

Tourism generates substantial revenue, but benefits are unevenly distributed. Tourism companies (often foreign or Nairobi-based) capture significant share of tourism revenue. Local Samburu communities receive modest benefits through employment and small business opportunities. Benefit-sharing arrangements remain limited.

## Employment and Skills

Tourism provides employment as guides, drivers, lodge staff, cooks, and support workers. Tourism employment typically requires minimal formal education, though language skills (English and Swahili) enhance employment prospects. Seasonal employment patterns affect livelihood stability.

## Cultural Commodification Concerns

Cultural tourism raises concerns about cultural authenticity and Samburu agency. Communities may feel pressured to perform culture for tourists rather than maintain culture for community purposes. Tourism may distort cultural practice, creating stylized performances rather than authentic practice.

## Maralal International Camel Derby

The International Camel Derby, held annually in Maralal, attracts tourists and media attention. The event showcases camel racing and Samburu culture. Tourism revenue from the event provides income for Maralal and surrounding communities. The event has grown substantially since its establishment.

## Environmental Impacts

Tourism creates environmental impacts through lodge construction, waste production, and wildlife disturbance. Vehicles cause vegetation damage and wildlife stress. Waste management remains inadequate in some tourism facilities. Environmental sustainability requires careful tourism management.

## Community Participation in Tourism

Community involvement in tourism decision-making varies. Some communities have participated in tourism planning and benefit-sharing. Others have limited participation and must adapt to tourism impacts without sufficient voice. Equitable community participation remains aspirational in many contexts.

## Off-Season and Seasonality

Tourism is seasonal, with peaks during international vacation periods and dry seasons when game viewing is optimal. Off-season periods see minimal tourism and reduced employment. Seasonal income volatility affects community livelihood stability and livelihood planning.

## Marketing and Promotion

Tourism marketing by national and international agencies promotes Kenya and [[Samburu]] as tourist destinations. Local marketing efforts remain limited. Internet and social media increasingly shape tourism destination choices. [[Samburu County]] tourism promotion could be enhanced.
