Entertainment journalism in Kenya developed as media coverage of music, theatre, film, celebrity news, and pop culture. Major newspapers including Daily Nation and The Standard developed entertainment sections addressing audience interest in popular culture. Entertainment reporting provided accessible content appealing to broad audiences while serving as vehicle for cultural commentary and criticism. Entertainment journalism reflected Kenya's vibrant music and entertainment industries and audience interest in celebrity news.

Music journalism became particularly significant as Kenya developed diverse music genres including Benga, Afrobeats, gospel, and reggae. Music journalists covered recording artists, concert events, and music industry developments. Music journalism's importance reflected music's cultural significance and substantial audience interest. The genre allowed reporters to combine cultural criticism with entertainment reporting, creating content appealing to music enthusiasts.

Television entertainment programming and talk shows provided platforms for celebrity interviews, music performances, and entertainment news. Morning television shows on NTV Kenya, Citizen TV, and KTN featured entertainment segments reaching audiences during peak viewing times. Television's visual capacity allowed presentation of music videos and performance footage, creating entertainment content complementing journalism. Entertainment programming balanced news and information with entertainment creating accessible viewing that reached broad audiences.

Film journalism addressing movie releases and film criticism developed through entertainment sections of newspapers and specialized film reviews. Entertainment reporters covered international and Kenyan film releases, providing film criticism and analysis. Film journalism provided cultural commentary while covering entertainment industry developments. The journalism created space for film criticism and cultural analysis alongside entertainment reporting.

Celebrity news and gossip journalism addressed audience interest in Kenyan and international celebrities' personal lives, relationships, and activities. This entertainment journalism form reached large audiences and generated significant media attention. Celebrity journalism sometimes raised ethical questions about privacy and newsworthiness, illustrating tensions between entertainment appeal and journalistic standards. Entertainment journalism's prominence reflected commercial media's dependence on audience appeal and advertising revenue.
