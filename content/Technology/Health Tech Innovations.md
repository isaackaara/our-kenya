Health technology innovations in Kenya addressed healthcare access challenges through digital solutions extending services beyond traditional clinic-based delivery. Telemedicine platforms connected patients with healthcare providers remotely, enabling consultation without travel. Mobile health applications delivered health information and primary care guidance. These innovations addressed constraints including shortage of trained healthcare workers, geographic distances to health facilities, and cost barriers limiting healthcare access.

Mobile health (mHealth) application development accelerated from 2010 onwards as smartphones became ubiquitous. Applications provided symptom checking, medication information, and health education accessible without healthcare provider interaction. Disease-specific applications supported patients managing chronic conditions including diabetes and hypertension. Maternal and child health applications provided pregnancy guidance and childhood development tracking. Integration with mobile payment systems enabled service monetization supporting long-term sustainability.

Electronic health records systems modernized health information management across public and private facilities. Digital records improved clinical decision-making through comprehensive patient history access. Integration across health facilities enabled coordinated care for patients utilizing multiple providers. Government health system digitization initiatives promoted EHR adoption, though implementation proceeded variably across facilities. Interoperability challenges constrained effectiveness of fragmented health information systems.

Diagnostic technology innovation included digital microscopy and laboratory equipment improving testing accessibility. Portable diagnostic devices enabled testing in primary health facilities previously dependent on referral to hospital laboratories. Point-of-care testing devices provided rapid results enabling immediate treatment decisions. These technologies particularly improved tuberculosis, malaria, and HIV testing accessibility in underserved areas.

Community health worker technology equipped health workers with tools improving performance. Mobile applications provided clinical guidance supporting diagnostic and treatment decisions. SMS reminders supported appointment attendance and medication adherence. Data collection through mobile applications provided performance metrics and supervision support. These technologies extended healthcare system capacity by augmenting community health worker effectiveness. By 2020, health technology had become integral to Kenya's healthcare system modernization strategy.
