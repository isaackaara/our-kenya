Water technology solutions addressed Kenya's water scarcity and quality challenges, particularly acute in arid and semi-arid regions. Rainwater harvesting technologies captured seasonal rainfall for dry season use. Boreholes and wells provided groundwater access in water-scarce areas. Water treatment technologies improved safety and quality. Investment in water infrastructure supported agricultural productivity and domestic water security in water-constrained regions.

Drip irrigation technology revolutionized water utilization efficiency in agriculture. Localized water application reduced losses from evaporation and runoff compared to flood irrigation. Crops received precise water quantities supporting optimal growth. Drip system adoption expanded significantly among smallholder farmers, representing effective adaptation to water scarcity. Affordability improvements through technology advancement and financing innovations expanded farmer access.

Water purification technologies addressed bacterial and chemical contamination threatening health and agricultural productivity. Sand filters, biosand filters, and ultraviolet systems provided treatment options at various scales from household to community levels. Solar-powered water treatment reduced operational costs in off-grid areas. Affordability improvements enabled adoption in low-income communities. Combination of technology and behavior change addressed water safety challenges.

Water harvesting and storage technologies captured seasonal rainfall for dry season utilization. Roof rainwater harvesting provided household water security. Community rainwater harvesting systems served multiple households. Sand dams captured subsurface water flows extending groundwater availability. Artificial aquifer recharge systems infiltrated water during wet seasons replenishing groundwater. These storage technologies addressed seasonal water variability.

Smart water management systems utilized sensors and automation optimizing water usage. Soil moisture sensors informed irrigation timing preventing over-watering. Smart meters detected leaks enabling rapid repair. Automated systems maintained water quality parameters. These technologies supported water security through use efficiency. Integration with renewable energy systems including solar power reduced operational costs. By 2020, water technology had become critical to Kenya's climate change adaptation strategy.
