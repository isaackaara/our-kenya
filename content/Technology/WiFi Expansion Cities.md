WiFi network expansion in Kenyan cities from 2010 onwards provided high-speed internet access in public spaces, educational institutions, and commercial establishments. Municipal governments in Nairobi, Mombasa, and secondary cities invested in public WiFi infrastructure to enhance urban services and attract residents and businesses. Libraries, parks, government offices, and transportation hubs implemented WiFi access points creating free or subsidized internet connectivity for populations unable to afford mobile data.

Nairobi emerged as the primary focus for WiFi expansion, with the city council investing in WiFi networks covering central business districts, parks, and public facilities. Google's WiFi Stumbler project and related initiatives provided free internet in select Nairobi neighborhoods through partnerships with community organizations and private sector sponsors. These public WiFi initiatives aimed to expand digital inclusion by removing cost barriers to internet access for low-income populations.

Commercial establishments including cafes, restaurants, hotels, and shopping centers provided WiFi access as customer amenities. Free WiFi attracted customers willing to pay premium prices for service and environment quality, creating competitive advantage for establishments offering connectivity. Business travelers, students, and remote workers utilized cafe WiFi for work and entertainment, driving demand for reliable high-speed internet in commercial spaces. WiFi signal strength and stability became competitive differentiators among hospitality businesses.

Educational institutions invested in WiFi infrastructure to enable technology-enhanced learning experiences. Schools and universities deployed WiFi networks enabling students to access online learning platforms, educational content, and digital collaboration tools. This infrastructure investment supported curriculum modernization incorporating digital literacy and technology skills alongside traditional academic subjects. Distance learning initiatives during health crises particularly benefited from pre-existing WiFi infrastructure enabling rapid transition to online instruction.

Security challenges associated with public WiFi networks emerged as usage expanded. Unsecured public networks created vulnerabilities to man-in-the-middle attacks and data interception affecting users transmitting sensitive information. Financial institutions and security-conscious users restricted sensitive transactions on public networks. VPN adoption increased among sophisticated users seeking privacy and security on public networks. Despite security concerns, public WiFi expansion remained valued as accessibility improvement enabling digital services access for cost-constrained populations.
