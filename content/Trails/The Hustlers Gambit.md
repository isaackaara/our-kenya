<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">William Ruto began selling chicken in Eldoret markets where hawkers understood that value is created not by possession but by transaction. From county hustler to deputy president to eventual head of state, he learned that ambition could leapfrog class even when geography and education stood against it. This trail follows the political entrepreneur who gambled everything on class resentment being stronger than tribal loyalty, and whether democracy rewards the marketable over the merited.</p>
