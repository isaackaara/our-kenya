<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">In March 2018, two men whose supporters had died for their rivalry walked into Harambee House and shook hands, then stood together for cameras without deciding who had won. The handshake that ended Kenya's most bitter election was also a surrender: to accept that some Questions cannot be settled by votes. This trail explores how compromise becomes victory when continuation promises worse, and whether accommodation heals democracy or merely postpones the next violence.</p>
