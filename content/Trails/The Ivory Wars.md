<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">Poaching is not hunting but warfare, with elephants as territory and ivory as nuclear material. The war began in 1970s when Japanese demand met African supply, and Kenya discovered that national parks could not protect themselves from the poverty that surrounded them. This trail follows the moral calculus of those who sold national heritage for school fees, and the rangers who learned that conservation requires winning hearts before securing borders.</p>
