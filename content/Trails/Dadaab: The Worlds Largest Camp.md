<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">What began as three temporary white tents in the desert became Kenya's third largest city by population. Dadaab sits on the edge of famine and survival, where Somali families who fled civil war built permanent lives among 330,000 people in a camp designed for 90,000. This trail explores how temporary became permanent, how 25 years of displacement created an accidental metropolis in Kenya's most hostile landscape.</p>
