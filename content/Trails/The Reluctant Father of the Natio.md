<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">Jomo Kenyatta never asked to be father of Kenya, arriving at nationhood carrying the accumulated weight of disowned lands and broken oaths. The Anglican catechism teacher who became Mau Mau's negotiate learned that defeating an empire requires refusing the temptation to revenge itself. This trail traces how one man's ambivalence about power's uses came to embody a nation's reluctance to repeat its colonizers' sins.</p>
