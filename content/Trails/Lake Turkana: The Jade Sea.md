<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">Lake Turkana sits where two continents once collided, creating a landscape so brutal that life survives only by making peace with impossible conditions. The Jade Sea harbors Nile crocodiles that learned to live without rivers, Turkana nomads who found fish in the deepest desert, and fossils that walked upright under East African skies three million years before the first human footprint. This trail follows the collision between geology and biology where life was born and made brutal.</p>
