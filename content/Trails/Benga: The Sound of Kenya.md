<p class="trail-intro-paragraph">The Luo musicians left their villages carrying transistor radios and electric guitars, trading nyatiti songs for amplified heartbreak. From Kakamega nightclubs to Nairobi's Millionaires' Club, Benga grew from village wedding music into Kenya's defining sound. This trail traces the migration of rhythm from Lake Victoria shores to urban dancehalls, where guitars learned to speak in Luo tongues and men sang of love lost between city and countryside.</p>
