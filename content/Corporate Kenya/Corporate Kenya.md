The economic and corporate history of [[Kenya]] is the story of extraction, adaptation, and entrepreneurship. From [[Colonial Economy|colonial commodity chains]] built to feed the British empire, to [[Post-Independence Economic Policy|post-independence state capitalism]] and [[Parastatals Kenya|parastatal dominance]], to today's private-sector-led model, Kenya's corporate sector reflects the deep structures of power, family, and capital accumulation that have shaped the nation.

This vertical maps [[Kenya]]'s corporate landscape across [[Banking History Kenya|banking]], [[Telecoms History Kenya|telecommunications]], [[Kenya Agricultural History|agriculture]], [[Manufacturing History Kenya|manufacturing]], [[Silicon Savannah|technology]], [[Insurance History Kenya|insurance]], and [[Mombasa Port History|trade]]. It examines the companies, families, and regulatory frameworks that have defined Kenyan business.

## Key Themes

**Colonial Extraction** - The railway, settler farms, and Asian trading intermediaries created Kenya's first corporate economy. Everything was built to extract wealth and export it to Britain.

**Post-Independence State Capitalism** - Jomo Kenyatta and Sessional Paper No. 10 (1965) adopted a mixed economy. Parastatals (state corporations) dominated. Africanisation meant power, not necessarily competence.

**The Moi Collapse** - Parastatals were looted. Banks collapsed. The state lost control. By the 1980s, Kenya needed structural adjustment.

**Liberalisation and Crisis** - IMF/World Bank programmes forced privatisation, devaluation, and retrenchment. The social cost was high. New private companies emerged, often owned by political insiders.

**The Tech Moment** - By 2007, M-Pesa launched. By 2010, the submarine cables arrived and Silicon Savannah was born. Kenya had mobile money before most of the world.

**Current Era** - Private-sector-led growth. Chinese competition. Regional trade. Persistent inequality. The Kenyatta and Kenyatta family families still own vast business empires. Asian business families remain powerful. Corruption persists.
