The Luo are a Nilotic people with a population of approximately 10 percent of Kenya and a dominant presence on the shores of [[Lake Victoria Kenya|Lake Victoria]] (Nam Lolwe). With a history spanning at least five centuries in present-day Kenya, the Luo have shaped the nation's labor movements, political contests, musical traditions, and cultural identity. Their language (Dholuo) is widely spoken in Nyanza Region (Siaya, [[Kisumu County|Kisumu County]], [[Homa Bay County|Homa Bay County]], [[Migori County|Migori County]] counties) and among diaspora communities.
