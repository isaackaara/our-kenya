Nairobi, Kenya's capital city, concentrated educational infrastructure and institutional prestige reflecting its role as national administrative and economic center. The [[University of Nairobi Founding|University of Nairobi]], [[Strathmore School Excellence|Strathmore School]], and numerous elite institutions located in Nairobi created educational monopoly advantaging urban residents and wealthy families. Rural students seeking elite education faced geographic and financial barriers accessing Nairobi institutions. The city's concentration of educational opportunity reproduced urban-rural divides in educational access and quality.
