Kenya's educational assessment systems have evolved from colonial Cambridge examinations toward Kenya-specific assessment frameworks. The Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) manages national assessments including Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) and Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE). Assessment standards remain crucial mechanisms determining student progression and institutional prestige. The shift toward Competency Based Curriculum prompted assessment reform emphasizing competency documentation rather than traditional examinations. Assessment remains contested territory where measurement philosophy reflects deeper disputes about educational purpose.
