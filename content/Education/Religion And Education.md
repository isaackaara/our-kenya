[[Religion|Religious]] institutions and faith traditions have substantially shaped Kenyan educational provision through mission schools, Islamic institutions, and faith-based organizations. The Catholic Church, Protestant denominations, and Muslim communities established schools integrating religious instruction with academic curricula. Religious education policy has evolved from colonial Christian dominance toward contemporary pluralism accommodating diverse faith traditions. Religion remains contested dimension of education policy regarding curriculum content, institutional control, and values transmission.
