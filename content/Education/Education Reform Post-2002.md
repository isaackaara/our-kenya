Post-2002 education reforms, beginning with Free Primary Education (2003), represented major policy shifts toward universal access. Successive governments emphasized education expansion through primary school fee abolition, secondary school bursaries, and university access initiatives. The 2010 Constitution devolved education to county governments, creating new governance structures. Contemporary reforms emphasize competency-based learning, digital integration, and inclusive education. Yet implementation challenges persist regarding resource adequacy and systemic inequalities.
