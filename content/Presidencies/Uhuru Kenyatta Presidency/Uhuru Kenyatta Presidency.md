# Uhuru Kenyatta Presidency (2013-2022)

[[Uhuru Kenyatta]] served as Kenya's fourth president for two consecutive terms spanning 2013 to 2022, a nine-year period marked by infrastructure expansion, constitutional consolidation, political turbulence, and deepening debt. A [[Kikuyu]] member and son of founding president [[Jomo Kenyatta]], Uhuru ascended to the presidency while under indictment at the International Criminal Court for alleged crimes against humanity stemming from the 2007-2008 post-election violence.

## Overview

Uhuru's tenure began with a narrow electoral victory in 2013 against Raila Odinga. His presidency was defined by several pivotal moments: the ICC cases that eventually collapsed, the controversial 2017 election annulled by the Supreme Court (a continental first), his dramatic March 2018 reconciliation with Odinga ("the handshake"), the ill-fated Building Bridges Initiative reform, and his decision not to back his own deputy William Ruto in 2022. On the positive side, Kenya saw ambitious infrastructure development, particularly the Standard Gauge Railway, attempts at healthcare universalization, and relative constitutional stability. On the negative side, the country accumulated massive public debt, grappled with significant corruption scandals, and experienced declining investment in critical sectors.

## Key Themes

**Infrastructure Ambition:** Uhuru championed megaprojects, especially the Standard Gauge Railway, framing them as modernization. Critics questioned whether projects were economically viable or strategically necessary.

**ICC Shadow:** Uhuru's presidency unfolded under the cloud of ICC charges. Their eventual withdrawal changed the narrative but also raised questions about pressure and due process.

**Election Legitimacy:** The 2017 election annulment shook regional confidence in democratic institutions. The Supreme Court's decision to nullify was unprecedented in Africa, yet the re-run suffered low turnout and opposition boycott.

**Political Realignment:** The 2018 handshake with Odinga was a seismic shift. It created new political geometry and contributed to Uhuru's estrangement from his own deputy, Ruto.

**Debt Spiral:** While infrastructure is asset-creating, the scale and terms of borrowing (particularly from China) raised sustainability questions that would confront the Ruto presidency.

**Corruption as Baseline:** Major scandals (NYS, dam projects, health ministry theft) suggested institutional weakness in accountability mechanisms despite constitutional reforms.

## Context

Uhuru inherited a stable post-2010 constitutional framework but presided over its first real stress tests, particularly the 2017 election crisis. His presidency bridged two distinct political eras: the ICC/post-violence trauma of 2013 and the IMF-supervised austerity of Ruto's early years.

The handshake in 2018 was pivotal globally, attracting international attention and leading to dialogue initiatives, but it also crystallized executive power at the expense of legislative independence and party discipline.
