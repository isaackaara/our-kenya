Ruto's presidency emphasized environmental conservation (tree planting, wildlife protection) and climate commitment, continuing Kenya's international climate negotiation participation. Yet early policies suggested environment was complementary priority rather than central development focus. Climate action remained rhetorical without major economic restructuring or greenhouse gas reduction targets.
