Rigathi Gachagua, appointed Deputy President as Mt Kenya representative, experienced public conflict with President Ruto leading to eventual impeachment in October 2023. Gachagua, a wealthy businessman from Mathira constituency, was positioned to represent Mt Kenya interests in Ruto government yet tensions emerged over resource distribution, ministerial appointments, and political positioning. Ruto's impeachment of Gachagua (through senate vote) illustrated that the DP position remained subordinate to presidential authority: even alliance partners could be removed if deemed inconvenient. The conflict suggested deepening coalition tensions and potential Mt Kenya realignment toward opposition. Gachagua's removal signaled that Ruto would not tolerate autonomous DP political base and would prioritize presidential authority over coalition stability. The episode illustrated constitutional mechanics of DP vulnerability despite elected position: unlike presidents who cannot be removed except through impeachment, DPs could be impeached relatively easily if legislative majority supported removal.
