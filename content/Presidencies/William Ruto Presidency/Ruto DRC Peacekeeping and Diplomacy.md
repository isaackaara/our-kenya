Ruto's early presidency continued Kenya's military deployment in Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) as part of multinational peacekeeping operations. The deployment, initiated under Uhuru, involved Kenyan troops supporting DRC government against various insurgent groups. Ruto's early stance suggested continuation of commitment, indicating Kenya would maintain continental security partnership obligations. Yet resource constraints meant that DRC engagement remained limited to military deployment without proportionate diplomatic or development initiative. The peacekeeping role provided Kenya international legitimacy and US partnership validation, justifying military spending while contributing to regional stability objectives.
