Ruto's political network included extensive connection to Kapsabet Boys High School alumni, reflecting his school background as important elite bonding mechanism. Kapsabet, a leading national secondary school in Nandi County, produced numerous Rift Valley politicians and business leaders. Ruto's presidency appeared to favor school connections in patronage distribution and appointment decisions. This reflected broader Kenya pattern: boarding school networks provided lasting elite bonding transcending ethnic and geographic lines. Kapsabet network gave Ruto allies distributed across Rift Valley and beyond, strengthening his political machine through school-based relationships developed during formative years.
