Raila Odinga, defeated in 2022 election and rejected Azimio coalition endorsement, remained opposition leader with uncertain political trajectory. Ruto's early presidency excluded Raila from government, treating him as opposition rather than partner. Raila maintained constituency in certain regions (Luo areas, parts of Nairobi) yet faced organizational challenges rebuilding opposition post-electoral defeat. Whether Raila would remain viable political force or gradual opposition decline would occur remained unclear. Ruto's approach to opposition appeared antagonistic rather than conciliatory, potentially generating grievance that could resurface in 2027 election campaigns.
