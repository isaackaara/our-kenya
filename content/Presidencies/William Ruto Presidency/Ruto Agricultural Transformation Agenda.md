Ruto emphasized agricultural transformation as development priority, continuing emphasis on food security and export competitiveness. Early policies focused on subsidized fertilizer distribution, seed provision, and market linkage development. Yet budget constraints limited implementation scale. The agenda reflected Ruto's agricultural background (botany degree) and Kalenjin rural roots, yet faced similar constraints as predecessors: limited government resources, farmer poverty limiting input adoption, and climate volatility reducing productivity gains. Early agricultural initiatives showed momentum yet sustainability remained uncertain given broader fiscal austerity.
