Ruto's presidency maintained cooperative bilateral relationship with Tanzania under President Samia Suluhu Hassan. Both leaders emphasized regional integration and security cooperation within East African Community framework. Early engagement suggested Ruto would maintain Kenya's traditional East African partnerships without pursuing transformative regional initiatives. Tanzania remained secondary to Kenya in bilateral dynamics due to economic scale differential, yet cooperation on security and trade continued. Regional integration remained primarily rhetorical rather than substantive, with actual progress on EAC ambitions limited by competing national interests and resource constraints.
