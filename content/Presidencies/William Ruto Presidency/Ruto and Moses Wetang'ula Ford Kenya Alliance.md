Moses Wetang'ula, Ford Kenya party leader from Luhya community, aligned with Ruto's Kenya Kwanza coalition, contributing Luhya support to Ruto's broader coalition. Wetang'ula's Ford Kenya party brought parliamentary numbers and community legitimacy beyond what Mudavadi's CCM carried. The dual Luhya alliance (Mudavadi and Wetang'ula) suggested Ruto was incorporating divided Luhya community into coalition, reducing potential for unified opposition. Wetang'ula retained opposition status publicly (Ford Kenya as separate entity) while maintaining de facto coalition relationship, positioning him to claim independence while benefiting from Kenya Kwanza dominance. The flexible alliance structure suited both: Ruto gained Luhya support without requiring organic coalition merger; Wetang'ula maintained party autonomy while accessing government patronage.
