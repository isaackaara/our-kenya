Jomo Kenyatta's Kenya became known for athletic prowess, particularly in distance running, and Kenyatta's government promoted sport as a means of building national identity and demonstrating Kenya's capabilities on the international stage. The success of Kenyan athletes, particularly in long-distance running events, became a source of national pride and contributed to Kenya's international reputation. Kenyatta's government invested in sports infrastructure, supported athlete development, and used sporting success as a metaphor for Kenya's postcolonial development.

Kenya's emergence as a world power in distance running began during Kenyatta's presidency. Kenyan runners, many of them drawn from Kikuyu and Kalenjin communities, began winning international competitions and setting records. The Olympic Games and international athletics championships became venues for Kenya to demonstrate its capabilities and to achieve international recognition. Kenyatta's government supported these athletes, provided funding for training and competition, and celebrated their successes as national achievements.

The connection between Kenyatta's Kenya and athletic success was not coincidental. Kenya's geography, with its high-altitude plateaus that naturally develop runner's lung capacity, created conditions favorable for distance running. Kenyatta's government recognized this advantage and pursued policies designed to develop Kenya's running talent. The government established training centers, supported coach development, and provided resources for athletes to compete internationally.

Kenyatta's interest in sport reflected broader ideologies of national development and national pride. Sporting success was presented as evidence of Kenya's modernity, its capacities, and its potential for development. Kenyan athletes competing successfully on the international stage demonstrated that Kenya was not a backward, underdeveloped nation but rather a nation capable of world-class achievement.

The success of Kenyan athletes also contributed to the development of tourism in Kenya. International visitors came to Kenya to watch athletes train, to visit the regions where famous runners lived and trained, and to experience Kenya's geography and culture. The connection between Kenyan running and tourism promoted Kenya's international reputation and contributed to foreign exchange earnings.

Kenyatta's support for sport also served domestic political purposes. Sporting success provided something for all Kenyans to take pride in, regardless of ethnicity or region. National sporting competitions and successes could be presented as achievements of the nation as a whole, transcending ethnic and regional divisions. The celebration of Kenyan athletes thus served Kenyatta's broader agenda of building national unity.

The athletic success of Kenya that began during Kenyatta's presidency would extend far beyond his death. Kenya would remain a world power in distance running throughout the remainder of the twentieth century. The foundations for Kenya's athletic dominance were laid during Kenyatta's presidency, contributing to Kenya's international reputation and its distinctive identity within the postcolonial world.
