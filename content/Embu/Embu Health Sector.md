Embu [[Embu County]] has expanded healthcare [[Embu Infrastructure]] and services since independence, with particular expansion since devolution in 2013. Health facilities range from basic health centers to the referral hospital. Access and quality remain variable, with disparities between urban and rural areas and socioeconomic status affecting health outcomes.

## Healthcare Facilities

Embu County has a network of government health facilities including community health centers, dispensaries, sub-county clinics, and Embu Teaching and Referral Hospital (the primary referral facility). Private clinics and hospitals supplement public sector provision. Traditional healers persist alongside formal health services.

## Healthcare Access

Healthcare access in Embu remains unequal, with better access in Embu town and surrounding areas and limited access in remote zones. Transportation to health facilities presents challenges for rural residents. User fees at health facilities, though reduced, create barriers for poorest residents. Mobile health clinics attempt to extend access to remote areas.

## Disease Burden

Embu County faces disease burdens including malaria (in lower elevation zones), respiratory infections, gastrointestinal diseases, and hypertension (in urban areas). HIV/AIDS, though declining in prevalence, remains significant. Maternal and child health indicators have improved but remain below national targets. Diabetes and other non-communicable diseases are increasing.

## Maternal and Child Health

Maternal mortality rates in Embu have declined but remain elevated. Skilled birth attendance has increased, though remains incomplete, particularly in rural areas. Immunization coverage has improved significantly. Under-five mortality has declined, though progress has plateaued in recent years.

## Maternal Health Services

Antenatal care coverage has expanded, with many women accessing early pregnancy care. Facility-based delivery has increased though home births remain common in some areas. Post-partum care services have expanded. Family planning services have improved, though unmet need remains.

## Healthcare Workforce

Embu County health facilities face healthcare worker shortages, including doctors, nurses, and specialized personnel. Incentives to attract health workers to rural areas remain limited. Health worker training institutions operate in Embu, though graduate retention within county remains variable. Healthcare worker morale and remuneration affect service quality.

## Medical Supplies and Equipment

Health facilities sometimes experience shortages of essential drugs and supplies, disrupting service delivery. Equipment maintenance and replacement remain challenging in resource-constrained environments. County government procurement systems affect supply chain efficiency.

## Mental Health Services

Mental health services in Embu remain limited, with few specialized mental health facilities. Psychiatric nurses and mental health counselors provide limited coverage. Mental health needs, including depression, anxiety, and substance abuse, remain largely unaddressed.

## Health Insurance and Financing

Health insurance coverage through NHIF (National Hospital Insurance Fund) has expanded, though remains incomplete. Out-of-pocket health expenditures remain substantial, creating financial barriers to care. County health financing depends on national allocations and county revenue.
