# Embu Infrastructure

Embu [[Embu County]] has expanded infrastructure dramatically since independence and particularly since [[Embu and Devolution]] (2013 onwards). Roads, electricity, water systems, and telecommunications have improved, though gaps remain between urban and rural areas, and between well-served and marginalized locations.

## Road Infrastructure

Embu County has expanded road networks from colonial-era limited connectivity to more comprehensive coverage. The main highway connecting Nairobi and Moshi (Tanzania) passes through Embu, facilitating commerce and transportation. County roads and rural access roads have been improved through county government investment and national road funds.

## Electricity Access

Electricity access in Embu has expanded significantly, with grid extension reaching most towns and many rural areas. The national electricity utility Kenya Power serves urban and peri-urban areas. Rural electrification projects have expanded access, though generation costs remain high relative to rural incomes, limiting consumption in some areas.

## Water Systems

Water scarcity represents a significant challenge despite Embu's generally favorable rainfall. Piped water systems in towns provide supply to urban residents, though water shortages occur periodically. Rural water systems have been developed through county investment and community initiatives, though maintenance and sustainability challenges persist.

## Healthcare Facilities

Embu County has expanded health facility networks including primary health centers, dispensaries, and the referral hospital. Embu Teaching and Referral Hospital serves as the highest-level facility. Health worker shortages and supply chain disruptions affect facility capacity, though service provision has improved since devolution.

## Educational Infrastructure

School construction has accelerated with devolution, expanding secondary school capacity significantly. Primary school infrastructure has been improved through the free primary education program. Technical institutes have been established to provide alternative post-secondary education. However, facility quality disparities persist.

## Telecommunications Infrastructure

Mobile phone networks provide coverage to most of Embu County, enabling communications and digital services. Internet connectivity through mobile networks has expanded, though broadband access remains limited outside towns. These networks enable e-commerce, mobile money services, and digital government services.

## Transportation and Logistics

Embu town has expanded as a trade hub with wholesale markets, transportation terminals, and logistics services. Regular long-distance bus services connect Embu to Nairobi and other regions, facilitating commerce and personal travel. This transportation infrastructure supports economic activity.

## Challenges and Gaps

Infrastructure development has been uneven, with urban areas better served than remote rural areas. Infrastructure maintenance remains challenging, with some facilities deteriorating after construction. Climate change threatens water systems in drier areas, requiring ongoing adaptation investment.
