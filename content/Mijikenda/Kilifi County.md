Kilifi County is the heartland of the Giriama and northern Mijikenda peoples. Located in Kenya's coastal region, the county extends from the Sabaki River in the north to the Mombasa hinterland in the south, covering approximately 1,200 square kilometers with a diverse landscape of coastal plains, forests, creeks, and agricultural hinterland.

## Geography and Key Towns

The county seat is Kilifi town, located on a scenic creek. Other major towns include Malindi, a historic Swahili coastal center that has become a major tourism destination, and Watamu, a beach resort town. The county's population according to the 2019 census is approximately 1.1 million people, making it one of Kenya's more densely populated coastal counties.

## Economy

The primary economic activities in Kilifi County are tourism, fishing, and small-scale agriculture. Tourism is centered on beaches (particularly around Watamu and Malindi), historical sites (Gedi Ruins, Rabai Museum), marine parks (Watamu Marine National Park), and kaya forests. Tourism provides employment and income but also creates pressures on coastal land and resources.

Fishing remains economically important, both as a source of protein and as a source of income, with [[Mijikenda Fishermen]] engaging in traditional and commercial methods. Traditional fishing methods (nets, traps, small boats) coexist with commercial fishing operations. Fish are sold locally and traded inland.

Agriculture includes [[Mijikenda and Coconut|coconut cultivation]], cassava farming, mango production, cashew nut cultivation, and vegetables. Salt production in some coastal areas provides income for fishing communities. These agricultural activities are limited by land pressure and by the suitability of coastal soils.

## Major Attractions

Kilifi County contains several major tourist attractions. The Ruins of Gedi are an abandoned Swahili settlement from the 14th-16th centuries, one of Kenya's most significant archaeological sites. The Watamu Marine National Park provides opportunities for snorkeling and diving. The Malindi Museum documents Swahili history. The Rabai Museum commemorates the Church Missionary Society's first mainland Kenya mission. The kaya forests, particularly those designated as UNESCO World Heritage Sites, attract increasing numbers of cultural tourism visitors.

## Land and Population Pressure

Kilifi County faces increasing land pressure from population growth and tourism development. Agricultural land is becoming increasingly scarce relative to population, leading to fragmented holdings and limited farm productivity. Young people migrate to urban areas seeking employment. The dynamics of tourism development have accelerated land commodification, with outside investors purchasing or leasing coastal land for resort development.

## Political Significance

Kilifi County is politically significant as the heartland of Giriama political movements and as the location of some of Kenya's most notable Mijikenda political figures. The [[The Giriama Uprising 1913-1914]] centered on Kilifi County, driven by resistance to colonial taxation and labor demands. Contemporary Kilifi politics involves tensions between coastal autonomy movements and national integration, land rights advocacy, and tourism development pressures.
