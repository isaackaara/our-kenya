Film genres in Kenyan cinema reflected both international film traditions and locally distinctive narrative interests. Genres provided frameworks for audience expectations and industry categorization, though films often mixed genres and defied simple categorization.

Comedy films represented substantial portion of Kenyan theatrical cinema, emphasizing humor and entertainment value over narrative complexity. Comedies achieved commercial success through audience preference for entertaining escapism. Comedies often incorporated music and dance sequences, blending film and entertainment performance traditions. Successful comedies generated sequel productions and actor career development.

Drama films addressed serious subjects and character development, incorporating conflict and emotional engagement. Dramas varied from family-oriented stories to more sophisticated social critique. Television drama emerged as particularly significant format, with episodic series developing narrative over multiple episodes.

Action films featured physical combat, vehicle chases, and elaborate action sequences. Action sequences appealed to audiences seeking entertainment spectacle. Stunt professionals and action choreography were essential to action film production. International action films were prominent in Kenya's theatrical cinema, though locally produced action films were less common.

Romance and love story themes appeared in Kenyan films, though often integrated with other genres rather than pure romance focus. Romance elements contributed to entertainment appeal and emotional engagement across genres. Gender representations in romance films often reflected traditional relationship patterns, though this varied with particular films.

Crime films and television dramas featured detective, police, or criminal narratives. These dramas appealed to audiences interested in suspense and mystery. Crime television series achieved popularity through episodic storytelling building across seasons.

Thriller productions emphasized suspense and audience tension, utilizing narrative uncertainty and pacing to create emotional engagement. Thrillers employed both realistic and fantastic premises, generating diverse entertainment approaches.

Historical and period dramas drew from Kenya's colonial and post-independence history. Historical narratives connected audiences to national history and heritage. Some historical dramas featured international partnerships and funding.

Science fiction and fantasy films were less common in Kenyan cinema compared to other genres, though some productions utilized imaginative premises. The higher production costs for effects and elaborate scenarios limited science fiction production.

Documentary films, while sometimes considered distinct from entertainment genres, varied in documentary approach and subject matter. Documentary traditions included observational, expository, and advocacy approaches.

Genre conventions provided audience expectations and marketing frameworks. Audiences selected films partly through genre preference. Successful genre films sometimes generated sequels and franchise possibilities as audiences sought repetition of satisfying narrative formulas.
