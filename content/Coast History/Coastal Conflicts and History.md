Coastal conflicts emerged from multiple sources including resource competition, political authority disputes, and economic inequality, creating challenges for social stability and inclusive development. Pre-colonial conflicts between rival sultanates and city-states for trade control and territorial authority defined early coastal history. Portuguese conquest generated military conflicts disrupting existing political structures and commercial networks. Arab Omani rule replaced Portuguese control through military campaigns establishing new political hierarchy. Subsequent conflicts between Omani rulers and local populations centered on tax extraction and political authority. Colonial conquest generated armed resistance from coastal populations defending sovereignty and autonomy. Colonial labor requisitions and taxation created economic conflicts between colonial authorities and coastal populations. Competing coastal community claims regarding fisher resource access and territorial fishing grounds generated disputes. Land conflicts emerged from colonial appropriation and post-colonial land concentration in limited hands. Commercial conflicts between merchant traders and artisanal producers created economic tension. Religious conflicts between Islamic and Christian populations emerged with missionary expansion during colonial period. Ethnic tensions developed as colonial labor recruitment brought interior populations to coastal zones. Class conflicts emerged between wealthy coastal merchants and poor workers and fisher populations. Political conflicts during post-independence period centered on governance authority and resource distribution. Contemporary environmental conflicts pit conservationists against fishing and pastoral communities resisting resource restrictions. Climate change impacts including sea level rise and drought create new resource scarcity driving conflicts. Urban conflicts between formal residents and squatter settlement populations reflect housing inequality. Gender conflicts emerged regarding property rights and control of household resources. Water access conflicts pit urban, agricultural, and pastoral populations against each other. Port and harbor conflicts pit development pressures against fishing community access concerns. Educational access conflicts reflect unequal distribution of school infrastructure. Contemporary conflict resolution mechanisms remain inadequate for addressing underlying structural inequalities. Sustainable peace requires addressing historical injustices and economic inequality driving contemporary conflicts.
