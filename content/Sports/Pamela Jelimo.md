Pamela Jelimo won Kenya's first-ever Olympic gold medal in women's athletics at the 2008 Beijing Olympics in the 800 meters, running 1:54.87. This victory established Jelimo as a breakthrough athlete for Kenya's female distance running and demonstrated that Kenyan women could compete at Olympic level in track and field.

Jelimo's Olympic gold preceded broader success by Kenyan female distance runners but was historically the breakthrough moment that established female Kenyan athletics as globally competitive. Her achievement elevated Kenya's women's athletics profile and inspired subsequent generations of female distance runners.
