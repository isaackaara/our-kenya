Women's football in Kenya has grown from minimal institutional support and participation to developing professional and semi-professional leagues. The Kenyan women's football league provides competitive opportunities, though at significantly lower investment levels than men's football. The women's national team (Harambee Starlets) competes internationally with growing capability.

This growth pattern mirrors women's athletics transformation and reflects broader emergence of women's sports in Kenya.
