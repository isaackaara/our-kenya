[[Gurdwaras]] (Sikh temples) in Kenya serve as religious centers and community gathering spaces for [[Sikhs]]. They host daily prayers, ceremonies, langar meals, and community events. Major gurdwaras exist in [[Nairobi]], [[Mombasa]], and [[Kisumu]].

## The Guru Nanak Gurdwara, Nairobi

The [[Guru Nanak Gurdwara]] in Nairobi is the most prominent Sikh religious institution in Kenya. It hosts daily prayers (from early morning), community celebrations, and major Sikh festivals. The Gurdwara's architecture features distinctive Sikh design elements, including a dome (dome-shaped roof) and decorated gate.

## Daily Spiritual Practice

Sikhs gather at gurdwaras for prayer, meditation, and spiritual study. The Guru Granth Sahib (Sikh scripture) is kept at the gurdwara and treated with utmost respect. Sikhs remove their shoes and cover their heads before entering, showing respect for the sacred space. Daily practice reinforces Sikh commitment and community solidarity.

## The Langar Tradition

Every gurdwara serves langar (free community meal) to all visitors, regardless of religion or caste. Langar is a central Sikh institution reflecting Sikh commitment to equality and charity. Volunteers from the community prepare and serve langar. Many non-Sikhs visit gurdwaras for langar meals.

## Major Festivals and Celebrations

Sikh festivals (Guru Nanak Jayanti, Baisakhi, Hola Mohalla) are celebrated at gurdwaras with elaborate ceremonies, music, processions, and communal meals. These celebrations affirm Sikh identity and attract broad community participation.

## Gurdwaras in Other Towns

Beyond Nairobi, gurdwaras exist in Mombasa, Kisumu, and other towns with Sikh populations. These gurdwaras serve local Sikh communities and reflect the geographic distribution of Sikh settlement.

## Educational Function

Gurdwaras teach Sikhism to children, offering instruction in Punjabi language, Sikh history, and religious philosophy. This education transmits Sikh identity and traditions to younger generations growing up in Kenya.

## Architectural Distinctiveness

Sikh gurdwaras feature distinctive architecture: turrets, domes, decorated gates, and interiors with scripture inscriptions. The architecture makes gurdwaras visually distinctive in Nairobi's religious landscape.

## Contemporary Gurdwara Life

Contemporary gurdwaras balance traditional religious practice with adaptation to Kenyan context. Youth groups, social service activities, and modern educational programs supplement traditional worship. Many gurdwaras are actively engaged with broader Kenyan communities.
