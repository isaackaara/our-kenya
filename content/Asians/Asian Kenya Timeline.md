# Asian Kenya Timeline

A chronological reference of major dates and events in the history of [[Asian Kenyans|Asians]] in [[Kenya]], from the [[Indian Ocean]] trading era through 2026.

## Pre-Colonial Era

- 1500: Gujarati ships documented at Malindi and Mombasa by Portuguese explorer Duarte Barbosa
- Centuries before 1500: South Asian (particularly Gujarati) merchants trading on the East African coast, integrated into Indian Ocean commerce networks

## Colonial Era (1895-1963)

- 1895: British establish protectorate over East Africa; first Indian workers arrive for railway construction
- 1896-1901: Uganda Railway construction employs approximately 32,000 Indian laborers
- 1898-1899: Tsavo Man-Eaters kill dozens of Indian railway workers
- 1901: Railway construction complete; approximately 2,500 Indian laborers remain in East Africa
- 1902: A.M. Jeevanjee founds the African Standard newspaper in Mombasa
- 1905: Jeevanjee sells African Standard; renamed East African Standard
- 1925: A.M. Jeevanjee donates Jeevanjee Gardens to Nairobi
- 1920s onward: British establish separate "Asian seats" in Legislative Council
- 1920s-1940s: Indian community expands beyond traders into professions, businesses, and industries
- 1948: Indian population reaches approximately 90,000
- 1962: Census records 176,613 Asians in Kenya; one-third of Nairobi's population
- 1963: Kenya achieves independence; Asians forced to choose citizenship

## Post-Independence Era (1963-2000)

- 1963: Kenyan independence; Asians make citizenship choices; tension over post-colonial status
- 1964: Pio Gama Pinto establishes Lumumba Institute
- 1965: Pio Gama Pinto assassinated, February 24
- 1967-1968: Africanisation crisis; Kenyatta government passes laws requiring African ownership or partnership
- 1968: British Commonwealth Immigrants Act restricts entry of UK passport holders of Asian origin
- 1970s: Massive Asian emigration to Britain, Canada, United States
- 1972: Idi Amin expels Uganda's Asians; many flee through Kenya; some settle in Kenya
- 1980s: Asian population stabilizes at approximately 100,000-120,000 (compared to 176,000 at independence)
- 1985: Bidco Africa founded by Vimal Shah family; begins as edible oils producer
- 1999: Nation Media Group launches NTV television channel

## Contemporary Era (2000-2026)

- 2007-2008: Post-election violence targets Asian businesses; looting and arson; some emigration
- 2019 Census: Records 47,555 Asian Kenyans with citizenship; 42,972 without, totaling approximately 90,000
- 2017-2018: Post-election crisis; ethnic-based boycotts of Bidco and other Asian businesses
- 2018 (March): "Handshake" between President Kenyatta and opposition leader Odinga lifts boycotts
- 2026 (March): Aga Khan announces sale of majority shareholding in Nation Media Group after 60 years

---

**Historical Continuities:**
- Pre-colonial Asian trading networks on the coast
- Railway laborers remaining to establish permanent community (1901)
- Merchant and professional roles in colonial economy
- Colonial racial hierarchy placing Asians between Europeans and Africans
- Separate political representation in colonial period
- Independence citizenship crisis
- Africanisation pressures and selective emigration
- Periodic episodes of violence targeting Asians
- Ongoing question of belonging and full integration
- Significant philanthropic and economic contributions despite political marginalization
