Devki Group, led by Narendra Raval (known as the "Steel King"), became one of East Africa's most prominent steel and industrial enterprises. Starting with steel rolling mills, Devki Group expanded into cement manufacturing, construction materials, and national school construction projects. The group exemplifies how some Asian entrepreneurs identified critical industrial needs in Kenya's developing economy and built major enterprises to meet those needs.

## Narendra Raval and the Steel Vision

Narendra Raval came from a [[Gujarati Dominance in Business|merchant family]] with interests in [[Indian Traders and the Duka|trade and commerce]]. Raval recognized that Kenya's expanding construction sector would require large quantities of reinforced steel bars and other steel products. Rather than importing all steel needs from abroad, Raval envisioned developing local steel manufacturing capacity. This vision led to the establishment of Devki Group's steel rolling mills.

## Steel Rolling Mills

The core business of Devki Group became steel rolling mills. These mills processed imported steel billets (semi-finished steel) into finished steel products such as reinforced steel bars. Steel bars are essential inputs for building construction, bridge construction, and other infrastructure projects. Kenya's rapidly developing economy created strong demand for these products.

## Manufacturing and Processing

Devki's steel mills employed modern rolling and processing technology to convert raw or semi-finished steel into standardized products. The mills had to maintain strict quality controls to ensure that steel products met building codes and engineering standards. Quality assurance became a competitive advantage for Devki, as engineers and builders recognized Devki steel as reliable and meeting international standards.

## Expansion into Cement

From steel, Devki Group expanded into cement manufacturing. Cement is another essential building material, and Kenya's construction boom created strong demand. Devki's cement operations involved either manufacturing cement from raw materials or processing imported clinker into finished cement. The expansion into cement complemented the steel business, as both were essential for construction.

## National School Construction

One of Devki Group's most significant undertakings was the construction of schools across Kenya. The Kenyan government embarked on massive school-building programs, particularly after independence, to expand educational access. Devki Group won contracts to construct schools using their steel and other building materials. This government contract work provided substantial and stable revenues while contributing to Kenya's educational infrastructure.

## Employment and Economic Contribution

Devki Group became a significant employer in Kenya, with hundreds of direct employees in manufacturing and construction. The group's suppliers and logistics partners provided additional employment. Devki Group's contributions to Kenya's infrastructure through school construction and other projects were significant.

## Diversification Strategy

Beyond steel, cement, and construction, Devki Group diversified into other industrial and commercial activities. The group's business portfolio expanded to include real estate, property development, and other ventures. This diversification reduced dependence on any single sector and provided multiple revenue sources.

## Market Position and Competition

Devki Group achieved market leadership in steel and cement sectors in Kenya. The company's quality reputation, manufacturing capacity, and government relationships enabled it to maintain competitive advantage. Other competitors entered these sectors, but Devki Group maintained significant market share due to scale, quality, and established relationships.

## Government Relationships

Devki Group's success was partly based on strong relationships with the Kenyan government. Government contracts for school construction and other infrastructure provided stable, large-scale revenue. The group's leaders were recognized as reliable and professional business partners by government officials. These government relationships complemented Devki's private-sector business.

## Challenges and Economic Conditions

Devki Group faced periodic challenges from Kenya's economic cycles. During periods of low growth, demand for construction materials declined. Currency fluctuations affected input costs for imported materials. Political instability occasionally disrupted government contract awards. Despite these challenges, Devki maintained its market position and profitability over decades.

## Narrative of Economic Contribution

The Devki Group story was frequently cited as evidence of the positive contributions made by Asian entrepreneurs to Kenya's economic development. Devki Group created jobs, produced essential building materials, and contributed to Kenya's infrastructure. Narendra Raval was presented as an economic hero who had identified Kenya's needs and built enterprises to meet them.
